**寒溪語**或稱**宜蘭克里奧爾語**、**日語客里謳**\[1\]，是一種基於[日語的](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/日語 "wikilink")[克里奧爾語](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/克里奧爾語 "wikilink")，目前使用於[宜蘭縣](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/宜蘭縣 "wikilink")[大同鄉的數個部落](../Page/大同鄉_\(台灣\).md "wikilink")\[2\]。這種語言出現於1930年代至1940年代，由於[日本殖民政府在台灣推行強力的日語教育](../Page/台灣日治時期.md "wikilink")，[日語逐漸成為山區](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/日語 "wikilink")[各族原住民之間的共通語](../Page/臺灣原住民族.md "wikilink")，[宜蘭縣部分](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/宜蘭縣 "wikilink")[泰雅族](../Page/泰雅族.md "wikilink")甚至喪失了[族語能力](../Page/泰雅語.md "wikilink")，改以日語為母語。且日語母語者和泰雅語母語者都無法理解寒溪語\[3\]。

寒溪語一直到2006年才由[國立東華大學](../Page/國立東華大學.md "wikilink")教授簡月真與日本語言學家識別出來\[4\]\[5\]，並以語言通行的地區之一－宜蘭縣大同鄉寒溪村－命名\[6\]。2006年，[中華民國原住民族委員會曾在官方族語認定的四十餘個方言別中另增](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/中華民國原住民族委員會 "wikilink")「宜蘭寒溪泰雅語」一項\[7\]，但隨即在2011年將其排除\[8\]；再加上近年來[中華民國國語](../Page/中華民國國語.md "wikilink")的普及，使得寒溪語受到極大的威脅\[9\]。

## 名稱

使用寒溪語的四個部落都位於[宜蘭縣](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/宜蘭縣 "wikilink")，但對於自己的語言有著不同的稱呼。[大同鄉寒溪村稱寒溪語為](../Page/大同鄉_\(台灣\).md "wikilink")「nihongo」、「kangke no hanasi」、「kangke no ke」、「寒溪泰雅語」，東岳村有「nihongo」、「tang-ow no hanasi」、「tang-ow no ke」、「地方語言」等，金洋村有「kinus no hanasi」、「博愛路的話」，澳花村有「nihongo」、「zibun no hanasi」、「土話」等\[10\]。

## 歷史

1895年[清朝](../Page/清朝.md "wikilink")於[甲午戰爭戰敗](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/甲午戰爭 "wikilink")，同年簽訂《[馬關條約](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/馬關條約 "wikilink")》，日本因此在台灣展開了長達五十年的統治\[11\]\[12\]1910年代起，南澳地區開始實施「集體遷村」（），深山的部落被迫分散、移至淺山或平地另組新部落；此舉造成寒溪村、東岳村、金洋村及澳花村等四個村落中同時有[泰雅人和](../Page/泰雅族.md "wikilink")[賽德克人混居](../Page/賽德克族.md "wikilink")。語言、文化相異的兩個族群為了相互溝通，便採用泰雅語、賽德克語和日語接觸後形成的「[接觸語言](../Page/語言接觸.md "wikilink")」作為[共通語](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/共通語 "wikilink")，約1930年代後出生的世代更以此作為第一語言，母語者的出現也使這種接觸語言逐漸發展為成熟的克里奧爾語。\[13\]

## 性質

寒溪語是世界上唯一基於日語的[克里奥尔语](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/克里奥尔语 "wikilink")\[14\]，以[賽德克語](../Page/賽德克語.md "wikilink")都達群的單詞及語法為基礎，並吸收了大量的[日語](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/日語 "wikilink")、[賽考利克泰雅語](../Page/賽考利克泰雅語.md "wikilink")等語種之詞語與語法\[15\]。

語言學資料庫[Glottolog將寒溪語歸類在日本語族下](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glottolog "wikilink")\[16\]。

## 地理分布

The [creole](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creole_language "wikilink") is spoke in [Yilan County](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yilan_County,_Taiwan "wikilink") in Eastern Taiwan, mainly in Tungyueh Village, Chinyang Village, Aohua Village, and Hanhsi Village, with evidence of differences [varieties](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variety_\(linguistics\) "wikilink") of the creole found in each.\[17\]\[18\]儘管不知道寒溪語使用者的確切人數，但可能推測應該少於四個村的總人口，即大約3000人\[19\]\[20\]，或介於兩千至三千人之間\[21\]。While the creole is currently used among all generations,\[22\] younger generations are receiving less exposure to it, causing the language to become [endangered](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endangered_language "wikilink").\[23\] While older speakers may not be fluent in [Mandarin](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese "wikilink"), younger generations are consistently using Mandarin more.\[24\]

Japanese language still has some influence in Taiwan society today. Japanese based Internet sites in Japanese are viewed by Taiwanese, with “few other countries \[producing\] as much information in Japanese as Taiwan”.\[25\] Evidence of Japanese signboards, particularly with the use of the Japanese [hiragana](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/hiragana "wikilink") character の (pronounced “no”) may be seen in Taiwan.\[26\] Taiwanese citizens who received Japanese education during the annexation of the nation still speak Japanese fluently today.\[27\] There are three generations of Yilan Creole speakers, with the older and middle generations using the creole significantly more than the younger generation.\[28\]

In Tungyueh Village, younger generations seem to have lost the Yilan Creole, while there are still fluent younger generation speakers of the Creole in Aohua.\[29\] After a push for preserving a more traditional and pure sense of [Atayal heritage](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atayal_people "wikilink"), the Yilan Creole that is imbued with Japanese features was removed from language examinations. This further instilled a tendency toward Mandarin and a push away from Yilan Creole in young speakers.\[30\] While older generations prefer to speak Japanese or Yilan-Creole with people of the same age, they will often use Atayal or Mandarin mixed with Yilan Creole when speaking with younger generations; younger generations will similarly prefer Mandarin with same-age speakers, but may use Yilan Creole with older generations.\[31\]

Due to the migration of Japanese from the western part of the Japan to Taiwan in the early half of the twentieth century, with seventy percent of Taiwan’s immigrants being from western Japan, Yilan Creole acquired some of the regional dialects of western Japan. However, these [regional dialects](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dialect "wikilink") are still used unconsciously.\[32\]

## 音系

整體而言，寒溪語的語音系統和澤敖利泰雅語十分接近，而大量來自日語的借詞則引入了許多泰雅語原先沒有的輔音；相較起來，賽德克語對寒溪語的影響則十分不明顯\[33\]。

寒溪語繼承了[日語輔音中的](../Page/日語音系.md "wikilink")[濁](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/清濁音 "wikilink")[塞音](../Page/塞音.md "wikilink")（、和）、[濁齒齦擦音](../Page/濁齒齦擦音.md "wikilink")、[清齦顎擦音](../Page/清齦顎擦音.md "wikilink")、[清齒齦塞擦音](../Page/清齒齦塞擦音.md "wikilink")，以及齦顎塞擦音和；但未保留[清雙脣擦音](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/清雙脣擦音 "wikilink")和[小舌鼻音](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/小舌鼻音 "wikilink")\[35\]。

從泰雅語繼承的輔音則包括[聲門塞音](../Page/聲門塞音.md "wikilink")、[齒齦流音](../Page/齿龈边近音.md "wikilink")、[清軟顎擦音](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/清軟顎擦音 "wikilink")，但未保留[清小舌塞音](../Page/清小舌塞音.md "wikilink")。此外，雖然日語可以置於音節尾的輔音只有鼻音，可以置於音節首的鼻音則只有和，但寒溪語的、、、和都可以出現在音節尾，如*mi**t***（山羊）、*biyo**k***（豬）、*yuta**s***（祖父）、*wala**x***（雨）、*tala**h***（紅色），則可以出現在音節首，如***ng**san*（家）\[36\]。

部分日語單詞中的和在寒溪語中會被置換為，如日語單詞「」（*suwa**r**e*，坐下）變成*suwa**l**e*；「」（*kyô**d**ai*，兄弟）變成*kyô**l**ai*\[37\]。

### 元音

寒溪語共有六個[元音](../Page/元音.md "wikilink")，其中僅是源於泰雅語，其餘五個[音位](../Page/音位.md "wikilink")為日語和泰雅語共享\[38\] ；另有研究認為寒溪語不存在元音\[39\]。另外，雖然[標準日語中將](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/標準日語 "wikilink")*u*讀作[不圓唇的](../Page/元音圓唇度.md "wikilink")，但所有寒溪語含*u*的詞彙皆被讀作圓唇的\[40\]。

### 超音段特徵

泰雅語是[輕重音語言](../Page/輕重讀.md "wikilink")\[41\]，且所有原生單詞的重音都在最後一個音節；而[日語則是](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/日語高低重音 "wikilink")[高低重音](../Page/高低重音.md "wikilink")語言\[42\]。寒溪語的重音系統與泰雅語較為接近，其重音通常落在最後一個音節，例如（我）、（之前）\[43\]。

一些日語詞彙含有[長輔音和](../Page/輔音延長.md "wikilink")[長元音](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/長元音 "wikilink")，但進入寒溪語中其[音長](../Page/音長.md "wikilink")可能會被縮短，如單詞「」（*ga**kk**ô*，學校）轉為*ga**k**o*，「」（*by**ô**ki*，疾病）轉為*by**o**ki*。然而，上例「」（*kyô**d**ai*）轉為*kyô**l**ai*時，元音長度並沒有發生變化\[44\]。

## 語法

### 構詞

寒溪語同時使用[詞綴](../Page/詞綴.md "wikilink")和時間[副詞來標記](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/副詞 "wikilink")[動詞的](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/動詞 "wikilink")[時態](../Page/時體氣.md "wikilink")，即使該詞源於泰雅語，依然套用日語的詞形變化\[45\]。

Some processes of negation in Yilan Creole use Japanese derived forms to accommodate the [已然語氣](../Page/已然語氣.md "wikilink") that is part of Atayal grammar.\[46\]

## 詞彙

In Yilan Creole, phonological forms of words are derived from Japanese, while the [semantic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semantics "wikilink") properties are derived from Atayal.\[56\] According to a study on Yilan Creole in Tungyueh Village by Zeitoun, Teng, and Wu, “the proportion of Atayal-derived words in the Yilan Creole basic vocabulary is 18.3%, and that of Japanese-derived words is 35.6%. Both Atayal-derived words and Japanese-derived words can be used for 33.8% of the items”.\[57\] [Mandarin](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese "wikilink") and [Southern Min](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Min "wikilink") words also exist, but far less. Older generation speakers tend to use Atayal and Japanese variants more often than younger generational speakers, who prefer Mandarin variants, when they exist. Mandarin based words lose their tone in Yilan Creole.\[58\]

Many Atayal words relating to nature, animals, and plants survived in the creole. Vocabulary of most concepts such as these related to traditional [Atayal](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atayal_people "wikilink") and [Seediq](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seediq_people "wikilink") life and culture are retained in Yilan Creole.\[59\]

### 後綴

The verb [suffix](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/suffix "wikilink") *–suru* in Yilan Creole**',** derived from the Japanese verb ‘to do’, is similar to its Japanese counterpart, except in that it is a [bound morpheme](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bound_and_unbound_morphemes "wikilink") while the Japanese *-suru* can stand alone as a [free morpheme](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bound_and_unbound_morphemes "wikilink").\[60\] Also, Yilan Creole –*suru* can attach to nouns, adjectives, and, among young generational speakers, verbs. However, older generation speakers do not accept verbs + -*suru* combinations.

Another affix in Yilan Creole is the Japanese derived –*rasyeru* for causative forms. However, while Japanese [inflection](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/inflection "wikilink") differs between consonant versus vowel ending verbs, the Yilan Creole suffix does not.\[61\]

### 複合詞

There are four types of [compound words](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compound_\(linguistics\) "wikilink") in Yilan Creole:\[62\]

Type 1: Atayal-derived word + Atayal-derived word (e.g., *hopa*-*la’i*)

Type 2: Atayal-derived word + Japanese-derived word (e.g., *hopa*-*tenki*)

Type 3: Japanese-derived word + Atayal-derived word (e.g., *naka*-*lukus*, *kako*-*balay*)

Type 4: Japanese-derived word + Japanese-derived word (e.g., *naka*-*pangcyu*, *unme*-*zyoto*)

While Type 1 compound words occur in Atayal, Japanese does not have occurrences of Type 4 compound words in its own language, suggesting that Type 2, 3, and 4 compound words are creations of Yilan Creole.\[63\]

### 形容詞與副詞

[Adjectives](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adjective "wikilink") and [adverbs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/adverb "wikilink") in Yilan Creole derive from both Japanese and Atayal.\[67\] Ayatal adjectives are primarily used for colors and subjective feelings. Unlike Japanese, adjectives in the creole languages are not inflected and tense is expressed through temporal adverbs.\[68\] Adjectives in Yilan Creole may also act as adverbs when modifying verbs. For example, the word *lokah* ‘good, strong’ “functions as an adjective when describing *anta* ‘you’ in the phrase *lokah anta* ‘ you (are) strong’ … while *lokah* functions as an adverb as in *lokah benkyo* ‘to study hard’”.\[69\]

## 文字

寒溪語並沒有規範的正寫法；一些研究資料在轉寫時，源於日語的單詞使用[訓令式羅馬字書寫](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/訓令式羅馬字 "wikilink")，源於漢語的單詞以[漢語拼音書寫](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/漢語拼音 "wikilink")，其餘則以及[泰雅語拉丁字母書寫](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/泰雅語音系#音位字母 "wikilink")。\[70\]\[71\]

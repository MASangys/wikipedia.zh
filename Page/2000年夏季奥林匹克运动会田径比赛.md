On April 10, 2008, the IOC disqualified both U.S. relay teams and asked for Jones' teammates' medals to be returned.[1](http://www.olympic.org/uk/news/olympic_news/full_story_uk.asp?id=2536) [France](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/France_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics "wikilink") ([Linda Ferga](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linda_Ferga "wikilink"), [Muriel Hurtis](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muriel_Hurtis "wikilink"), [Fabe Dia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fabe_Dia "wikilink"), [Christine Arron](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christine_Arron "wikilink"), [Sandra Citte](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandra_Citte "wikilink")\*) finished fourth in the 4×100 m relay in a time of 42.42, and [Nigeria](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics "wikilink") ([Olabisi Afolabi](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olabisi_Afolabi "wikilink"), [Opara Charity](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opara_Charity "wikilink"), [Rosemary Okafor](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosemary_Okafor "wikilink"), [Falilat Ogunkoya-Osheku](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falilat_Ogunkoya-Osheku "wikilink"), [Doris Jacob](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doris_Jacob "wikilink")\*) finished fourth in the 4×400 m relay in a time of 3:23.80, but no medals were redistributed, the IOC opting to leave the medal spots forfeited by the U.S. teams vacant.

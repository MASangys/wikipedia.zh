The **Proposed 2019 amendment to the Constitution of Malaysia** (literally **Amendment of the Article (1) 2 of the Constitution of Malaysia**) or more commonly known as the **Restoration of Sabah and Sarawak Status as Equal Partners within the Federation of [Malaysia](../Page/马来西亚.md "wikilink")** is a proposed [constitutional amendment](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/憲法修正 "wikilink") in the country [Parliament](../Page/马来西亚国会.md "wikilink") (*[Dewan Rakyat](../Page/馬來西亞下議院.md "wikilink")*) between the [federal government](../Page/马来西亚政府.md "wikilink") and governments of  and  to restore the status of the current two states in [East Malaysia](../Page/東馬.md "wikilink") of [Sabah](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/沙巴 "wikilink") and [Sarawak](../Page/砂拉越.md "wikilink") as according to the original content of [Malaysia Agreement](../Page/马来西亚协定.md "wikilink") signed in 1963.\[1\]\[2\]\[3\]

Through the second bill reading followed by more than six-hours debate in the country Parliament on 9 April,\[4\]\[5\] the bill for the constitution amendment was unable to be passed following its failure to reach two-thirds majority support (148 votes) with 138 [member of parliament](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/国会议员 "wikilink") (MPs) (mostly comprising the government and their allied parties) supporting the move for the restoration of the two entities status while 59 (mostly comprising the MPs from the [opposition](../Page/马来西亚在野党.md "wikilink")) choose to "abstained" in the voting with no MPs from both sides of the political factions opposing the amendment.\[6\]</ref>\[7\]\[8\]\[9\]

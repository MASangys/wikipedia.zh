**唐·佩皮恩·希珀**（英文：**Don Pepijn
Schipper**，出生于1980年2月27日），艺名为**唐·迪亚布罗**（英文：**Don
Diablo**），中文昵称为大菠萝（Diablo的音译），是一名荷兰DJ、制作人、音乐家及[庫福爾登的](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/庫福爾登 "wikilink")[電子舞曲歌手及词曲作者](../Page/電子舞曲.md "wikilink")。他在2018年的《[DJ
Mag](../Page/DJ雜誌.md "wikilink")》百大DJ榜中排名第7。\[1\]他还是1001
Tracklists的2018年度制作人。\[2\]2016年，他在[Beatport的年度未来贝斯艺术家榜上获得桂冠](../Page/Beatport.md "wikilink")。
\[3\]

## 早期生活

希珀于1980年2月27日出生于[荷兰的](../Page/荷兰.md "wikilink")[庫福爾登](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/庫福爾登 "wikilink")。在年仅15岁时就签下了第一个厂牌合约。2005年末，他在家乡荷兰成立了自己的厂牌兼媒体品牌——Sellout
Sessions。在地下发布了热曲《Blow》、《Who's Your Daddy》、《Pain Is Temporary, Pride Is
Forever》、《》和《》后，成功接连不断。《Blow》是首张在国际范围的俱乐部内掀起热潮的唱片，版权授权了超过10个国家。希珀还完成了研究，获得了新闻业学士学位。

## 职业生涯

### 1997–2005: 缓慢起步

迪亚布罗曾在加拿大、英国、美国、澳大利亚、巴西以及以色列演出。他曾在诸多俱乐部及音乐节上演出：曾在2002年担任"Passion"的俱乐部上担任驻场DJ，还曾在Ministry
of
Sound、Gatecrasher、Dancevalley、Godskitchen、Impulz、Mysteryland、Creamfields、Haoman
17、Turnmills、Chasing Summer、Innercity、Northern Lights、Extrema
Outdoorn、ElectroNation and Razzmatazz及Space in
Ibiza演出。迪亚布罗也与包括、、[迪波洛](../Page/迪波洛.md "wikilink")、、、、Rox、、在内的制作人合作，混音了[巴士底樂團](../Page/巴士底樂團.md "wikilink")、[Birdy](../Page/Birdy_\(歌手\).md "wikilink")、[泰尼·坦普](../Page/泰尼·坦普.md "wikilink")、[米卡](../Page/米卡_\(歌手\).md "wikilink")、[化學兄弟](../Page/化學兄弟.md "wikilink")、、[街头霸王](../Page/街頭霸王_\(虛擬樂團\).md "wikilink")、、[伊基·波普和](../Page/伊基·波普.md "wikilink")的作品。据北美音乐博客主“The
Hype Machine”所写，迪亚布罗曾在世界上多次获得“最多次被写入博客的艺术家”称号。\[4\]

2004年，迪亚布罗启动了音乐项目——Divided。此项目有两部热门专辑——《The Music, the
People》（《艺术，人民》）及2005年出版的《》。《轻松爱人》是一首原由及[菲爾·柯林斯创作于](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/菲爾·柯林斯 "wikilink")1985年的专辑，于20年后由Divided创作出舞曲重制版。

### 2006–2009: 《人生如节》

2006年，迪亚布罗被授权给创作的英国榜单前三作品《》（《超越者》）。此专辑原授予由迪亚布罗与阿姆斯特丹俱乐部宣传者罗伊·阿夫尼（Roy
Avni）（又名电子国度，Electro Nation）。2008年，迪亚布罗发布了其处女座——专辑《Life Is a
Festival》（《人生如节》）。 2009年，与英国歌手、作曲家、饶舌歌手及制作人所创作的《""》登上了英国舞曲榜第15名。

### 2010–2013

In 2010, a collaboration with  resulted in the hit single "" that
charted in the Dutch Singles Chart as well as the Belgian
[Ultratip](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultratop榜 "wikilink") chart.

In 2013 Diablo released new songs on 's Axtone label, [Nicky
Romero](../Page/尼基·羅米洛.md "wikilink")'s Protocol Recordings, 's Size X
imprint and [Spinnin' Records](../Page/斯宾尼唱片.md "wikilink") label. He
created the theme song for the 2013 video game *[Batman: Arkham
Origins](../Page/蝙蝠侠：阿卡姆起源.md "wikilink")*.

His song "Starlight (Could You Be Mine)", made with UK DJ/producer Matt
Nash, was debuted during the last [Swedish House
Mafia](../Page/瑞典浩室黑手黨.md "wikilink") concert, after
Axwell signed it to his label.\[5\] The song became a Beatport Top 5
hit, and later several of his other releases reaching the Beatport Top
10.\[6\] The music video, directed by Kyle Padilla, was published on 7
February 2014, around seven months after the song's release.

During 2013  debuted six of Don's releases on his BBC Radio 1 show. He
had a residency at Las Vegas' night club "Light", played the Departures
Ibiza closing party alongside  and , and toured the UK with [Nicky
Romero](../Page/尼基·羅米洛.md "wikilink"). He had a sold out solo tour in
Asia and the USA.

### 2014–2016

In 2014, Diablo premiered his new visual concept "The Hexagon" with Los
Angeles-based visual company "VSquared Labs".

In October 2014, Diablo made his debut on DJ Mag's Top 100 DJs list at
No. 82.\[7\] In October 2015, Diablo was awarded with [DJ
Magazine](../Page/DJ雜誌.md "wikilink")'s "highest climber of the year"
prize, jumping 52 places to No. 30 on the Top 100 DJs list for
2015.\[8\]

Diablo launched his Hexagon record label. The first release, a remix of
Alex Adair's "You Make Me Feel Better" held the no. 1 position on
Beatport for almost two weeks and the label was awarded "best new label
of the year".\[9\]

In 2015 his HEXAGON radio show was aired in over 35 countries and holds
a top 10 position in the iTunes podcast section every week.

Diablo has directed all of his own music videos. In 2013 Warner Brothers
asked Don to create the worldwide theme song for the much acclaimed
*[Batman: Arkham Origins](../Page/蝙蝠侠：阿卡姆起源.md "wikilink")* game. The
track and accompanying video became a viral hit; it was viewed millions
of times on YouTube.

In 2015 Diablo collaborated with fellow Dutch DJ
[Tiësto](../Page/提雅斯多.md "wikilink") on the track "" featuring
vocals from Danish singer and producer .

Throughout 2016 and 2017, Diablo went on Tour Across Europe, this sold
out across multiple venues including his first headline show in London.

### 2017: *Past.Present.Future*

In 2017, Don Diablo released *Past.Present.Future*, a compilation of
previously released tracks, including hits "On My Mind," "Cutting
Shapes," and his collaboration with [Tiësto](../Page/提雅斯多.md "wikilink")
"Chemicals" featuring Thomas Troelsen.\[10\]

Diablo won DJ Mag's 2017 Highest Future House award and was ranked \#11
overall in the [DJ Mag](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/DJ_Mag "wikilink")
Top 100 Poll.\[11\]

A packed global tour schedule saw him play the mainstages of
Tomorrowland, EDC, Creamfields, Mysteryland, Lollapalooza, and Ultra
Music Festival whilst selling out his Headline Artist shows in the UK,
US & European markets.

On March 17, 2017, Don Diablo premiered a new collaboration with MARNIK
and ' unaccredited  singer, Reece Bullimore entitled, Children of a
Miracle.\[12\]

On September 6, 2017, Don Diablo released a single with Nashville-based
singer / songwriter Holly Winter, titled "Don't Let Go".\[13\]

On November 2017, Diablo released “Take Her Place” with electronic trio
A R I Z O N A. He announced through Instagram that he will be releasing
his first album in 10 years, entitled “FUTURE”, accompanied by a world
tour set to kick off during the first half of 2018.

On December 15, 2017, he released his sixth single, entitled "You Can't
Change Me". The music video stars BONETICS, a dancer that competed on
[Britain's Got Talent](../Page/英国达人.md "wikilink").\[14\]

### 2018-Present: *Future*

On January 19, Don Diablo released the seventh single from *Future*,
"People Say", with actor/songwriter, Paije Richardson.

On February 8, he embarked on his sold out Future North American Tour,
making stops at Los Angeles' Fonda Theatre and New York's Terminal 5,
among others.\[15\]

His second studio album, **, was released via his label Hexagon on 9
February 2018.\[16\]\[17\]

On March 13, he released the eighth single to *FUTURE* entitled "Head
Up" with singer James Newman. On March 23, he released the ninth single
to *FUTURE* entitled "Believe", available on the deluxe edition
(released same day) with actor/singer-songwriter [Ansel
Elgort](../Page/安塞爾·埃爾格特.md "wikilink").\[18\] On April 25, he
released the tenth single to *FUTURE* entitled "Give Me Love" with
singer .\[19\]

On May 11, his remix of [Kygo](../Page/凱戈.md "wikilink") and The Night
Game's song "Kids In Love" was released.\[20\] On July 12, he released
his single "No Good", in partnership with artist Zonderling. On August
3rd, he released his new single, Heaven to Me, featuring Alex Clare. On
September the 9th, he released a single called "Survive" featuring Emeli
Sandé and Gucci Mane.

## Philanthropy

Diablo was the first ambassador for Dance4Life, an international
UN-supported action campaign to fight back against HIV and AIDS. Diablo
travelled to South Africa to visit several Dance4Life affiliated
projects in Johannesburg and Cape Town. MTV Networks shot an intense
documentary of Don's visit to these projects.\[21\]

Furthermore, in 2018 Don Diablo's tour has a major charitable aspect, as
all ticket proceeds are set to be donated to the Dutch Cancer
Society.\[22\]

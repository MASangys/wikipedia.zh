**M-LOK**，即“模块化锁定”（）的缩写，是由[美国](../Page/美国.md "wikilink")枪械配件生产商[马格普工业公司](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/马格普工业公司 "wikilink")[专利](../Page/专利.md "wikilink")研发的一款免费许可\[1\]的“负空间”（negative
space，指凹洞式）导轨界面系统（rail interface
system），用来部分取代[皮卡汀尼导轨主要用来给](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/皮卡汀尼导轨 "wikilink")[护木提供各种附件的安装点](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/护木 "wikilink")。与传统的皮卡汀尼导轨相比，“负空间”界面重量轻，线条更平滑，而且不影响枪管风冷散热。

M-LOK是从马格普之前研发不成功的MOE（Magpul Original
Equipment，“马格普原创器材”）项目基础上改进而来的设计，两者有相似之处但是并不能相互兼容。作为“负空间”设计，M-LOK主要与复仇者武器系统（VLTOR
Weapon
Systems）[开源的KeyMod设计进行竞争](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/开源 "wikilink")\[2\]。

## 历史

[Magpul_MOE_M-LOK_handguard_on_a_privatly_assembled_AR-15.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Magpul_MOE_M-LOK_handguard_on_a_privatly_assembled_AR-15.jpg "fig:Magpul_MOE_M-LOK_handguard_on_a_privatly_assembled_AR-15.jpg")[半自动步枪](../Page/半自动步枪.md "wikilink")上的马格普M-LOK护木\]\]
[M110A1_SDMR.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:M110A1_SDMR.jpg "fig:M110A1_SDMR.jpg")
SDMR featuring a Geissele M-LOK rail handguard\]\] A prototype of the
MOE slot was revealed by Magpul in late 2007 together with their Masada
Concept Rifle (which would later be known as the [Adaptive Combat
Rifle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adaptive_Combat_Rifle "wikilink")).
The MOE slot system was released by Magpul in 2009 as a feature on their
MOE handguards, and at the same time compatible accessories such as
Picatinny rail sections, direct MOE mounted light mounts, grips, bipod
studs, etc. were released.

The MOE slot standard was never officially released, and a drawback to
the system was that the rear side of the panel had to be accessed in
order to mount an accessories, limiting its application. The MOE slot
system uses a [weld
nut](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/weld_nut "wikilink") which has to be
placed manually on the inside of the handguard before mounting, making
the slot system unsuited for applications such as
[free-floating](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/free-floating_barrel "wikilink")
handguards. Also, depending on the accessory item, the spacing
increments between the MOE slots were not small or uniform enough to
adjust the desired placement of accessories.

Acknowledging shortcomings of the MOE systems, Magpul drafted M-LOK as a
new and improved mounting standard which was released in 2014 replacing
the existing MOE slot. The M-LOK rail specification included
[metric](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/metric_system "wikilink")
dimensions instead of
[imperial](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/imperial_units "wikilink"), and
utilizes a [T-slot
nut](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/T-slot_nut "wikilink") capable of
only 90-degree rotation, reinforced by [thread-locking
fluid](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/thread-locking_fluid "wikilink"),
making it suited for applications on free-floating handguards. It was
designed to work with both metal and polymer parts.

In 2016 [Colt
Canada](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colt_Canada "wikilink") developed
and released the Modular Rail Rifle (MRR) that uses a monolithic [upper
receiver](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/receiver_\(firearm\) "wikilink")
with the M-LOK attachment system.\[3\] In 2017, several companies
produce M-LOK handguards as well as accessories like Picatinny rail
strips, [vertical
foregrips](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/vertical_foregrip "wikilink"),
[bipods](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/bipod "wikilink"),
[sling](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/sling_\(firearms\) "wikilink")
adaptors, and
[flashlight](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/tactical_light "wikilink")
mounts.\[4\]

[21st_century_bench.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:21st_century_bench.jpg "fig:21st_century_bench.jpg")的公共长椅\]\]
**新未来主义** 是20世纪末至21世纪初的 [艺术](../Page/艺术.md "wikilink"),
[设计](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/设计 "wikilink"), 和
[建筑运动](../Page/建筑.md "wikilink")。 这可以被认为是违背了
[后现代主义](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/后现代建筑 "wikilink")
的态度，代表对更美好未来的
[理想化](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/唯心主义 "wikilink")
的信念，以及“需要将现代融洽与技术相结合”。\[1\]

这种 [超前](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/超前 "wikilink") 的运动\[2\]
是对快速发展的城市的审美和功能的未来主义的重新思考。
[第二次世界大战结束后在世界范围内开始的工业化给人们带来了新的生活思潮](../Page/第二次世界大战.md "wikilink")，艺术和建筑，导致后现代主义，新现代主义和新未来主义。\[3\]

在西方国家，[未来主义建筑演变为](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/未来主义建筑 "wikilink")[Art
Deco](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Art_Deco "wikilink")，[Googie
movement和](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Googie_architecture "wikilink")[high-tech
architecture](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/high-tech_architecture "wikilink")，最近进入[新未来主义](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neo-futurism "wikilink")。\[4\]

## Origins

Pioneered in the late 1960s and early 70s by American architects
[Buckminster
Fuller](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buckminster_Fuller "wikilink")\[5\]\[6\]
and [John C. Portman,
Jr.](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_C._Portman,_Jr. "wikilink");\[7\]\[8\]\[9\]
Finnish-American architect and industrial designer [Eero
Saarinen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eero_Saarinen "wikilink"),\[10\]\[11\]
[Archigram](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archigram "wikilink"), a
British avant-garde architectural group ([Peter
Cook](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Cook_\(architect\) "wikilink"),
Warren Chalk, [Ron
Herron](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ron_Herron "wikilink"), Dennis
Crompton, [Michael
Webb](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Webb_\(architect\) "wikilink")
and David Greene) based at the [Architectural
Association](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Architectural_Association_School_of_Architecture "wikilink"),
London;\[12\] American avant-garde architectural group ArchiGO, centered
around the [Illinois Institute of
Technology](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illinois_Institute_of_Technology "wikilink");\[13\]\[14\]
Danish architect [Henning
Larsen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henning_Larsen "wikilink");\[15\]
Czech architect [Jan
Kaplický](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jan_Kaplický "wikilink");\[16\]\[17\]\[18\]
Italian [light
sculptor](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/light_art "wikilink") Marco
Lodola;\[19\]\[20\]\[21\] American concept artist [Syd
Mead](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syd_Mead "wikilink");\[22\] American
theatre screenwriter Greg Allen\[23\] and Russian poets Andrei
Voznesensky, Serge Segay and Rea Nikonova.\[24\]

Although it was never built, the Fun Palace (1961) interpreted by
architect [Cedric
Price](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cedric_Price "wikilink") as a
"giant neo-futurist machine"\[25\]\[26\] influenced other architects,
notably [Richard
Rogers](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Rogers "wikilink") and
[Renzo Piano](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renzo_Piano "wikilink"),
whose [Pompidou
Centre](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centre_Georges_Pompidou "wikilink")
extended many of Price's ideas.

## Definition

Neo-futurism was relaunched in 2007 after the dissemination of "The
Neo-Futuristic City
[Manifesto](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifesto "wikilink")"\[27\]\[28\]\[29\]
included in the candidature presented to the [Bureau International des
Expositions](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bureau_International_des_Expositions "wikilink")
(BIE)\[30\] and written by innovation designer [Vito Di
Bari](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vito_Di_Bari "wikilink"),\[31\]\[32\]\[33\]
a former executive director at
[UNESCO](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO "wikilink")),\[34\] to
outline his vision for the city of
[Milan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milan "wikilink") at the time of
the Universal [Expo
2015](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expo_2015 "wikilink"). Di Bari
defined his neo-futuristic vision as the "cross-pollination of art,
cutting edge technologies and ethical values combined to create a
pervasively higher quality of life";\[35\] he referenced the [Fourth
Pillar of Sustainable Development
Theory](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_development#Culture "wikilink")\[36\]
and reported that the name had been inspired by the [United
Nations](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "wikilink") report
*[Our Common
Future](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Our_Common_Future "wikilink")*.\[37\]

[Jean-Louis
Cohen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Louis_Cohen "wikilink") has
defined neo-futurism\[38\]\[39\] as "a corollary to technology, being
the structures built today byproducts of new materials to create
previously impossible forms." Etan J. Ilfeld wrote that in the
contemporary neo-futurist aesthetics "the machine becomes an integral
element of the creative process itself, and generates the emergence of
artistic modes that would have been impossible prior to computer
technology."\[40\] Reyner Banham's definition of "une architecture
autre" is a call for an architecture that technologically overcomes all
previous architectures but possessing an expressive form,\[41\] as
Banham stated about neo-futuristic "Archigram’s Plug-in Computerized
City, form does not have to follow function into oblivion."\[42\]

## In art and architecture

Inspired by Futurist architect [Antonio
Sant'Elia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antonio_Sant'Elia "wikilink")
and pioneered from early 1960s and late 1970s by thought leader Hal
Foster,\[43\] French architect Denis Laming, American architects
[William
Pereira](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Pereira "wikilink")\[44\]\[45\]\[46\]
and [Charles
Luckman](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Luckman "wikilink")\[47\]\[48\]
and Danish architects [Henning
Larsen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henning_Larsen "wikilink")\[49\]
and [Jørn
Utzon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jørn_Utzon "wikilink").\[50\] The
neo-futurist avant-garde movement has been relaunched in 2007 by Expo
2015 Innovation Designer [Vito Di
Bari](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vito_Di_Bari "wikilink").
Neo-Futurist architecture and art have been creatively inspired by
Iraqi-British Pritzker Prize architect [Zaha
Hadid](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zaha_Hadid "wikilink"),\[51\]\[52\]\[53\]
and Spanish architect [Santiago
Calatrava](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santiago_Calatrava "wikilink");\[54\]\[55\]\[56\]
Indian sculptor [Anish
Kapoor](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anish_Kapoor "wikilink")\[57\]\[58\]
and Dutch kinetic sculptor [Theo
Jansen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theo_Jansen "wikilink").\[59\]
Italian Innovation Designer [Vito Di
Bari](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vito_Di_Bari "wikilink") is
considered the thought leader of the movement,\[60\] his vision of the
“cross-pollination of art and technology for a better world” has been
defined by [Steve
Jobs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Jobs "wikilink")\[61\] as the
"post-PC DNA"\[62\]\[63\] and it is shared by acclaimed architects,
designers and artists such as the youngest recipient ever of the
[Pritzker
Prize](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pritzker_Prize "wikilink") Japanese
architect [Ryue
Nishizawa](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryue_Nishizawa "wikilink"),
architect of Ecoville in Curitiba and of the first rotating residential
buildings Bruno de Franco, Design for Asia Award 2004-7-9 recipient Hong
Kong Architect Gary Chang, Brazilian architect Rodrigo Ohtake, Lubetkin
Prize Winner British designer [Thomas
Heatherwick](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Heatherwick "wikilink"),
Design for Asia 2008 Award recipient Japanese artist [Tokujin
Yoshioka](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokujin_Yoshioka "wikilink"),
Italian artist Mario Arlati, Polish artist Karina Smigla-Bobinski.

## People

The relaunch of neo-futurism in the 21st century has been creatively
inspired by the Iraqi-British [Pritzker Architecture
Prize](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pritzker_Architecture_Prize "wikilink")-winning
architect [Zaha
Hadid](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zaha_Hadid "wikilink"),\[64\]\[65\]\[66\]
Spanish architect [Santiago
Calatrava](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santiago_Calatrava "wikilink")\[67\]
\[68\]\[69\] and by Vito DiBari.\[70\]\[71\]\[72\]

Neo-futurist architects, designers and artists are French architect
;\[73\]\[74\]\[75\] American artists Josh Hadar, Erin Sparler,\[76\]
Marlow Rodale,\[77\] Panayiotis Terzis,\[78\] and Miguel Ovalle;\[79\]
urban-noise artist Joseph Young;\[80\] French designer Patrick
Jouin\[81\] British artist [Olivia
Peake](http://www.oliviapeake.com);\[82\] Japanese designer Yuima
Nakazato,\[83\]\[84\] Swedish artist [Simon
Stålenhag](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simon_Stålenhag "wikilink"),\[85\]
and Greek artist [Charis
Tsevis](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charis_Tsevis "wikilink").\[86\]\[87\]\[88\]
Neo-futurism has absorbed some of the [high-tech
architecture](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/high-tech_architecture "wikilink")’s
themes and ideas, incorporating elements of high-tech industry and
technology іnto building design:\[89\] technology and context is the
focus of some architects of this movement such as Buckminster Fuller,
Norman Foster,\[90\]\[91\] Kenzo Tange, Renzo Piano, Richard Rogers,
Frei Otto, and Santiago Calatrava.\[92\]\[93\]

**俄勒冈和加利福尼亚铁路赠地**面积约为，位于[俄勒冈州](../Page/俄勒冈州.md "wikilink")西部18个县内。1866年，[美国政府曾经提出将铁路沿线的土地奖励给在](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/美国政府 "wikilink")[波特蘭 (俄勒岡州)和](../Page/波特蘭_\(俄勒岡州\).md "wikilink")[旧金山](../Page/旧金山.md "wikilink")之间修建铁路的公司，此后[俄勒冈和加利福尼亚铁路拿到了这一片土地](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/俄勒冈和加利福尼亚铁路 "wikilink")。1916年，联邦政府赎回了这些土地。这些土地目前由[美国土地管理局管理](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/美国土地管理局 "wikilink")。18个县可以获得采伐土地上的树木的收入的一半，而另一半交给[联邦政府](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/联邦政府 "wikilink")。土地的采伐收入是这些县财政的一个重要来源。

The most recent source of income from the lands, an extension of the [Secure Rural Schools and Community Self-Determination Act of 2000](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secure_Rural_Schools_and_Community_Self-Determination_Act_of_2000 "wikilink"), was last renewed in 2013 but at vastly reduced spending levels, leaving some counties scrambling to find new sources of funding. In late 2013, the [美国众议院](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/美国众议院 "wikilink") was considering a bill that would resume the funding and increase timber harvests to provide additional income to the counties.\[2\]

## 起源

为了促进[美国](../Page/美国.md "wikilink")西部各州的经济发展，1866年7月[美国国会](../Page/美国国会.md "wikilink")通过了《[俄勒冈和加利福尼亚铁路法案](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/俄勒冈和加利福尼亚铁路法案 "wikilink")》。该法案给[波特蘭和](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/波特兰 "wikilink")[旧金山](../Page/旧金山.md "wikilink")之间修建铁路的公司的土地。其中，在俄勒冈州内，修建每公里铁路能得到的土地。\[3\]\[4\]两家同样名为[俄勒冈中央铁路的公司对修建铁路有兴趣](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/俄勒冈中央铁路 "wikilink")，它们分别提出在[威拉米特河](../Page/威拉米特河.md "wikilink")两岸修建铁路。两家铁路公司最终合并，形成[俄勒冈和加利福尼亚铁路](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/俄勒冈和加利福尼亚铁路 "wikilink")。\[5\]1869年，国会要求以2.50美元每英亩的价格将土地卖给土地附近的定居者。\[6\]这些土地地图上以棋盘格形状，其中一部分交给铁路公司，另一部分留在联邦政府以供未来开发。\[7\]

[Logging,_1910_from_Centennial_History_of_Oregon.png](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Logging,_1910_from_Centennial_History_of_Oregon.png "fig:Logging,_1910_from_Centennial_History_of_Oregon.png") 1872年，铁路从波特蘭延伸到[罗斯堡 (俄勒冈州)](../Page/罗斯堡_\(俄勒冈州\).md "wikilink")。\[8\]铁路沿线多个镇得到发展，也有新的城镇出现。\[9\]

## 丑闻

铁路建成时沿线还是大面积的原始森林，有很大的采伐价值。\[10\]由于土地凹凸不平，未采伐的土地难以吸引外人定居。\[11\]因此当时有一些人购买小片土地后将土地转让回铁路公司，铁路公司再将大片土地转让给伐木商。\[12\]

铁路公司雇佣了一个名为[Stephen A. Douglas Puter的人](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_A._Douglas_Puter "wikilink")，该人从波特兰召集了大量人以低价购买土地，再用高价卖给伐木商，每英亩土地可以卖40美元。\[13\]1904年，[The Oregonian揭发了该丑闻](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Oregonian "wikilink")，此时出售的土地中75%是违反规定的。\[14\]

1904年到1910年间，多达100人被卷入该丑闻，包括[美国参议院](../Page/美国参议院.md "wikilink")议员[John H. Mitchell](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_H._Mitchell "wikilink")、[美国众议院议员](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/美国众议院 "wikilink")[John N. Williamson和](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_N._Williamson "wikilink")[Binger Hermann](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binger_Hermann "wikilink")、[美国俄勒冈联邦地区法院法官](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/美国俄勒冈联邦地区法院 "wikilink")[John Hicklin Hall](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Hicklin_Hall "wikilink")。\[15\]\[16\]\[17\]

## 土地的收回

[BLM_Oregon_forestry.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:BLM_Oregon_forestry.jpg "fig:BLM_Oregon_forestry.jpg") now manages timber on more than 2 million acres of land in Oregon that formerly belonged to the O\&C Railroad\]\] 最高法院的审判发现[南太平洋运输公司](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/南太平洋运输公司 "wikilink")（1887年收购了[俄勒冈和](../Page/俄勒冈州.md "wikilink")[加利福尼亚铁路](../Page/加利福尼亚州.md "wikilink")）不仅违规出售土地，而是在1903年宣布停止出售土地，这样使公司保留了大面积的土地。\[18\]\[19\]\[20\]

波特兰律师[Walter Lafferty代表](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Lafferty "wikilink")18个县向该公司发起诉讼，要求没收该公司出售土地得到的收益。\[21\][美国最高法院](../Page/美国最高法院.md "wikilink")1915年判决联邦政府赎回公司仍然持有的土地。1916年，[Chamberlain–Ferris Act正式以](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chamberlain–Ferris_Act "wikilink")2.5美元每英亩的价格从公司赎回了持有的2800000英亩土地。涉及的18个县也得到了一定赔偿。\[22\]\[23\]\[24\]\[25\]

由于这些土地都归联邦政府所有，涉及的18个县没有得到更多利益。\[26\]\[27\] As these problems compounded into the 1920s, the 18 counties organized the Association of O\&C Counties (AOCC) to give itself a voice in [华盛顿哥伦比亚特区](../Page/华盛顿哥伦比亚特区.md "wikilink"). One of its cofounders, [道格拉斯縣 (俄勒岡州)](../Page/道格拉斯縣_\(俄勒岡州\).md "wikilink") district attorney and future U.S. Senator [Guy Cordon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guy_Cordon "wikilink"), began lobbying Oregon's congressional delegation for relief.\[28\]\[29\] In 1926, a bill introduced by Oregon Senator [Robert N. Stanfield](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_N._Stanfield "wikilink"), which became known as the Stanfield Act, was passed. This law provided that the U.S. government pay the counties in lieu of property taxes they would have received if the land were privately owned.\[30\]\[31\]\[32\]\[33\]\[34\] But since the U.S. government was to be reimbursed from timber revenues, and since timber revenue remained low, very few payments were actually made to the counties, and Congress began to work on new legislation.\[35\]\[36\]\[37\]

## 俄勒冈和加利福尼亚铁路赠地法案

[Oandc-rac.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Oandc-rac.jpg "fig:Oandc-rac.jpg")\]\] 1937年，国会通过了俄勒冈和加利福尼亚铁路赠地法案()，允许[美国内政部](../Page/美国内政部.md "wikilink")“可持续性”采伐这些土地。\[38\]\[39\] 其中50%的收入分配给各县，另有25%用于偿还曾经给涉及的18个县的赔偿。\[40\]该法案明确提出这些土地需要合理管理，使得采伐能够永久持续。\[41\]

Under the O\&C Act, the Department of the Interior under its [General Land Office](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Land_Office "wikilink") and later succeeded by the [Bureau of Land Management](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bureau_of_Land_Management "wikilink"), managed more than 44 billion board feet of standing inventory in 1937 into more than 60 billion board feet by the mid-1990s, and harvested more than 44 billion board feet over that time period.\[42\] In 1951, the U.S. Treasury had been fully reimbursed, and the 25 percent of the revenue that had previously gone to the Treasury now reverted to the counties; in 1953, the counties opted to divert that money to maintenance of the land and roads, reforestation, as well as recreational facilities and other improvements.\[43\]\[44\]\[45\] A 1970 [GAO](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_Accountability_Office "wikilink") report contained an estimate that from its implementation through 1969, the counties had received a total of $300 million as a result of the Act.\[46\] The authors of the report also estimated that most counties received more from the government payments than they would have if the land had been held privately.\[47\]

The O\&C Act achieved what the previous legislation had failed to do: provide a stable revenue to the counties. This revenue became a vital part of the budgets of the O\&C counties, paying for county-provided services such as law enforcement and corrections and health and social services.\[48\] With this funding seemingly guaranteed, the counties kept other taxes much lower than other counties in the state, increasing their dependence on the timber payments. For example, the property tax in [寇里縣 (俄勒岡州)](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/寇里縣_\(俄勒岡州\) "wikilink") is 60 cents per $1,000 of assessed value, far below the state average of $2.81 per $1000.\[49\]

## Decline in timber revenue and revised Congressional action

In 1989, annual timber harvest revenue on federal forest land nationwide peaked at $1.5 billion.\[50\] Following that year, the impact of overharvesting and increased environmental concerns began to negatively impact timber sales on the O\&C lands.\[51\] In 1994, the federal [Northwest Forest Plan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northwest_Forest_Plan "wikilink") was implemented. Designed to guide forest management of federal lands while protecting [原始森林](../Page/原始森林.md "wikilink") habitat for endangered species such as the [北方斑點鴞](../Page/北方斑點鴞.md "wikilink"), the plan restricted the land available for timber harvest. By 1998, revenue on federal forest lands fell to a third of the peak 1989 revenue, with areas in the Northwest particularly hard-hit.\[52\]\[53\]

To offset the effects of the loss of timber revenue, in 1993, President [比尔·克林顿](../Page/比尔·克林顿.md "wikilink") proposed a 10-year program of payments, set at 85 percent of the average O\&C Act payments from 1986 to 1990, and declining 3 percent annually.\[54\] These "spotted owl" or "safety net" payments were passed by Congress as part of the [Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act of 1993](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omnibus_Budget_Reconciliation_Act_of_1993 "wikilink") ().\[55\]\[56\]

With the payments set to expire in 2003, work began in 1999 to seek an extension to the payments. The O\&C counties joined with other rural counties (including 15 of Oregon's other 18 counties) that also faced falling timber revenues to lobby Congress for another solution.\[57\] In 2000, Congress passed the [Secure Rural Schools and Community Self-Determination Act](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secure_Rural_Schools_and_Community_Self-Determination_Act_of_2000 "wikilink") (), which authorizes western counties, including the O\&C counties, to receive federal payments to compensate for loss of timber revenue until 2006. Payments to O\&C counties, which included O\&C revenue as well as revenue on [美国国家森林局](../Page/美国国家森林局.md "wikilink") land, averaged about $250 million per year from 2000 to 2006.\[58\]\[59\] The act was extended for one year in 2007, and in 2008, a four-year extension was included in the [經濟穩定緊急法案](../Page/經濟穩定緊急法案.md "wikilink") that phased out the program by 2012.\[60\]\[61\] The extension expired on September 30, 2011 and the final payment of just over $40 million was delivered to the O\&C counties in early 2012.\[62\]

In late 2011, Oregon Senators [榮·懷登](../Page/榮·懷登.md "wikilink") and [傑夫·默克利](../Page/傑夫·默克利.md "wikilink") proposed legislation to extend the payments for another five years. The move was backed by Governor [約翰·基茨哈伯](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/約翰·基茨哈伯 "wikilink") and the entire Oregon congressional delegation.\[63\] Republican and Democratic members of Oregon's congressional delegation also proposed setting aside some of the federal land in Oregon as public trusts in which half would be designated for harvest to provide revenue for the counties, and half designated as a conservation area.\[64\] President [贝拉克·奥巴马](../Page/贝拉克·奥巴马.md "wikilink")'s proposed [2013 United States federal budget](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_United_States_federal_budget "wikilink") included $294 million to extend the program for fiscal year 2013 with a plan to continue the payments for four more years, with the amount declining 10% each year.\[65\]\[66\]

In March 2012, the U.S. Senate added an amendment to the surface transportation bill that authorized a one-year extension to the Secure Rural Schools and Community Self-Determination Act. Oregon counties would have received a total of $102 million from the legislation in 2012, to be divided among all 33 counties that currently receive payments. In 2008, Oregon received $250 million from the program.\[67\] The full transportation bill, including the amendment, passed the Senate by a 74–22 vote,\[68\]\[69\] but the U.S. House of Representatives refused to vote on the Senate bill, instead passing a three-month extension to the current transportation bill that did not contain a county payments extension.\[70\] In July 2012, the Secure Rural Schools Act renewal amendment was included in the [transportation bill](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moving_Ahead_for_Progress_in_the_21st_Century_Act "wikilink") approved by Congress and signed by the President.\[71\]\[72\]\[73\] This was widely expected to be the last renewal of the program,\[74\] but in September 2013, Congress passed another one-year extension to the program, though again at reduced levels.\[75\]

## Future of the O\&C counties

With future revenue uncertain, several Oregon counties now face a severe financial crisis to pay for county services, including law enforcement, social services, justice and corrections systems, election services and road maintenance among others.\[76\] With county services required by state law and bankruptcy not permitted, counties have considered merging to save costs, and explored new sources of revenue.\[77\]

One of the hardest-hit counties, [寇里縣 (俄勒岡州)](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/寇里縣_\(俄勒岡州\) "wikilink"), introduced a ballot measure to add a 3% [营业税](../Page/营业税.md "wikilink") to pay for county services.\[78\] Oregon is one of only five states in the United States with no county or state sales tax, and the tax has been voted down regularly by voters whenever it has been proposed (though some areas assess a gas tax, and two cities in tourist areas, [阿什兰 (俄勒冈州)](../Page/阿什兰_\(俄勒冈州\).md "wikilink") and [Yachats](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yachats,_Oregon "wikilink"), assess a local tax on prepared food).\[79\] In [約瑟芬縣](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/約瑟芬縣 "wikilink"), after a proposed property tax increase to pay for law enforcement was defeated in May 2012, the sheriff's office reduced its staff by 2/3 and released inmates from the county jail to reduce spending.\[80\] [雷恩縣 (俄勒岡州)](../Page/雷恩縣_\(俄勒岡州\).md "wikilink") released 96 prisoners from its prisons and laid off 40 law enforcement personnel to cut costs.\[81\]

In 2012, the [俄勒冈州议会](../Page/俄勒冈州议会.md "wikilink") passed a law to allow O\&C counties to use timber funds previously reserved for road maintenance to pay for law enforcement patrols.\[82\]

In late 2013, the House passed a forest management bill co-sponsored by Oregon Representatives [Peter DeFazio](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_DeFazio "wikilink"), [格雷格·瓦爾登](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/格雷格·瓦爾登 "wikilink"), and [庫爾特·史瑞德](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/庫爾特·史瑞德 "wikilink") that would include increased timber harvests on O\&C lands along with resumption of some Secure Rural Schools funding. President Obama has indicated he is likely to veto the bill.\[83\]

**Kota** is a language of the [Dravidian language
family](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dravidian_languages "wikilink")
with about 9000 native speakers in the
[尼尔吉里丘陵](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/尼尔吉里丘陵 "wikilink") of
[泰米尔纳德邦](../Page/泰米尔纳德邦.md "wikilink") state,
[印度](../Page/印度.md "wikilink"). It is spoken mainly by the
tribal . In the late 19th century, the native speaking population was
about 1,100.\[1\] In 1990, the population was only 930, out of an ethnic
population of perhaps 1,400, despite the great increase in the
population of the area.\[2\] The language is 'critically endangered' due
to the greater social status of neighbouring languages.\[3\] The Kota
language may have originated from  and is closely related to . The Kota
population is about 2500. The origin of the name Kota is derived from
the Dravida root word 'Ko' meaning Mountain.\[4\]\[5\]

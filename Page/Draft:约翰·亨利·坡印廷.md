## 生平

约翰·亨利·坡印廷是[一位论派](../Page/一位论派.md "wikilink")牧师托马斯·埃尔福德·坡印廷最小的儿子。1852年，坡印廷出生于英国[兰开夏郡](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/兰开夏郡 "wikilink")[埃克尔斯蒙顿一位论派教堂内的牧师公寓](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/埃克尔斯 "wikilink")，1846至1878年间他的父亲在这所教堂担任牧师。孩提时代，坡印廷就在教堂附近一所由他父亲开办的学校上学。1867年至1872年，他就读于[曼彻斯特维多利亚大学](../Page/曼彻斯特维多利亚大学.md "wikilink")（今[曼彻斯特大学](../Page/曼彻斯特大学.md "wikilink")），物理学师从[Osborne Reynolds](../Page/奥斯鲍恩·雷诺.md "wikilink") and 。1872年至1876年坡印廷在剑桥大学进行数学研究，师从，并获得较高的声誉。1870年代后期，他还在剑桥大学的[卡文迪许实验室](../Page/卡文迪许实验室.md "wikilink")工作，当时的实验室主任是[詹姆斯·克拉克·麦克斯韦](../Page/詹姆斯·克拉克·麦克斯韦.md "wikilink")。\[3\]

Poynting was the youngest son of Thomas Elford Poynting, a [Unitarian](../Page/一位论派.md "wikilink") minister. He was born at the parsonage of the Monton Unitarian Chapel in Eccles, Lancashire (his father serving as minister there from 1846 to 1878.) In his boyhood he was educated at the nearby school operated by his father. From 1867 to 1872 he attended [Owens College](../Page/曼彻斯特维多利亚大学.md "wikilink"), now the [University of Manchester](../Page/曼彻斯特大学.md "wikilink"), where his physics teachers included [Osborne Reynolds](../Page/奥斯鲍恩·雷诺.md "wikilink") and . From 1872 to 1876 he was a student at [Cambridge University](../Page/剑桥大学.md "wikilink"), where he attained high honours in mathematics after taking grinds with . In the late 1870s he worked in the [Cavendish Laboratory](../Page/卡文迪许实验室.md "wikilink") at Cambridge under [James Clerk Maxwell](../Page/詹姆斯·克拉克·麦克斯韦.md "wikilink").\[4\]

He was the developer and [eponym](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/:wikt:eponym "wikilink") of the [Poynting vector](../Page/坡印廷向量.md "wikilink"), which describes the direction and magnitude of [electromagnetic](../Page/电磁学.md "wikilink") energy flow and is used in the [Poynting theorem](../Page/坡印亭定理.md "wikilink"), a statement about energy conservation for [electric](../Page/電場.md "wikilink") and [magnetic](../Page/磁場.md "wikilink") fields. This work was first published in 1884. He performed a measurement of [Newton's](../Page/艾萨克·牛顿.md "wikilink") [gravitational constant](../Page/万有引力常数.md "wikilink") by innovative means during 1893. In 1903 he was the first to realise that the [Sun](../Page/太阳.md "wikilink")'s radiation can draw in small particles towards it:\[5\] this was later named the [Poynting–Robertson effect](../Page/坡印亭-羅伯遜效應.md "wikilink").

He discovered the torsion-extension coupling in . This is now known as the (positive)  in torsion.

Poynting and the Nobel prizewinner [J. J. Thomson](../Page/约瑟夫·汤姆孙.md "wikilink") co-authored a multi-volume undergraduate physics textbook, which was in print for about 50 years and was in widespread use during the first third of the 20th century.\[6\] Poynting wrote most of it.\[7\]

He was awarded an [honorary](../Page/名誉学位.md "wikilink") MSc in Pure Science in 1901 by [Birmingham University](../Page/伯明翰大学.md "wikilink").\[8\]

Poynting lived at 11 St Augustine's Road,  with his family and servants for some years. He previously lived at 66 Beaufort Road,  (demolished) and died of a diabetic coma, aged 61, at 10 Ampton Road, Edgbaston in 1914.

## Legacy

Poynting's most famous student may have been [Alfred J. Lotka](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/阿弗雷德·洛特卡 "wikilink"), who was inspired by Poynting to apply the ideas of physical chemistry to biology. Lotka dedicated his classic book on mathematical population biology to Poynting.

[Craters](../Page/撞击坑.md "wikilink") on [Mars](../Page/火星.md "wikilink") and the [Moon](../Page/坡印廷环形山.md "wikilink") are named in his honour, as is the main Physics building at the [University of Birmingham](../Page/伯明翰大学.md "wikilink") and the departmental society there, the Poynting Physical Society. He is credited with coining the expression "greenhouse effect" in 1909 to explain how human behaviour might increase global temperatures. .\[9\]

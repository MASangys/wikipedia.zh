**芬威體育集團**（，簡稱**FSG**）是一家[美國體育公司](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/美國 "wikilink")，擁有[大聯盟棒球隊](../Page/美國職業棒球大聯盟.md "wikilink")[波士頓紅襪](../Page/波士頓紅襪.md "wikilink")和[英超足球隊](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/英超 "wikilink")[利物浦](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/利物浦足球俱乐部 "wikilink")。

FSG was founded in 2001 as **New England Sports Ventures** (NESV) when [约翰·亨利](../Page/约翰·亨利.md "wikilink") joined forces with [Tom Werner](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Werner "wikilink"), [Les Otten](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Les_Otten "wikilink"), [The New York Times Company](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times_Company "wikilink"), and other investors to successfully bid for the Red Sox. NESV formally announced its name change to Fenway Sports Group in March 2011.\[1\]

In addition to owning the Red Sox and Liverpool F.C., the Boston-based [limited liability company](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/limited_liability_company "wikilink") also owns the home stadiums for both teams ([Fenway Park](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fenway_Park "wikilink") and [Anfield](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anfield "wikilink")) and [Fenway Sports Management](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fenway_Sports_Management "wikilink") (which in turn owns the [Salem Red Sox](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salem_Red_Sox "wikilink") of the Class A [Carolina League](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carolina_League "wikilink"), a [minor league baseball](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/minor_league_baseball "wikilink") franchise), plus 80% of the [New England Sports Network](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England_Sports_Network "wikilink") (NESN) and 50% of [Roush Fenway Racing](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roush_Fenway_Racing "wikilink"), with cars entered (current as of 2018 season) in the [Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monster_Energy_NASCAR_Cup_Series "wikilink") and [NASCAR Xfinity Series](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASCAR_Xfinity_Series "wikilink") racing competitions.

In an April 2014 article in *[Forbes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbes "wikilink")*, senior editor Kurt Badenhausen called FSG "the most sophisticated, synergistic player in the coming age of international sports conglomerates".\[2\]

## History

New England Sports Ventures, LLC, was incorporated in [Delaware](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delaware "wikilink") in 2001. The company registered to do business in Massachusetts in the same year.\[3\]

## Properties

### Boston Red Sox

The Boston Red Sox are a [professional baseball team](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/professional_baseball "wikilink") based in [Boston](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston "wikilink"), [Massachusetts](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts "wikilink"), and a member of the [Major League Baseball](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Baseball "wikilink")’s [American League Eastern Division](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_League_East "wikilink"). Founded in  as one of the American League's eight charter franchises, the Red Sox's home [ballpark](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/baseball_park "wikilink") has been [Fenway Park](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fenway_Park "wikilink") since . The "Red Sox" name was chosen by the team owner, [John I. Taylor](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_I._Taylor "wikilink"), around , following previous Boston teams that had been known as the "[Red Stockings](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlanta_Braves "wikilink")". Boston was a dominant team in the new league, defeating the [Pittsburgh Pirates](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates "wikilink") in the first [World Series](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Series "wikilink") in  and winning four more championships by .

In , the Red Sox were sold by the [Yawkey Trust](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/JRY_Trust "wikilink") to Fenway Sports Group. They had endured one of the longest championship [droughts](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_franchise_postseason_droughts "wikilink") in baseball history, called by some the "[Curse of the Bambino](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curse_of_the_Bambino "wikilink")" after its alleged beginning with the Red Sox's sale of [Babe Ruth](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Babe_Ruth "wikilink") to the rival Yankees in 1918, an 86-year wait before the team's sixth World Championship in . Red Sox history has also been marked by the team's intense [rivalry](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yankees_–_Red_Sox_rivalry "wikilink") with the [New York Yankees](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Yankees "wikilink"), arguably the fiercest and most historic in North American professional sports. Since , the Red Sox have been perennial playoff contenders and have won four World Series, emerging as one of the most successful MLB teams of the last decade.

#### Fenway Park

[Fenway Park](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fenway_Park "wikilink") is a [baseball park](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/baseball_park "wikilink") near [Kenmore Square](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenmore_Square "wikilink") in [Boston](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston "wikilink"), [Massachusetts](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts "wikilink"). Located at 4 Jersey Way, it has served as the home ballpark of the [Boston Red Sox](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox "wikilink") [baseball](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/baseball "wikilink") club since it opened in 1912, and is the oldest [Major League Baseball](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Baseball "wikilink") [stadium](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/stadium "wikilink") currently in use. It is, along with Chicago's [Wrigley Field](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wrigley_Field "wikilink"), one of the pair of the original ["jewel box" standard ballparks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baseball_park#Jewel_box_ballparks "wikilink") that are still in use. Considered to be one of the best-known sports venues in the U.S., Fenway Park became the oldest venue used by a professional sports team in the United States in 1999 when the [Detroit Tigers](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Detroit_Tigers "wikilink") moved out of [Tiger Stadium](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiger_Stadium_\(Detroit\) "wikilink") which opened the same day as Fenway Park.\[4\]

### NESN

The [New England Sports Network](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England_Sports_Network "wikilink") (NESN) is a regional [cable television](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cable_television "wikilink") network that covers the six [New England](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England "wikilink") states except [Fairfield County](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fairfield_County,_Connecticut "wikilink"), Connecticut and [Southbury, Connecticut](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southbury,_Connecticut "wikilink"), a town in [New Haven County](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Haven_County,_Connecticut "wikilink") that is covered by [New York City](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "wikilink") sports networks. FSG is NESN's majority owner (80%) and the [Boston Bruins](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston_Bruins "wikilink") own the remaining 20%. Although it mainly broadcasts non-national Boston Red Sox and Boston Bruins games, NESN also features minor league baseball, regional college sports including college hockey games on Friday nights, various outdoor shows, and sports talk shows featuring the sports columnists of *[The Boston Globe](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Boston_Globe "wikilink")*, as that paper is also owned by John Henry.

### Roush Fenway Racing

Roush Fenway Racing (formerly Roush Racing) is a racing team competing in [NASCAR](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASCAR "wikilink") racing. As one of NASCAR's largest premier racing teams, Roush runs the No. 6 and No. 17 [Ford Mustangs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ford_Mustang "wikilink") in the [Monster Energy Cup Series](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monster_Energy_NASCAR_Cup_Series "wikilink") and previously competed in the [Xfinity Series](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xfinity_Series "wikilink") (1992–2018), [NASCAR Gander Outdoors Truck Series](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASCAR_Gander_Outdoors_Truck_Series "wikilink") (1996–2009) and [ARCA RE/MAX Series](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/ARCA_RE/MAX_Series "wikilink").

Roush first entered NASCAR competition in 1988, but had competed and won championships in various drag racing and sports car racing series since the mid-1960s. The racing business was originally a small branch of co-owner [Jack Roush](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Roush "wikilink")'s successful automotive engineering and road-racing equipment business based in [Livonia, Michigan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Livonia,_Michigan "wikilink").

On February 14, 2007, FSG purchased 50% of Roush Racing to create a new corporate entity, Roush Fenway Racing.\[5\]

### Fenway Sports Management

Fenway Sports Management (FSM) was established by FSG in 2004. It styles itself as a "new kind of sports marketing agency", created by FSG to expand its footprint beyond its most famous holdings, the Boston Red Sox of Major League Baseball and Fenway Park. FSM entered into an exclusive sponsorship sales agreement with [MLB Advanced Media](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/MLB_Advanced_Media "wikilink") and [Boston College's major intercollegiate sports](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston_College_Eagles "wikilink") in 2004\[6\] and as of 2014 counts the Red Sox, Liverpool F.C., [LeBron James](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/LeBron_James "wikilink"),\[7\] [Johnny Manziel](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johnny_Manziel "wikilink"),\[8\] BC, Roush Fenway Racing, MLB.com, the PGA Tour's [Deutsche Bank Championship](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deutsche_Bank_Championship "wikilink"), NESN, the [Pawtucket Red Sox](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pawtucket_Red_Sox "wikilink") and the [Salem Red Sox](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salem_Red_Sox "wikilink") among its client base.

### Liverpool F.C.

Having previously partnered with the English soccer club [Fulham F.C.](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulham_F.C. "wikilink"),\[9\]\[10\] on October 6, 2010, FSG agreed to buy [Liverpool F.C.](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liverpool_F.C. "wikilink")\[11\] from owners [George N. Gillett, Jr.](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_N._Gillett,_Jr. "wikilink") and [Tom Hicks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Hicks "wikilink"), after the board voted 3-2 to oust them from the club.\[12\]\[13\] It purchased through subsidiary, "N.E.S.V. I, LLC" (incorporated in Delaware) and UK based holding company "UKSV Holdings Company Limited".

#### Anfield

[Anfield](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anfield "wikilink") is a soccer stadium in the city of Liverpool, England. Built in 1884 and originally the home of [Everton F.C.](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Everton_F.C. "wikilink"), the stadium has been home to Liverpool F.C. since it was formed in 1892. FSG purchased Anfield along with Liverpool F.C. in October 2010.

### Salem Red Sox

In December 2007, Fenway Sports Group agreed to purchase the [Salem Avalanche](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salem_Avalanche "wikilink") of the [Carolina League](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carolina_League "wikilink"), Single A Advanced affiliated of the [Houston Astros](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Houston_Astros "wikilink") with the anticipation of converting the team to a [Boston](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox "wikilink")-affiliated club. In 2009, the [Roanoke Valley](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roanoke_Valley "wikilink")-based team adopted the Red Sox moniker, including a logo and color scheme to match their parent club. Since Fenway acquired the club, it has had a relative amount of success, claiming one league title, two division titles, and making three playoff appearances.

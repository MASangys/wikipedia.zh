**芬威體育集團**（，簡稱**FSG**）是一家[美國體育公司](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/美國 "wikilink")，擁有[大聯盟棒球隊](../Page/美國職業棒球大聯盟.md "wikilink")[波士頓紅襪](../Page/波士頓紅襪.md "wikilink")和[英超足球隊](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/英超 "wikilink")[利物浦](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/利物浦足球俱乐部 "wikilink")。

FSG was founded in 2001 as **New England Sports Ventures** (NESV) when
[约翰·亨利](../Page/约翰·亨利.md "wikilink") joined forces with [Tom
Werner](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Werner "wikilink"), [Les
Otten](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Les_Otten "wikilink"), [The New
York Times
Company](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times_Company "wikilink"),
and other investors to successfully bid for the Red Sox. NESV formally
announced its name change to Fenway Sports Group in March 2011.\[1\]

In addition to owning the Red Sox and Liverpool F.C., the Boston-based
[limited liability
company](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/limited_liability_company "wikilink")
also owns the home stadiums for both teams ([Fenway
Park](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fenway_Park "wikilink") and
[Anfield](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anfield "wikilink")) and [Fenway
Sports
Management](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fenway_Sports_Management "wikilink")
(which in turn owns the [Salem Red
Sox](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salem_Red_Sox "wikilink") of the
Class A [Carolina
League](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carolina_League "wikilink"), a
[minor league
baseball](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/minor_league_baseball "wikilink")
franchise), plus 80% of the [New England Sports
Network](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England_Sports_Network "wikilink")
(NESN) and 50% of [Roush Fenway
Racing](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roush_Fenway_Racing "wikilink"),
with cars entered (current as of 2018 season) in the [Monster Energy
NASCAR Cup
Series](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monster_Energy_NASCAR_Cup_Series "wikilink")
and [NASCAR Xfinity
Series](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASCAR_Xfinity_Series "wikilink")
racing competitions.

In an April 2014 article in
*[Forbes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbes "wikilink")*, senior
editor Kurt Badenhausen called FSG "the most sophisticated, synergistic
player in the coming age of international sports conglomerates".\[2\]

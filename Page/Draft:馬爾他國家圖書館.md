The **National Library of Malta** (), often known as the **Bibliotheca** (), is a [reference library](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/reference_library "wikilink") in [Republic Square](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_Square,_Valletta "wikilink"), [Valletta](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valletta "wikilink"), Malta. It was founded by Grand Master [Emmanuel de Rohan-Polduc](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmanuel_de_Rohan-Polduc "wikilink") in 1776 out of the collections of the knight Louis Guérin de Tencin. It has been a [legal deposit](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/legal_deposit "wikilink") library since 1925, and it has the largest collection of [Melitensia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melitensia "wikilink") along with that of the [University of Malta](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Malta "wikilink"). The library also contains the archives of the [Order of St. John](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller "wikilink"), the Università of Mdina and the Università of Valletta.

The library is housed in a late 18th-century [neoclassical](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture "wikilink") building in the city centre, close to the [Grandmaster's Palace](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grandmaster's_Palace_\(Valletta\) "wikilink").

## History

The origins of the National Library of Malta go back to 1555, when Grand Master [Claude de la Sengle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Claude_de_la_Sengle "wikilink") decreed that all books belonging to deceased members of the [Order of St. John](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller "wikilink") were to be passed to the Order's treasury.\[1\]

In 1760, Louis Guérin de Tencin, the Bailiff Grand Cross of the Order, purchased a collection of 9700 books which had belonged to [Joaquín Fernández de Portocarrero](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joaquín_Fernández_de_Portocarrero "wikilink") for 7000 [scudi](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maltese_scudo "wikilink"). A year later, he opened a public library in a building known as *Il Forfantone* in [Valletta](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valletta "wikilink"), containing books from his own library as well as Portocarrero's collection, the library of Comm. Sainte-Jay (which was previously kept at the sacristy of the [Conventual Church of St. John](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._John's_Co-Cathedral "wikilink")), and books donated by members of the Order. De Tencin appointed [Giovanni Pietro Francesco Agius de Soldanis](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giovanni_Pietro_Francesco_Agius_de_Soldanis "wikilink") as librarian, and paid his salary himself. De Tencin died in 1766 before managing to secure funds for maintaining the library.\[2\]

In 1776, Grand Master [Emmanuel de Rohan-Polduc](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmanuel_de_Rohan-Polduc "wikilink") formally founded the ***Bibliotheca Publica***, and it was also called the ***Bibliotheca Tanseana*** in De Tencin's honour. The *Forfantone* was considered too cramped to house the new library, so the Polish-born Italian architect [Stefano Ittar](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefano_Ittar "wikilink") was commissioned to design a new library building in 1786. The building was built on the site of the *Conservatoria*, where gold and silver [bullion](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/bullion "wikilink") of the Order's treasury were stored.\[3\] It was completed in 1796, but it remained empty for some years due to the [French invasion of 1798](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_campaign_of_1798#Malta "wikilink"). It was eventually inaugurated on 4 June 1812 by [Civil Commissioner](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Civil_Commissioners_of_Malta "wikilink") Sir [Hildebrand Oakes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hildebrand_Oakes "wikilink"), and it became known as the **Malta Public Library**.\[4\]

It became a [legal deposit](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/legal_deposit "wikilink") library by Act no. II of 1925, and in 1936 it was granted the title of **Royal Malta Library** by King [George V](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_V "wikilink"). In 1937, the archives of the Order were transferred to the library from the [Public Registry](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banca_Giuratale_\(Valletta\) "wikilink").\[5\]

In 1976, the [Central Public Library](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Public_Library_\(Malta\) "wikilink") was opened in [Floriana](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Floriana "wikilink"), and the Valletta library became a [research](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/research_library "wikilink") and [reference library](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/reference_library "wikilink") known as the **National Library of Malta**.\[6\]

## Architecture

The library building was designed by the Polish-Italian architect [Stefano Ittar](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefano_Ittar "wikilink"), and it is an early example of [neoclassical architecture](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/neoclassical_architecture "wikilink") in Malta. It has a symmetrical façade with [Doric](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doric_order "wikilink") and [Ionic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionic_order "wikilink") columns. The first floor is supported on a [loggia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/loggia "wikilink"), with the main doorway in the centre. A balustraded balcony is located above the doorway, and it is supported by Doric and Ionic columns.\[7\] The building is also well known for its neo-classic monumental staircase that spreads from the main entrance to the upper floors.\[8\]

Evidence shows that while Stefano was the main architect, his son Sebastiano Ittar continued the design after his father died before finalizing the work.\[9\]

The library building was included on the Antiquities List of 1925.\[10\] It is now scheduled as a Grade 1 national monument, and it is also listed on the [National Inventory of the Cultural Property of the Maltese Islands](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Inventory_of_the_Cultural_Property_of_the_Maltese_Islands "wikilink").\[11\]

## Collections

[Pie_Postulatio_Voluntatis.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pie_Postulatio_Voluntatis.jpg "fig:Pie_Postulatio_Voluntatis.jpg")

The National Library of Malta's collections include:

  - *[Pie Postulatio Voluntatis](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pie_Postulatio_Voluntatis "wikilink")*, a [papal bull](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/papal_bull "wikilink") issued by [Pope Paschal II](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope_Paschal_II "wikilink") in 1113 confirming the establishment of the Order of St. John\[12\]
  - the complete archives of the Order of St. John from the Middle Ages to 1798\[13\]
  - the archives of the Treasury of the Order\[14\]
  - the archives of the Università of [Mdina](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mdina "wikilink") and that of Valletta from 1450 to 1818\[15\]
  - 1617 codices and manuscripts\[16\]
  - 60 [incunabula](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incunable "wikilink"), including [Quintilian](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quintilian "wikilink")'s *[Institutiones Oratoriæ](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institutio_Oratoria "wikilink")* (1476), 's *Obsidionis Rhodiae urbis descriptio* (1480) and *Rhodiorum Historia* (1496), [Ptolemy](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemy "wikilink")'s *[Cosmographia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_\(Ptolemy\) "wikilink")* (1490), the *Privilegia Ordinis Sancti Joannis Hierosolymitani a Summis Pont. concessa* (1495) and Plautus' *Comœdiæ* (1499)\[17\]
  - a large collection of [Melitensia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melitensia "wikilink") (books, pamphlets, newspapers etc. by Maltese authors or related to Malta), including some of the earliest newspapers published in Malta during the [French occupation](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_occupation_of_Malta "wikilink")\[18\]
  - maps dating from the 16th to the 20th centuries, including maps of local roads, [fortifications](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/fortifications_of_Malta "wikilink") and archaeological sites\[19\]
  - a collection of fine bindings, including volumes made for King [Louis XV of France](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_XV_of_France "wikilink") and later donated to de Tencin\[20\]

**Research Unix**，是一系列的[Unix](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unix "wikilink")[作業系統的通稱](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/作業系統 "wikilink")，由[貝爾實驗室的計算機科學研究中心](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/貝爾實驗室 "wikilink")（，部門編號1127）研發，因此得名，共有10個版本。這些Unix系統運行在[DEC](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/DEC "wikilink") [PDP-7](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/PDP-7 "wikilink")，[PDP-11](../Page/PDP-11.md "wikilink")，[VAX](../Page/VAX.md "wikilink")，以及Interdata 7/32與8/32電腦上。

## 版本

| Manual Edition                                                         | Release date  | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1st Edition                                                            | Nov. 3, 1971  | First edition of the Unix manual, based on the version that ran on the PDP-11 at the time. Includes the [Thompson shell](../Page/Thompson_shell.md "wikilink"), [`mail`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/mail_\(Unix\) "wikilink"), [`cp`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cp_\(Unix\) "wikilink"), and [`su`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/su_\(Unix\) "wikilink"). The operating system was two years old,\[1\] having been ported from the PDP-7 to the PDP-11/20 in 1970.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| 2nd Edition                                                            | Jun. 12, 1972 | Total number of installations at the time was 10, "with more expected", according to the preface of the manual.\[2\] Includes [`echo`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/echo_\(command\) "wikilink") and the first [C compiler](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/C_compiler "wikilink").                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| 3rd Edition                                                            | Feb. 1973     | Introduced the [C programming language](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/C_programming_language "wikilink"), [pipes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unix_pipe "wikilink"), [`crypt`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/crypt_\(Unix\) "wikilink"), and [`yacc`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/yacc "wikilink"). Commands are split between `/bin` and `/usr/bin`, requiring a [search path](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/search_path "wikilink")\[3\] (`/usr` was the mountpoint for a second hard disk). Total number of installations was 16.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| 4th Edition                                                            | Nov. 1973     | First version written in C. Also introduced [groups](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_identifier_\(Unix\) "wikilink"), [`grep`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/grep "wikilink"), and [`printf`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/printf "wikilink").\[4\] Number of installations was listed as "above 20". The manual was formatted with [troff](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/troff "wikilink") for the first time. Version described in Thompson and Ritchie's [CACM](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communications_of_the_ACM "wikilink") paper,\[5\] the first public exposition of the operating system.\[6\]                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| 5th Edition                                                            | Jun. 1974     | Widely licensed to educational institutions. Introduced [`find`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/find_\(Unix\) "wikilink"), [`dd`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/dd_\(Unix\) "wikilink"), and the [sticky bit](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/sticky_bit "wikilink"). Targeted the PDP-11/40 and other 11 models with 18 bit addresses. Installations "above 50".                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| [6th Edition](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Version_6_Unix "wikilink") | May 1975      | Includes [`ratfor`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/ratfor "wikilink") and [`bc`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/bc_\(Unix\) "wikilink"). First version to be also licensed to commercial users,\[7\] and to be ported to non-PDP hardware. May 1977 saw the release of MINI-UNIX, a "cut down" v6 for the low-end PDP-11/10.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| [7th Edition](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Version_7_Unix "wikilink") | Jan. 1979     | Includes the [Bourne shell](../Page/Bourne_shell.md "wikilink"), [`cpio`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cpio "wikilink"), [`sed`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/sed "wikilink"), [`ioctl`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/ioctl "wikilink"), [`awk`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/awk "wikilink"), [`f77`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/f77 "wikilink"), [`spell`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/spell_\(Unix\) "wikilink"), and [`stdio`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/stdio "wikilink"). The ancestor of all modern UNIX systems and the last release of Research Unix to see widespread external distributions. Merged most of the utilities of [PWB/UNIX](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/PWB/UNIX "wikilink") with an extensively modified kernel with almost 80% more lines of code than V6. In February, a port called [32V](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNIX/32V "wikilink") was made to DEC's [VAX](../Page/VAX.md "wikilink") hardware; 32V was the basis for [4BSD](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkeley_Software_Distribution "wikilink"). |
| 8th Edition                                                            | Feb. 1985     | A modified 4.1cBSD for the [VAX](../Page/VAX.md "wikilink"), with a [System V](../Page/UNIX_System_V.md "wikilink") shell and [sockets](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkeley_sockets "wikilink") replaced by [Streams](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/STREAMS "wikilink"). Used internally, and only licensed for educational use.\[8\] The [Blit](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blit_\(computer_terminal\) "wikilink") graphics terminal became the primary user interface.\[9\] Added a network filesystem that allowed accessing remote computers' files as `/n/`*`hostname`*`/`*`path`*, and a regular expression library that introduced an API later mimicked by [Henry Spencer](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Spencer "wikilink")'s reimplementation.\[10\] First version with no assembly in the documentation.                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| 9th Edition                                                            | Sep. 1986     | Incorporated code from 4.3BSD; used internally. Featured a generalized version of the [Streams](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/STREAMS "wikilink") [IPC](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interprocess_communication "wikilink") mechanism introduced in V8. The mount system call was extended to connect a stream to a file, the other end of which could be connected to a (user-level) program. This mechanism was used to implement network connection code in userspace.\[11\] Other innovations include [`make`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/make_\(Unix\) "wikilink") and [`Sam`](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_\(text_editor\) "wikilink").\[12\] According to Dennis Ritchie, V9 and V10 were "conceptual": manuals existed, but no OS distributions "in complete and coherent form".\[13\]                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| 10th Edition                                                           | Oct. 1989     | Last Research Unix. Although the manual was published outside of AT\&T by Saunders College Publishing,\[14\] there was no full distribution of the system itself.\[15\] Novelties included graphics [typesetting](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/typesetting "wikilink") tools designed to work with [troff](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/troff "wikilink"), a [C](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/C语言 "wikilink") [interpreter](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interpreter_\(computing\) "wikilink"), animation programs, and several tools later found in Plan 9: the [Mk](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/mk_\(software\) "wikilink") build tool and the [rc shell](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/rc_shell "wikilink"). V10 was also the basis for [Doug McIlroy](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doug_McIlroy "wikilink") and James A. Reeds' [multilevel-secure](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/multilevel_security "wikilink") operating system IX.\[16\]                                                                                                    |

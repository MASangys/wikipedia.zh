以下是**世界海拔高度排名前100位的山峰**，全部位於亞洲，且多數是國土位於青藏高原的四個國家：中國、印度、尼泊爾、巴基斯坦境內的山脈，只列出在客觀標準下不屬於側峰的独立山峰。

## 考慮因素

如何区分擁有多個山峰的高山與单独的高山一直是个难题。一種較常見而且直观的判断方法是利用最高鞍狀山脊與毗鄰較高山峰的高度差距，这一方法被稱為[地形突起](../Page/地形突起度.md "wikilink")（）或re-ascent（較高的山峰稱為“母峰”）。高山的通用定義是突起地形超過300米（或1,000英尺）的山峰。或者，相對突出（突出／高）被使用（通常7-8%）于反映出在更高山脈裡，一切都具有更大規模。下面的列表列出突出至少达500米（相对突出接近7%）的100个最高山峰。基於突出的列表的一個缺點是，它可能排除通过一道高的山脊与更高峰連接的著名或壯觀的山峰，像[艾格峰或](../Page/艾格峰.md "wikilink")[努子峰](../Page/努子峰.md "wikilink")。一些這类有幾乎足夠的突出的山峰和山被包括在列表中，但是未作编号。

所列出山峰高度不太可能都精确到米的程度；的确，在测量远离海岸线的山峰高度时，[海平面高度就是个问题](../Page/海平面.md "wikilink")。不同来源的测量数据之间经常相差数米，本列表中数据可能与维基百科其它地方的数据会有所差别。一个比较极端的例子是，位于[青藏高原北部的](../Page/青藏高原.md "wikilink")[木孜塔格山](../Page/木孜塔格山.md "wikilink")（）通常高度被列為7,723-7,754米，但其高度似乎可能只有6,973-6,987米。很多[喀喇崑崙山山峰在不同的地圖上的相差可能超過](../Page/喀喇崑崙山.md "wikilink")100米，而甚至目前已經被非常徹底度量過的[珠穆朗瑪峰](../Page/珠穆朗瑪峰.md "wikilink")，數值也介於8,844至8,850米之間。這些差異旨在強調所列高度的不確定性。雖然世界上有些地方（尤其是大部分高山地區）沒有被完全勘測，但因人造衛星能量度難以進入的地方的高度，所以忽略這些高山的機會甚微。不過高度和突出地形仍有可能被修改，在未來不排除改變排名或加入其它山峰的可能。因此表中包含所有高於7,200公尺的高峰。

## 地理分佈

列表中大部分山峰位於[印度](../Page/印度.md "wikilink")、[中國](../Page/中华人民共和国.md "wikilink")、[巴基斯坦和](../Page/巴基斯坦.md "wikilink")[尼泊爾交界的](../Page/尼泊爾.md "wikilink")[喜馬拉雅和](../Page/喜馬拉雅.md "wikilink")[喀喇昆仑山脈](../Page/喀喇昆仑山脉.md "wikilink")。实际上，所有高於7000公尺的世界高峰均位於亞洲內一個地帶。这一地带的西方是位於[阿富汗和](../Page/阿富汗.md "wikilink")[巴基斯坦邊界的](../Page/巴基斯坦.md "wikilink")[诺沙克峰](../Page/诺沙克峰.md "wikilink")〔Noshaq，7,492米〕，北面是位於[吉爾吉斯和](../Page/吉爾吉斯.md "wikilink")[中國邊界的](../Page/中华人民共和国.md "wikilink")[托木尔峰](../Page/托木尔峰.md "wikilink")（7,439米），東面是位於[四川的](../Page/四川.md "wikilink")[贡嘎山](../Page/贡嘎山.md "wikilink")〔7,556米〕，以及南面位於[印度和](../Page/印度.md "wikilink")[尼泊爾邊界的](../Page/尼泊爾.md "wikilink")[卡布魯峰](../Page/卡布魯峰.md "wikilink")（7,412米）。

下面為標示山峰位置的衛星圖片，數字代表山峰的排名。為保持圖片清楚，高度較小而與較高山峰重疊的山峰在圖片中省略，方格內的地區山峰密度最高，另外兩幅已放大的圖片顯示方格內的所有山峰。

## 注釋

1.  For Nepal, the heights indicated on the Nepal Topographic Maps are
    followed. For China and the [Baltoro](../Page/Baltoro.md "wikilink")
    Karakoram, the heights are those of "The Maps of Snow Mountains in
    China". For the [Hispar
    Karakoram](../Page/Hispar_Muztagh.md "wikilink") the heights on a
    Russian 1:100,000 topo map
    [1](https://web.archive.org/web/20080427142155/http://echidna.rutgers.edu/expeditions/Hispar/Default.htm)
    seem to be more accurate than the customarily quoted heights
    probably based on US army maps from the 50s
    [2](https://web.archive.org/web/20130725020735/http://www.sol.co.uk/v/viewfinder/elevmisquotes.html#hispar#hispar).
    Elsewhere, unless otherwise indicated, heights are those in Jill
    Neate's "High Asia".

2.  This field is complicated by border line disputes. Mountains
    indicated to be on the Pakistan-Sinkiang border are all in the
    [Kashmir](../Page/Kashmir.md "wikilink") region. Those indicated by
    "Kashmir" only are in the [Siachen
    glacier](../Page/Siachen_glacier.md "wikilink") area claimed by both
    Pakistan and India. The Bhutan / Tibet border is also disputed.

3.  The region is beautifully covered by Terraserver.com and Google Maps
    Satellite imagery. Coordinates were established by comparing
    topographical maps with these satellite images.

4.  The [prominence](../Page/prominence.md "wikilink") data were
    extracted from a combination of maps and computer aided analysis of
    [NASA](../Page/NASA.md "wikilink")'s 3"
    [SRTM](../Page/SRTM.md "wikilink") data. Prominences over 1,450 m
    were copied from [this
    website](http://www.peaklist.org/ultras.html)。

5.  Here defined as the first higher mountain beyond the key saddle with
    500 m prominence itself.

6.  The number of ascents and failed attempts up to 2004 is extracted
    from the [Alpine Club Himalayan
    index](http://www.alpine-club.org.uk/hi/). These are the number of
    expeditions（not individuals）that announced their ascent or attempt
    in a journal. They are probably quite accurate for the rarely
    climbed peaks（though omissions were noted）,but greatly underestimate
    the number of ascending parties on the easier and/or more popular
    mountains, like most
    [eight-thousanders](../Page/eight-thousander.md "wikilink"). For
    instance, Mt Everest has been scaled 2,251 times by individuals up
    to 2004
    [3](http://www.everesthistory.com/everestsummits/summitsbyyear.htm).

7.  Given the large differences between multiple "final" measurements of
    [Mt Everest](../Page/Mt_Everest.md "wikilink")，the traditional 8,848
    m is listed.

8.  The highest（Eastern）summit of [Saser Kangri
    II](../Page/Saser_Kangri_II.md "wikilink") has not yet been climbed
    or attempted. The lower West peak, 2.5 km away, has been climbed in
    1984 and twice since.

9.  The highest point of the [Kabru](../Page/Kabru.md "wikilink")
    massif（the North summit）has not been reached. Lower summits, 2-3
    km to the South, have been climbed, the first time already in 1935.

10. [Chomolhari](../Page/Chomolhari.md "wikilink") may be considerably
    lower than the official 7,315 m, which corresponds to exactly 24,000
    ft
    [4](https://web.archive.org/web/20130725020735/http://www.sol.co.uk/v/viewfinder/elevmisquotes.html#chomolhari#chomolhari)

11. "Discovered" by Jonathan de Ferranti. The height is unknown, but
    over 7,200 meters on both Chinese and Russian maps of the area.

12. The name and information about this summit was extracted from the
    May 2003 edition of Japanese Alpine News.

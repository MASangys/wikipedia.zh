**乳糜管**是可以吸收[小肠](../Page/小肠.md "wikilink")[肠绒毛](../Page/肠绒毛.md "wikilink")中饮食[脂肪](../Page/脂肪.md "wikilink")的。

甘油三酯通过[脂肪酶](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/脂肪酶 "wikilink")、[胆汁和](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/胆汁 "wikilink")[水解](../Page/水解.md "wikilink")乳化，产生[脂肪酸](../Page/脂肪酸.md "wikilink")、甘油二酯和的混合物\[1\]。These then pass from the intestinal lumen into the [enterocyte](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/enterocyte "wikilink"), where they are re-esterified to form [triglyceride](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/triglyceride "wikilink"). The triglyceride is then combined with [phospholipids](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/phospholipids "wikilink"), [cholesterol](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cholesterol "wikilink") [ester](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/ester "wikilink"), and [apolipoprotein B48](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apolipoprotein_B "wikilink") to form [chylomicrons](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/chylomicrons "wikilink"). These chylomicrons then pass into the lacteals, forming a milky substance known as [chyle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/chyle "wikilink"). The lacteals merge to form larger lymphatic vessels that transport the chyle to the [thoracic duct](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/thoracic_duct "wikilink") where it is emptied into the bloodstream at the [subclavian vein](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/subclavian_vein "wikilink").\[2\]\[3\]

At this point, the fats are in the bloodstream in the form of chylomicrons. Once in the blood, chylomicrons are subject to delipidation by [lipoprotein lipase](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/lipoprotein_lipase "wikilink"). Eventually, enough lipid has been lost and additional apolipoproteins gained, that the resulting particle (now referred to as a chylomicron remnant) can be taken up by the [liver](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/liver "wikilink"). From the liver, the fat released from chylomicron remnants can be re-exported to the blood as the triglyceride component of [very low-density lipoproteins](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/very_low-density_lipoprotein "wikilink"). Very low-density lipoproteins are also subject to delipidation by vascular lipoprotein lipase, and deliver fats to tissues throughout the body. In particular, the released fatty acids can be stored in [adipose](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/adipose "wikilink") cells as triglycerides. As triglycerides are lost from very low-density lipoproteins, the lipoprotein particles become smaller and denser (since protein is denser than lipid) and ultimately become [low-density lipoproteins](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/low-density_lipoprotein "wikilink"). A great deal has been written about low-density lipoproteins because they are thought to be [atherogenic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atherogenesis "wikilink").

Note that in contrast to any other route of absorption from the small intestine, the lymphatic system avoids [first pass metabolism](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/first_pass_metabolism "wikilink").

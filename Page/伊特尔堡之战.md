**伊特尔堡之战**（）是[第二次世界大战末期于](../Page/第二次世界大战.md "wikilink")[奥地利](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/奥地利 "wikilink")[提洛邦小镇](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/提洛邦 "wikilink")[伊特尔爆发的一场战斗](../Page/伊特尔_\(奥地利\).md "wikilink")。1945年5月4日，被关押于伊特尔堡的法国[囚犯为抵御附近的](../Page/战俘.md "wikilink")[武装党卫军](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/武装党卫军 "wikilink")，派人向[美军求助](../Page/美国陆军.md "wikilink")。赶来支援的不仅有杰克·李[上尉率领的美军第](../Page/上尉.md "wikilink")23装甲营先锋队，甚至还有[少校指挥的](../Page/少校.md "wikilink")[德国国防军士兵](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/德国国防军 "wikilink")。30人及4輛坦克的他们面对约150-200人及一門Flak
41 88mm和2們2cm
Flak的武装党卫军部队，在伊特尔堡内顽强抵抗了一昼夜，直到5月5日下午美军大部队赶到，成功解除了武装党卫军的包围，战斗以盟军的胜利告终。伊特尔堡之战是二战期间美军和德军唯一一次并肩战斗，同时法国囚犯下至[网球明星](../Page/网球.md "wikilink")，上至前[总理全部拿起武器为自由而战](../Page/总理.md "wikilink")，因此许多报道称之为“二战最奇怪的战斗”。

## 背景

现存的伊特尔堡始建于1878年，由女钢琴家在1884年购得，作为个人住宅。她的老师[李斯特·费伦茨](../Page/李斯特·费伦茨.md "wikilink")、[阿图尔·鲁宾斯坦和](../Page/阿图尔·鲁宾斯坦.md "wikilink")[彼得·伊里奇·柴可夫斯基等著名音乐家都曾在此生活过](../Page/彼得·伊里奇·柴可夫斯基.md "wikilink")。1902年，门特将伊特尔堡出售给柏林一名外科医生兼企业家尤金·迈尔，后者将其打造成一座现代化宾馆，在前来奥地利旅游的游客中口碑甚佳。1925年，[蒂罗尔州副州长](../Page/蒂罗尔州.md "wikilink")[弗朗茨·格鲁纳从迈尔手中买下这座城堡](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/弗朗茨·格鲁纳 "wikilink")，成为他艺术收藏的展览馆，吸引许多社会名流拜访。

1938年，[纳粹德国吞并奥地利](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/德奥合并 "wikilink")，在奥地利境内大肆扩张[盖世太保和](../Page/盖世太保.md "wikilink")[集中营设施](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/纳粹集中营 "wikilink")。伊特尔堡偏僻的地理位置和坚固的建筑结构引起了[纳粹党的注意](../Page/纳粹党.md "wikilink")。1940年初，德国政府从格鲁纳处租借了伊特尔堡的使用权，用作[德国禁烟联盟奥地利支部办公地点](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/納粹德國禁煙運動 "wikilink")。1942年11月，[海因里希·希姆莱从中介入将伊特尔堡充公](../Page/海因里希·希姆莱.md "wikilink")，并开始改造为一座关押高级囚犯的[監獄](../Page/監獄.md "wikilink")。原本的客房变成了带有栅栏和铁锁的囚室，豪华的主卧室则由集中营司令夫妇享有。1943年4月底，伊特堡改造完成，划归[达豪集中营系统管辖](../Page/达豪集中营.md "wikilink")，由一个营的[党卫军骷髅总队看守](../Page/骷髅总队.md "wikilink")，司令为前[迈丹尼克集中营司令](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/迈丹尼克集中营 "wikilink")。5月2日起，囚犯陆续从[布痕瓦尔德集中营等地押送至伊特尔堡](../Page/布痕瓦尔德集中营.md "wikilink")。关押在这里的都是[法兰西第三共和国的政要人士或社会名流](../Page/法兰西第三共和国.md "wikilink")，包括前总理[爱德华·达拉第和](../Page/爱德华·达拉第.md "wikilink")[保罗·雷诺](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/保罗·雷诺 "wikilink")、将军[毛利斯·甘莫林和](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/毛利斯·甘莫林 "wikilink")[馬克西姆·魏剛](../Page/馬克西姆·魏剛.md "wikilink")、外交官[安德烈·弗朗索瓦-龐賽](../Page/安德烈·弗朗索瓦-龐賽.md "wikilink")、工会领袖[莱昂·儒奥](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/莱昂·儒奥 "wikilink")、[夏尔·戴高乐将军的姐姐](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/夏尔·戴高乐 "wikilink")、网球明星等。[希特勒将这些囚犯集中于](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/阿道夫·希特勒 "wikilink")[德国境内的伊特尔堡](../Page/大德意志.md "wikilink")，准备在盟军进攻德国本土时作为[人质](../Page/人质.md "wikilink")。此外伊特尔堡还有一些东欧囚犯，用作维修保养的工人。

## 戰鬥經過

[Schloss_Itter_and_entrance_pathway_in_1979.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Schloss_Itter_and_entrance_pathway_in_1979.jpg "fig:Schloss_Itter_and_entrance_pathway_in_1979.jpg")

[Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10990,_Jean_Borotra.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10990,_Jean_Borotra.jpg "fig:Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10990,_Jean_Borotra.jpg")

On 3 May 1945, Zvonimir Čučković, an imprisoned
[南斯拉夫人民解放軍和游擊隊](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/南斯拉夫人民解放軍和游擊隊 "wikilink")
member from Croatia who worked as a handyman at the prison, left the
castle on the pretense of performing an errand for the prison's
commander . Čučković carried with him a letter in English seeking Allied
assistance he was to give to the first American he encountered.

The town of [沃格爾](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/沃格爾 "wikilink") lay
down the mountains but was still occupied by German troops. Čučković
instead pressed on up the Inn River valley towards
[因斯布魯克](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/因斯布魯克 "wikilink")
distant. Late that evening, he reached the outskirts of the city and
encountered an advance party of the 409th Infantry Regiment of the
American [103rd Infantry
Division](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/103rd_Infantry_Division_\(United_States\) "wikilink")
of the US [VI
Corps](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/VI_Corps_\(United_States\) "wikilink")
and informed them of the castle's prisoners. They were unable to
authorize a rescue on their own but promised Čučković an answer from
their headquarters unit by morning of 4 May.

At dawn, a heavily armored rescue was mounted but was stopped by heavy
shelling just past [延巴赫](../Page/延巴赫.md "wikilink") around halfway to
Itter, then recalled by superiors for encroaching into territory of the
U.S. [36th
Division](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/36th_Infantry_Division_\(United_States\) "wikilink")
to the east. Only two jeeps of auxiliary personnel continued.

Upon Čučković's failure to return, and the death at the prison of the
former commander of Dachau [Eduard
Weiter](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eduard_Weiter "wikilink")  under
suspicious circumstances on 2 May, Wimmer feared for his own life and
abandoned his post. The
*[骷髏總隊](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/骷髏總隊 "wikilink")*
guards departed the castle soon after, with the prisoners taking control
of the castle and arming themselves with the weaponry that remained.

Failing to learn of the result of Čučković's effort, prison leaders
accepted the offer of its Czech cook, Andreas Krobot, to bicycle to
Wörgl mid-day on 4 May in hopes of reaching help there. Armed with a
similar note, he succeeded in contacting [Austrian
resistance](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austrian_resistance "wikilink")
in that town, which had recently been abandoned by
*[德意志國防軍](../Page/德意志國防軍.md "wikilink")* forces but
reoccupied by roaming
*[武裝親衛隊](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/武裝親衛隊 "wikilink")*
troops. He was taken to Major Josef Gangl, commander of the remains of a
unit of *Wehrmacht* soldiers who had defied an order to retreat and
instead thrown in with the local resistance, being made its head.

Gangl sought to maintain his unit's position in the town to protect
local residents from SS reprisals. Nazi loyalists would shoot at any
window displaying either a white- or Austrian flag, and would
[草率處決](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/草率處決 "wikilink") males as
possible deserters. Gangl's hopes were pinned on the Americans reaching
Wörgl promptly and surrendering to them. Instead, he would now have to
approach them under a white flag to ask for their help.

Around the same time, a reconnaissance unit of four
[雪曼坦克](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/M4雪曼戰車 "wikilink") of the
23rd Tank Battalion, [12th Armored
Division](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/12th_Armored_Division "wikilink")
of the US [美國陸軍第21軍](../Page/美國陸軍第21軍.md "wikilink"), under the command
of Captain Lee, had reached
[庫夫施泰因](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/庫夫施泰因 "wikilink"),
Austria,  to the north. There, in the town square, it idled while
waiting for the 12th to be relieved by the [36th Infantry
Division](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/36th_Infantry_Division_\(United_States\) "wikilink").
Asked to provide relief by Gangl, Lee did not hesitate, volunteering to
lead the rescue mission and immediately earning permission from his HQ.

After a personal reconnaissance of the Castle with Gangl in the major's
[82式水桶車](../Page/82式水桶車.md "wikilink"), Lee left two of his tanks behind
but requisitioned five more and supporting infantry from the recently
arrived [142nd Infantry
Regiment](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/142nd_Infantry_Regiment_\(United_States\) "wikilink")
of the 36th. En route, Lee was forced to send the reinforcements back
when a bridge proved too tenuous for the entire column to cross once,
let alone twice. Leaving one of his tanks behind to guard it, he set
back off accompanied only by 14 American soldiers, Gangl, and a driver,
and a truck carrying ten former German artillerymen.  from the castle,
they defeated a party of SS troops that had been attempting to set up a
roadblock.

In the meantime, the French prisoners had requested an SS officer,
Kurt-Siegfried Schrader, whom they had befriended in Itter during his
convalescence from wounds, to take charge of their defense. Upon Lee's
arrival at the castle, prisoners greeted the rescuing force warmly but
were disappointed at its small size. Lee placed the men under his
command in defensive positions around the castle and positioned his
tank, *Besotten Jenny*, at the main entrance.

Lee had ordered the French prisoners to hide, but they remained outside
and fought alongside the American and *Wehrmacht* soldiers. Throughout
the night, the defenders were harried by a reconnaissance force sent to
assess their strength and probe the fortress for weaknesses. On the
morning of 5 May, a force of 100–150 *Waffen-SS* launched their attack.
Before the main assault began, Gangl was able to phone Alois Mayr, the
Austrian resistance leader in Wörgl, and request reinforcements. Only
two more German soldiers under his command and a teenage Austrian
resistance member, Hans Waltl, could be spared, and they quickly drove
to the castle. The Sherman tank provided machine-gun fire support until
it was destroyed by German fire from an
[88毫-{米}-高射砲](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/88毫米高射砲 "wikilink");
it was occupied at the time only by a radioman seeking to repair the
tank's faulty radio, who escaped without injury.\[1\]

Meanwhile, by early afternoon, word had finally reached the 142nd of the
desperation of the defenders' plight, and a relief force was
dispatched.\[2\] Aware he had been unable to give the 142nd complete
information on the enemy and its disposition before communications had
been severed, Lee accepted tennis star Borotra's offer to vault the
castle wall and run the gauntlet of SS strongpoints and ambushes to
deliver it.\[3\] He succeeded, requested a uniform, then joined the
force as it made haste to reach the prison before its defenders fired
their last rounds of ammunition.

The relief force arrived around 16:00, and the SS were promptly
defeated. Some 100 SS prisoners were reportedly taken.\[4\] The French
prisoners were evacuated towards France that evening,\[5\] reaching
Paris on 10 May.\[6\]

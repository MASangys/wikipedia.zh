**克莱沃**\[1\]（，1935年7月20日前的官方写法：Cleve\[2\]；；克莱沃兰语：Kleef；；；；）是[德国](../Page/德国.md "wikilink")西北部下莱茵河地区（Niederrhein）的一个[市镇和城市](../Page/市镇_\(德国\).md "wikilink")，靠近[荷兰](../Page/荷兰.md "wikilink")边境和[莱茵河](../Page/莱茵河.md "wikilink")。

从11世纪开始，克莱沃成为同名伯国的首府，其后来成为公国。今天，克莱沃是德国[北莱茵-威斯特法伦](../Page/北莱茵-威斯特法伦.md "wikilink")州[克萊沃县的首府](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/克莱沃县_\(北莱茵-威斯特法伦\) "wikilink")。这座城市是莱茵-瓦尔应用技术大学（Hochschule Rhein-Waal）的校区所在地之一。

## 市镇

The territory of Kleve comprises, next to the innercity, of fourteen villages and populated places:

[Bimmen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bimmen "wikilink"), [Brienen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brienen "wikilink"), [Donsbrüggen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donsbrüggen "wikilink"), Düffelward, [Griethausen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Griethausen "wikilink"), Keeken, [Kellen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kellen "wikilink"), Materborn, Reichswalde, Rindern, Salmorth, [Schenkenschanz](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schenkenschanz "wikilink"), Warbeyen and Wardhausen.

## 歷史

The name *Kleff* probably derives from [Middle Dutch](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Dutch "wikilink") *clef*, *clif* ‘cliff, bluff’, referring to the promontory on which the Schwanenburg castle was constructed. Since the city's coat of arms displays three [clovers](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/clover "wikilink") (German *Klee*, [Low German](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low_German "wikilink") *Kliev*), the city's name is sometimes linked by [folk etymology](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/folk_etymology "wikilink") to the clover, but the corresponding [Dutch](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_language "wikilink") word is *klever*.\[3\] Notably, Kleve was spelled with a *C* throughout its history until spelling reforms introduced in the 1930s required that the name be spelled with a *K*. In 2008, the [CDU](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Union_of_Germany "wikilink") announced its intention to return the name to its original spelling.

The Schwanenburg Castle, where the dukes of [Cleves](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Cleves "wikilink") resided, was founded on a steep hill. It is located at the northern terminus of the Kermisdahl where it joins with the Spoykanal, which was previously an important transportation link to the [Rhine](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhine "wikilink"). The old castle has a massive tower, the *Schwanenturm*  high, that is associated in legend with the [Knight of the Swan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knight_of_the_Swan "wikilink"), immortalized in [Richard Wagner](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Wagner "wikilink")'s *[Lohengrin](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lohengrin_\(opera\) "wikilink")*.

Medieval Kleve grew together from four parts — the Schwanenburg Castle, the village below the castle, the first city of Kleve on Heideberg Hill, and the *Neustadt* ("New City"), dating from the 14th century. In 1242 Kleve received city rights. The [Duchy of Cleves](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Cleves "wikilink"), which roughly covered today's districts of Kleve, [Wesel](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wesel_\(district\) "wikilink") and [Duisburg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duisburg "wikilink"), was united with the Duchy of [Mark](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_\(earldom\) "wikilink") in 1368, was made a duchy itself in 1417, then united with the neighboring duchies of [Jülich](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Jülich "wikilink") and Berg in 1521, when [John III, Duke of Cleves](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_III,_Duke_of_Cleves "wikilink"), married [Mary](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maria_of_Jülich-Berg "wikilink"), heiress of Jülich-Berg-Ravenburg. [Cleve_(Merian).jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Cleve_\(Merian\).jpg "fig:Cleve_(Merian).jpg") Kleve's most famous native is [Anne of Cleves](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anne_of_Cleves "wikilink") (1515–1557), daughter of [John III, Duke of Cleves](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_III,_Duke_of_Cleves "wikilink") and (briefly) wife of [Henry VIII of England](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England "wikilink"). Several local businesses are named after her, including the Anne von Kleve Galerie. [De_Kleefse_tuinen_Die_Gärten_von_Kleve_3.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:De_Kleefse_tuinen_Die_Gärten_von_Kleve_3.jpg "fig:De_Kleefse_tuinen_Die_Gärten_von_Kleve_3.jpg") The ducal dynasty became extinct in the male line in 1609, leading to a succession crisis in the duchies. After the [Thirty Years' War](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirty_Years'_War "wikilink") ended in 1648, the succession dispute was resolved with Cleves passing to the elector of [Brandenburg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electorate_of_Brandenburg "wikilink"), thus becoming an [exclave](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclave_and_exclave "wikilink") of the [Margraviate of Brandenburg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg "wikilink"), later [Brandenburg-Prussia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brandenburg-Prussia "wikilink").

During the Thirty Years' War the city had been under the control of the [Dutch Republic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_Republic "wikilink"), which in 1647 had given [Johann Moritz von Nassau-Siegen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Maurice_of_Nassau "wikilink") administrative control over the city. He approved a renovation of Schwanenburg Castle in the baroque style and commissioned the construction of extensive gardens that greatly influenced European landscape design. Significant amounts of his original plan for Kleve were put into effect and have been maintained to the present, a particularly well-loved example of which is the *Forstgarten* (Forest Garden). [Cleve_Stadt_und_Hafen_(um_1895).jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Cleve_Stadt_und_Hafen_\(um_1895\).jpg "fig:Cleve_Stadt_und_Hafen_(um_1895).jpg") The mineral waters of Kleve and the wooded parkland surrounding it made it a fashionable [spa](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/spa_town "wikilink") in the 19th century. At this time, Kleve was named "Bad Cleve" (English: Spa of Cleves).

During [World War II](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "wikilink") Kleve was the site of one of the two radio wave stations that served the [Knickebein](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Beams#Knickebein "wikilink") aircraft navigation system. [Luftwaffe](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luftwaffe "wikilink") bombers used radio beams from Kleve and a second station at Stolberg to navigate to British targets.\[4\] The Knickebein system was eventually jammed by the British. It was replaced by the higher frequency [X-Gerat](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Beams#X-Ger.C3.A4t "wikilink") system, which used transmitter stations located on the channel coast of France. [The_British_Army_in_North-west_Europe_1944-45_B14673.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:The_British_Army_in_North-west_Europe_1944-45_B14673.jpg "fig:The_British_Army_in_North-west_Europe_1944-45_B14673.jpg") Kleve was heavily bombed during the [Second World War](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "wikilink"), and over 90 percent of buildings in the city were severely damaged. Most of the destruction was the result of a raid late in the war in 1945, conducted at the request of Lieutenant-General [Brian Horrocks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Horrocks "wikilink") in preparation for [Operation Veritable](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Veritable "wikilink"). Horrocks recounted his decision in the 1973 television documentary *[The World at War](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_World_at_War "wikilink")*:

> "Then they came to me and they said, “Do you want the town of Cleves taken out?” By "taken out" they meant the whole of the heavy bombers putting on to Cleves. Now, I knew that Cleves was a very fine old historical German town. Anne of Cleves, one of Henry VIII’s wives, came from there. I knew that there were a lot of civilians in Cleves, men, women and children. If I said no, they would live. If I said yes, they would die. A terrible decision you’ve got to take. But... everything depended on getting a high piece of ground at [Materborn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Materborn "wikilink"). The German reserves would have to come through Cleves, and we would have to breach the [Siegfried Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siegfried_Line "wikilink") and get there. And your own lives, your own troops, must come first, so I said yes, I did want it taken out. But when all those bombers went over, the night just before [zero hour](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero_Hour_\(military_designation\) "wikilink"), to take out Cleves, I felt a murderer. And after the war I had an awful lot of nightmares, but always Cleves."\[5\]

Horrocks later said that this had been "the most terrible decision I had ever taken in my life" and that he felt "physically sick" when he saw the bombers overhead. \[6\] \[7\]

As a result of the bombing, relatively little of the pre-1945 city remains. Those structures spared include a number of historic villas built during the heyday of the spa Bad Kleves, located along the B9 near the Tiergarten. Of those buildings destroyed, many were reconstructed, including most of the Schwanenburg and the *Stiftskirche*, the [Catholic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Catholic "wikilink") [parish church](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/parish_church "wikilink"). Constructed on high ground, many of these landmarks can be seen from the surrounding communities.

Since 1953 there has been a broadcasting facility for [FM radio](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/FM_broadcasting "wikilink") and television from regional broadcaster [WDR](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westdeutscher_Rundfunk "wikilink") near Kleve. The current aerial mast was brought into service in 1993. The steel tube mast rises 126.4 metres high and has a diameter of 1.6 metres. It is stabilized by guy wires attached at 57 and 101.6 metres height.

After the Second World War important employers in the area were associated with the West German "Economic Miracle" (*Wirtschaftswunder*), and included the XOX Bisquitfabrik (XOX Biscuit Factory) GmbH and the Van den Berg'schen Margerinewerke (margarine plant), that manufactured biscuits and margarine. Another important employer was the Elefanten-Kinderschuhfabrik (Elefant Children's Shoes Factory).

在當地，零售業越來越重要，尤其是在2002年[歐元制度之後](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/歐元 "wikilink")。荷蘭公民經常會越過開放式邊境來光顧克莱沃的零售商，因此導致克莱沃的購物收入歐元大部分來自[荷蘭](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/荷蘭 "wikilink")。較低的房價也吸引了一波荷蘭公民在該地區置業。

## 人口學

### 人口普查數據

據2013年的克莱沃的統計年鑑\[8\]，50,650人居住在該城市。人口密度約為每平方公里517.9人。86.7％的居民擁有德國公民身份（包括擁有雙重國籍的居民），10.1％的人為歐盟公民身份（5.6％荷蘭人和2.9％波蘭人）。

2013年，該市人口分佈為21歲以下年齡的人士佔總人口的19.7％，21歲至40歲的人士佔25.6％，41歲至60歲的人士佔29.7％，61歲至80歲的人士佔20.1％，81歲及更年老的人士則佔4.9％。每過100名女性就有96.7名男性，每過100名21歲或以上的女性，就有93.9名男性。

81.3%的公民家庭中沒有18歲以下子女，9.2％有一個孩子，6.1％有兩個孩子，1.7％有三個孩子，只有0.1％人士表示自己有四個或更多的孩子。

### 宗教

As the rest of the [Lower Rhine region](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lower_Rhine_region "wikilink"), Kleve is a predominantly [Roman Catholic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church "wikilink") city.\[9\] The city is part of the [Diocese of Münster](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Münster "wikilink"). 61.1% of the residents are Roman Catholics, 14.4% Protestant, and 24.6% "Other". The largest section of this group are residents without any religious affiliation, but there are also sizeable [Russian Orthodox](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Church "wikilink") and [Muslim](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "wikilink") communities in Kleve.

The synagogue of Kleve was destroyed during ** and is today commemorated on the ** (Synagogue square) on which the building´s outline can be seen. The fifty killed Jewish citizens of Cleves are remembered with signs that tell their names, and dates and places of death.\[10\]

## 政府

### 市議會

Prior to the Nazi Era, Kleve's local politics were dominated by the Catholic [Centre Party](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centre_Party_\(Germany\) "wikilink"). This situation continued with the Christian Democratic successor party [CDU](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Union_of_Germany "wikilink") after the Second World War, in spite of resettled displaced people from eastern Germany, most of them Protestants. Until 2004 the CDU controlled an absolute majority of the city council.

Today, Kleve is governed by a coalition of CDU and the [Green Party](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alliance_'90/The_Greens "wikilink"). ### 市長

In 2015, Sonja Northing (no party affiliation) became mayor of Kleve, with 64.5% of the vote. Her candidacy was supported by the [SPD](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/SPD "wikilink") and [FDP](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Democratic_Party_\(Germany\) "wikilink"), and opposed by [CDU](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Union_of_Germany "wikilink") and Green Party candidates. Northing was the first mayor of Cleves since World War I who was not a CDU member.\[11\]

## 語言

克莱沃和的大部分地區的本土語言都是被稱為**克萊沃蘭語**（；）的[荷蘭語](../Page/荷蘭語.md "wikilink")方言，與[南海爾德蘭語的關係最為密切](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/南海爾德蘭語 "wikilink")；但該地官方語言是[德語](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/德語 "wikilink")，後者在年輕一代中占主導地位。

Because of its geographical location at the Dutch-German border, there is a strong overlap in culture and language. One example of this is [Govert Flinck](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Govert_Flinck "wikilink") (1615 – 1660), who though born in Kleve established himself as a [Dutch](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_people "wikilink") artist. On the other hand, the Dutch artist [Barend Cornelis Koekkoek](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barend_Cornelis_Koekkoek "wikilink") (1803 – 1862) settled in Kleve and became a successful landscape painter. His works are collected by and exhibited in the local museum Haus Koekkoek for his and others' romantic paintings.

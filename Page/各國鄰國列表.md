265. In 1949, during the [Chinese Civil
     War](../Page/Chinese_Civil_War.md "wikilink"), the government and
     leaders of the [Republic of
     China](../Page/Republic_of_China.md "wikilink") (ROC), led by
     [Chiang Kai-shek](../Page/Chiang_Kai-shek.md "wikilink"), retreated
     from mainland China to the island of
     [Taiwan](../Page/Taiwan.md "wikilink"). In Taiwan, the ROC
     continues to claim sovereignty over *all* China, including the
     mainland and Taiwan. The [People's Republic of
     China](../Page/People's_Republic_of_China.md "wikilink") (PRC),
     which has governed mainland China since the ROC government
     retreated, also claims to be the sole representative of *all*
     China, including Taiwan. Even though the ROC government in Taiwan
     has never asserted that Taiwan's sovereignty is separate from that
     of China's, for many practical purposes Taiwan is treated by other
     countries and international organizations as a country that is
     separate from the PRC. For the purposes of this list, Taiwan is
     considered to be an entity that is separate from China.

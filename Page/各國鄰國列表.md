265. In 1949, during the [Chinese Civil War](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War "wikilink"), the government and leaders of the [Republic of China](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China "wikilink") (ROC), led by [Chiang Kai-shek](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek "wikilink"), retreated from mainland China to the island of [Taiwan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "wikilink"). In Taiwan, the ROC continues to claim sovereignty over *all* China, including the mainland and Taiwan. The [People's Republic of China](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/People's_Republic_of_China "wikilink") (PRC), which has governed mainland China since the ROC government retreated, also claims to be the sole representative of *all* China, including Taiwan. Even though the ROC government in Taiwan has never asserted that Taiwan's sovereignty is separate from that of China's, for many practical purposes Taiwan is treated by other countries and international organizations as a country that is separate from the PRC. For the purposes of this list, Taiwan is considered to be an entity that is separate from China.

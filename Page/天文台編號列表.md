1878, an astronomical observatory for Tokyo University was founded. This
forerunner of the Tokyo Astronomical Observatory was called at that time
"Kansyoo-dai" in Japanese. It was used to teach astronomy to students of
the department of science. In 1888, an astronomical observatory, which
had been set up by the Navy as the largest one at that time, was
transferred from the Navy to the Ministry of Education and was
designated as the "Tokyo Astronomical Observatory". On this occasion,
the work of astronomical observation and calendar editing, both of which
had been done by the Ministry of International Affairs, were also
transferred to the Tokyo Astronomical Observatory. Terao Hisasi, a
professor of astronomy at Tokyo University, was appointed as the first
director. The Tokyo Astronomical Observatory moved to the outskirts of
Tokyo, Mitaka city in the following year of the Great Kanto Earthquake
of 1923. --\>

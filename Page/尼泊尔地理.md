**[尼泊尔](../Page/尼泊尔.md "wikilink")**是位于[南亚](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/南亚 "wikilink")[喜马拉雅山中段南麓的](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/喜马拉雅山 "wikilink")[内陆国家](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/内陆国家 "wikilink")，版图呈长方形，略呈西北-东南走向，东西宽约850公里，南北宽约200公里，北部与[中国接壤](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/中国 "wikilink")，西、南、东三面为[印度所环绕](../Page/印度.md "wikilink")。地势北高南低，北部为高山，中部为山区和河谷，南部是平原，相对高度差之大为世界所罕见。最高点为北部中尼边境上的[珠穆朗玛峰](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/珠穆朗玛峰 "wikilink")（尼方称萨加玛塔峰），同时也是世界最高峰。另外，全球[14座八千米以上山峰中有](../Page/八千米以上山峰列表.md "wikilink")8座位于尼泊尔境内及其边境上。

尼泊尔气候主要分北部高山、中部温带、南部亚热带三个气候区。北部高山地区冬季最低气温为零下41℃，南部夏季最高气温为45℃。气候受南亚热带季风影响很大。尼泊尔气候一般分为两季，每年10月至次年3月为旱季（冬季），每年4月至9月为雨季（夏季）。

## 水系

尼泊爾境內河流多湍急，部分發源於中國西藏，向南注入[印度](../Page/印度.md "wikilink")[恆河的各大支流](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/恆河 "wikilink")。主要有三大水系，由東向西依次為[戈西河](../Page/戈西河.md "wikilink")、[甘達基河](../Page/甘達基河.md "wikilink")（那拉雅尼河）及[加格拉河](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/加格拉河 "wikilink")（格爾納利河）；另外在東南端有一小塊地區屬於[默哈嫩達河水系](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/默哈嫩達河 "wikilink")。

戈西河在尼泊爾境內又稱為「沙普塔戈西河」（Sapta
Koshi，意指七條戈西河），導因於它在尼泊爾東部的[喜馬拉雅山區有七條主要支流](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/喜馬拉雅山脈 "wikilink")：、、塔馬科西河、里庫河、[都得科西河](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/都得科西河 "wikilink")、[阿龍河及](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/阿龍河 "wikilink")。阿龍河發源於[西藏境內](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/西藏 "wikilink")，離尼泊爾北部邊界約。遜科西河的一條支流[波特科西河也發源於西藏境內](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/波特科西河 "wikilink")，延著連結[加德滿都與](../Page/加德滿都.md "wikilink")[拉薩的](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/拉薩 "wikilink")南流。

甘達基河在尼泊爾境內稱為「那拉雅尼河」，也稱為「沙普塔甘達基河」（Sapta
Gandaki，意指七條甘達基河），因為它在尼泊爾中部的喜馬拉雅山區也有七條主要支流：卡里甘達基河、，以及特里蘇里河的五條支流，包括、馬蒂河、[馬沙陽蒂河](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/馬沙陽蒂河 "wikilink")、達勞蒂河及布利河。

加格拉河幹流上游在尼泊爾境內稱為「格爾納利河」（又譯卡拿里河），加格拉河的另一支流[夏達河為尼泊爾與印度的西側邊界](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/夏達河 "wikilink")。

## Trans-Himalayan Region

The main watershed between the
[Brahmaputra](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brahmaputra_River "wikilink")
(called *[Yarlung
Tsangpo](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yarlung_Zangbo_River "wikilink")*
in [Tibet](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibet "wikilink")) and the
[Ganges](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganges "wikilink") system
(including all of Nepal) actually lies north of the highest ranges.
Alpine, often semi-arid valleys—including
[Humla](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humla_district "wikilink"),
[Jumla](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jumla_district "wikilink"),
[Dolpo](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dolpo "wikilink"),
[Mustang](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mustang_District "wikilink"),
[Manang](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manang "wikilink") and
[Khumbu](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khumbu "wikilink")—cut between
Himalayan sub ranges or lie north of them.

Some of these valleys historically were more accessible from
[Tibet](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibet "wikilink") than Nepal and
are populated by people with
[Tibetan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibetan_People "wikilink")
affinities called
*[Bhotiya](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhotiya "wikilink")* or
*[Bhutia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhutia "wikilink")* including
the famous
[Sherpas](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sherpa_people "wikilink") in
Kumbu valley near Mount Everest. With [Chinese cultural
hegemony](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinicization_of_Tibet "wikilink")
in Tibet itself, these valleys have become repositories of traditional
ways. Valleys with better access from the hill regions to the south are
culturally linked to Nepal as well as Tibet, notably the [Kali Gandaki
Gorge](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kali_Gandaki_Gorge "wikilink")
where [Thakali](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thakali_people "wikilink")
culture shows influences in both directions.

Permanent villages in the mountain region stand as high as  with summer
encampments even higher. Bhotiyas graze
[yaks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/yak "wikilink"), grow cold-tolerant
crops such as
[potatoes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/potato "wikilink"),
[barley](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/barley "wikilink"),
[buckwheat](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/buckwheat "wikilink") and
[millet](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/millet "wikilink"). They
traditionally traded across the mountains, e.g., Tibetan salt for
[rice](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/rice "wikilink") from lowlands in
Nepal and India. Since trade was restricted in the 1950s they have found
work as high altitude porters, guides, cooks and other accessories to
tourism and alpinism.

## Climate

### Altitudinal belts

[Satellite_image_of_Nepal_in_October_2002.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Satellite_image_of_Nepal_in_October_2002.jpg "fig:Satellite_image_of_Nepal_in_October_2002.jpg")
Nepal's latitude is about the same as that of the US state of
[Florida](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida "wikilink"), however
with elevations ranging from less than  to over  and precipitation from
to over  the country has eight climate zones from tropical to perpetual
snow.\[1\]

The
**[tropical](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/tropical_climate "wikilink")
zone** below  experiences
[frost](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/frost "wikilink") less than once
per decade. It can be subdivided into lower tropical (below 300 meters
or 1,000 ft.) with 18% of the nation's land area) and upper (18% of land
area) tropical zones. The best
[mangoes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/mango "wikilink") and well as
[papaya](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/papaya "wikilink") and
[banana](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/banana "wikilink") are largely
confined to the lower zone. Other fruit such as
[litchee](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/litchee "wikilink"),
[jackfruit](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/jackfruit "wikilink"),
[citrus](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/citrus "wikilink") and mangoes of
lower quality grow in the upper tropical zone as well. Winter crops
include grains and vegetables typically grown in temperate climates. The
Outer Terai is virtually all in the lower tropical zone. Inner Terai
valleys span both tropical zones. The Sivalik Hills are mostly upper
tropical. Tropical climate zones extend far up river valleys across the
Middle Hills and even into the Mountain regions.

The **[subtropical](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subtropics "wikilink")
climate zone** from  occupies 22% of Nepal's land area and is the most
prevalent climate of the Middle Hills above river valleys. It
experiences frost up to 53 days per year, however, this varies greatly
with elevation, proximity to high mountains and terrain either draining
or ponding cold air drainage. Crops include rice, maize, millet, wheat,
potato, stone fruits and citrus.

The great majority of Nepal's population occupies the tropical and
subtropical climate zones. In the Middle Hills, upper-caste Hindus are
concentrated in tropical valleys which are well suited for rice
cultivation while
[Janajati](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adivasi "wikilink") ethnic
groups mostly live above in the subtropical zone and grow other grains
more than rice.

The **[Temperate
climate](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperate_climate "wikilink")
zone** from  occupies 12% of Nepal's land area and has up to 153 annual
days of frost. It is encountered in higher parts of the Middle Hills and
throughout much of the Mountain region. Crops include cold-tolerant
rice, maize, wheat, barley, potato, apple, walnut, peach, various cole,
amaranthus and buckwheat.

The
**[Subalpine](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subalpine#Subalpine_zone "wikilink")
zone** from  occupies 9% of Nepal's land area, mainly in the Mountain
and Himalayan regions. It has permanent settlements in the Himalaya, but
further south it is only seasonally occupied as pasture for sheep,
goats, yak and hybrids in warmer months. There are up to 229 annual days
of frost here. Crops include barley, potato, cabbage, cauliflower,
amaranthus, buckwheat and apple. Medicinal plants are gathered.

The
**[Alpine](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montane_ecology#Alpine_grasslands_and_tundra "wikilink")
zone** from  occupies 8% of the country's land area. There are a few
permanent settlements above 4,000 meters. There is virtually no plant
cultivation although medicinal herbs are gathered. Sheep, goats, yaks
and hybrids are pastured in warmer months.

Above 5,000 meters the climate becomes
**[Nival](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snow_line "wikilink")** and
there is no human habitation or even seasonal use.

Arid and semi-arid land in the
[rainshadow](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/rainshadow "wikilink") of
high ranges have a **[Transhimalayan
climate](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_desert#Cold_desert_climates "wikilink")**.
Population density is very low. Cultivation and husbandry conform to
subalpine and alpine patterns but depend on snowmelt and streams for
irrigation.

Precipitation generally decreases from east to west with increasing
distance from the [Bay of
Bengal](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal "wikilink"), source
of the summer monsoon. Eastern Nepal gets about  annually; the Kathmandu
area about  and western Nepal about . This pattern is modified by
adiabatic effects as rising air masses cool and drop their moisture

[Annapurna](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annapurna "wikilink") Himalaya
beyond a relatively low stretch of the [Mahabharat

### Seasons

The year is divided into a wet season from June to September—as summer
warmth over Inner Asia creates a low pressure zone that draws in moist
air from the Indian Ocean—and a dry season from October to June as cold
temperatures in the vast interior creates a high pressure zone causing
dry air to flow outward. April and May are months of intense water
stress when cumulative effects of the long dry season are exacerbated by
temperatures rising over  in the tropical climate belt. Seasonal drought
further intensifies in the
[Siwaliks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siwaliks "wikilink") hills
consisting of poorly consolidated, coarse, permeable sediments that do
not retain water, so hillsides are often covered with drought-tolerant
scrub forest. In fact much of Nepal's native vegetation adapted to
withstand drought, but less so at higher elevations where cooler
temperatures mean less water stress.

The summer monsoon may be preceded by a buildup of thunderstorm activity
that provides water for rice seedbeds. Sustained rain on average arrives
in mid-June as rising temperatures over Inner Asia creates a low
pressure zone that draws in moist air from the Indian Ocean, but this
can vary up to a month. Significant failure of monsoon rains
historically meant drought and famine while above-normal rains still
cause flooding and landslides with losses in human lives, farmland and
buildings.

The monsoon also complicates transportation with roads and trails
washing out while unpaved roads and airstrips may become unusable and
cloud cover reduces safety margins for aviation. Rains diminish in
September and generally end by mid-October, ushering in generally cool,
clear, and dry weather, as well as the most relaxed and jovial period in
Nepal. By this time, the harvest is completed and people are in a
festive mood. The two biggest and most important Hindu
festivals—[Dashain](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dashain "wikilink")
and [Tihar](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tihar_\(festival\) "wikilink")
([Dipawali](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diwali "wikilink"))—arrive
during this period, about one month apart. The post monsoon season lasts
until about December.

After the post monsoon comes the winter monsoon, a strong northeasterly
flow marked by occasional, short rainfalls in the lowlands and plains
and snowfalls in the high-altitude areas. In this season the Himalayas
function as a barrier to cold air masses from Inner Asia, so southern
Nepal and northern India have warmer winters than would otherwise be the
case. April and May are dry and hot, especially below  where afternoon
temperatures may exceed .

## Environment

The dramatic changes in elevation along this transect result in a
variety of [biomes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/biome "wikilink"),
from [tropical
savannas](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_grasslands,_savannas,_and_shrublands "wikilink")
along the Indian border, to
[subtropical](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/subtropical "wikilink")
[broadleaf](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_moist_broadleaf_forests "wikilink")
and [coniferous
forests](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_coniferous_forests "wikilink")
in the hills, to
[temperate](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/temperate "wikilink")
[broadleaf](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperate_broadleaf_and_mixed_forests "wikilink")
and
[coniferous](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperate_coniferous_forest "wikilink")
forests on the slopes of the Himalaya, to [montane grasslands and
shrublands](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/montane_grasslands_and_shrublands "wikilink"),
and finally rock and ice at the highest elevations.

This corresponds to the [Terai-Duar savanna and
grasslands](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terai-Duar_savanna_and_grasslands "wikilink")
ecoregion.

Subtropical forests dominate the lower elevations of the Hill Region.
They form a mosaic running east-west across Nepal, with [Himalayan
subtropical broadleaf
forests](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Himalayan_subtropical_broadleaf_forests "wikilink")
between  and [Himalayan subtropical pine
forests](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Himalayan_subtropical_pine_forests "wikilink")
between . At higher elevations, to , are found temperate broadleaf
forests: [eastern Himalayan broadleaf
forests](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/eastern_Himalayan_broadleaf_forests "wikilink")
to the east of the [Gandaki
River](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gandaki_River "wikilink") and
[western Himalayan broadleaf
forests](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/western_Himalayan_broadleaf_forests "wikilink")
to the west.

The native forests of the Mountain Region change from east to west as
precipitation decreases. They can be broadly classified by their
relation to the Gandaki River. From  are the
[eastern](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/eastern_Himalayan_subalpine_conifer_forests "wikilink")
and [western Himalayan subalpine conifer
forests](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/western_Himalayan_subalpine_conifer_forests "wikilink").
To  are the
[eastern](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/eastern_Himalayan_alpine_shrub_and_meadows "wikilink")
and [western Himalayan alpine shrub and
meadows](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/western_Himalayan_alpine_shrub_and_meadows "wikilink").

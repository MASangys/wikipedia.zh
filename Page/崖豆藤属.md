**崖豆藤属**（学名：***Millettia***），是[豆科](../Page/豆科.md "wikilink")[蝶形花亞科下的一个属](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/蝶形花亞科 "wikilink")，为[乔木或](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/乔木 "wikilink")[灌木](../Page/灌木.md "wikilink")植物。该属共有约150种，分布于[热带](../Page/热带.md "wikilink")和[亚热带](../Page/亚热带.md "wikilink")地区，例如[牛大力](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/牛大力 "wikilink")（又稱「[美麗崖豆藤](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/美麗崖豆藤 "wikilink")」；Snowy Millettia）\[1\]。

## 簡述

In 1834, in *Prodromus Florae Peninsulae Indiae Orientalis* [Robert Wight](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Wight "wikilink") and [George Arnott Walker-Arnott](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Arnott_Walker-Arnott "wikilink") describe *Millettia* as:

> [Calyx](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sepal "wikilink") cup-shaped, lobed or slightly toothed. [Corolla](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petal "wikilink") [papilionaceous](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papilionaceous_flower "wikilink"): vexillum recurved, broad, emarginate, glabrous or silky on the back. Stamens diadelphous (9 and 1), the tenth quite distinct. [Legume](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legume "wikilink") flat, elliptic or lanceolate, pointed, coriaceous, thick margined, wingless indehiscent, 1-2 seeded: valves closely cohering with each other all round the seeds and between them. Twining or arboreous. Leaves very large, unequally pinnated: leaflets opposite, with a setaceous partial stipule at the base of each partial [petiole](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petiole_\(botany\) "wikilink"). [Racemes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raceme "wikilink") axillary, more or less branched and compound. Flowers pretty large, purplish, pedicelled on shortish diverging partial [peduncles](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peduncle_\(botany\) "wikilink").\[2\]

## Etymology

Long known to residents of the [Indies](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indies "wikilink"), [China](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "wikilink"), and [Africa](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "wikilink"), this species has had many traditional names. One of the oldest references in [traditional Chinese medicine](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/traditional_Chinese_medicine "wikilink") is in *[Bencao Gangmu Shiyi](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bencao_Gangmu_Shiyi "wikilink")* ("Supplement to [Compendium of Materia Medica](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compendium_of_Materia_Medica "wikilink")") where is called jixueteng. The Chinese name literally translates to "stem of chicken's blood" which refers to the red resin present in the stems of several climbing legume shrubs.\[3\]

In the 1820s-1830s Charles Millett, a plant collector and an official with the [East India Company](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_India_Company "wikilink"), collected many samples of Millettia while living in [Canton](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "wikilink") and [Macao](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macao "wikilink"). He sent them to the [University of Glasgow](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Glasgow "wikilink")'s Botanical Garden. In 1834, [Robert Wight](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Wight "wikilink") and [George Arnott Walker-Arnott](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Arnott_Walker-Arnott "wikilink"), both Scottish botanists, published *Prodromus Florae Peninsulae Indiae Orientalis* where the genus Millettia is first mentioned. The authors named the genus after Charles Millett, incorrectly referring to him as Dr. Charles Millett.\[4\]Charles Millett of the East India Company has often been confused with Charles Millet, a French [ichthyologist](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/ichthyologist "wikilink"), who was active around the same time.\[5\]In addition J. A. Millet, a French botanist from the 18th century, is often misattributed as the source.\[6\]

## 用途

[水黄皮](../Page/水黄皮.md "wikilink")(（）的種子可供榨油，油含量約為重量的25%，在印度為[生物柴油](../Page/生物柴油.md "wikilink")的[原料](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/原料 "wikilink")\[7\]\[8\]。

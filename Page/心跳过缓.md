**Bradycardia** is a condition wherein an individual has a slow [heart
rate](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/heart_rate "wikilink"), typically
defined as a [heart
rate](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/heart_rate "wikilink") of under 60
[beats per minute](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heart_rate "wikilink")
(BPM) in adults.\[1\] Bradycardia typically does not cause symptoms
until the rate drops below 50 BPM. When symptomatic, it may cause
fatigue, weakness, dizziness, sweating, and at very low rates,
[fainting](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syncope_\(medicine\) "wikilink").\[2\]

During sleep, a slow heartbeat with rates around 40–50 BPM is common,
and is considered normal. Highly trained
[athletes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/sportsperson "wikilink") may
also have [athletic heart
syndrome](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/athletic_heart_syndrome "wikilink"),
a very slow resting heart rate that occurs as a sport adaptation and
helps prevent
[tachycardia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/tachycardia "wikilink")
during training.

The term relative bradycardia is used in explaining a heart rate that,
although not actually below 60 BPM, is still considered too slow for the
individual's current medical condition.

**心跳过慢**（Bradycardia）也称**心动过缓**、**心跳过缓**、**心动徐缓**、**徐脉**。是指心脏跳动速度低于正常水平，达到每分钟50次以下的现象。心跳过慢可能是由迷走神经亢奋、[窦房结病变](../Page/窦房结.md "wikilink")、窦房结的冲动波遭到阻挡等原因引起，常见于老年人、[心脏病患者](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/心脏病 "wikilink")\[3\]

[修辞学](../Page/修辞学.md "wikilink")中的**情感用词变化**（），是一种不规则动词的[变位](../Page/变位.md "wikilink")，目的是粉饰人类的癖好，把自己同样的行为描述得比别人更好。\[1\]1948年哲学家[伯特兰·罗素](../Page/伯特兰·罗素.md "wikilink")首次在BBC广播节目[The Brains Trust中提出了此概念](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Brains_Trust "wikilink")\[2\]，因此也称作**罗素用词变化**。罗素在节目中举了这些例子\[3\]：

> I am firm, You are obstinate, He is a pig-headed fool.
> I am righteously indignant, you are annoyed, he is making a fuss over nothing.
> I have reconsidered the matter, you have changed your mind, he has gone back on his word.

可以像[扣帽子](../Page/扣帽子.md "wikilink")的情感用词变化\[4\]，通过含糊一个争论的[謬論错误支持某观点](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/謬論 "wikilink")。这种用词结构也是一种[幽默](../Page/幽默.md "wikilink")\[5\]，[英國廣播公司電視情景喜剧](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/英國廣播公司電視 "wikilink")《[是，首相](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/是，首相 "wikilink")》中的[伯纳德·伍利这样说](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/伯纳德·伍利 "wikilink")：\[6\]\[7\]

> It's one of those irregular verbs, isn't it?
> I have an independent mind, You are eccentric, He is round the twist.\[8\]

> That's another of those irregular verbs, isn't it?
> I give confidential press briefings; you leak; he's being charged under section 2A of the Official Secrets Act.\[9\]

**扁蜷科**（學名：），亦作**扁卷螺科**，是[腹足綱之下的一個](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/腹足綱 "wikilink")[單系群](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/單系群 "wikilink")[支序](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/支序 "wikilink")，本科物種皆為可呼吸空氣的[淡水螺](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/淡水螺 "wikilink")\[1\]。原屬[有肺目](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/有肺目 "wikilink")[基眼亞目](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/基眼亞目 "wikilink")[椎實螺總科](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/椎實螺總科 "wikilink")，今屬[異鰓類](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/異鰓總目 "wikilink")[泛有肺類](../Page/泛有肺類.md "wikilink")[喜溼螺類支序](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/喜溼螺類 "wikilink")\[2\]。

本科多個物種的外殼都是[平旋壳](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/平旋壳 "wikilink")（）\[3\]，與一般腹足綱物種的外殼形狀不同。外殼看似是[右旋](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/右旋 "wikilink")，但其實是螺把殼上下顛倒來揹在身上，使[左旋的外殼看起來像是右旋](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/左旋 "wikilink")。
因其外形，本科物種又稱。

由於本科物種的頭和腹足都比較細小，使得牠們頭上絲般幼的觸角顯得很細長。

## 分類

對於部份分類元來說，是否應該歸入扁蜷科仍有爭議。這對於淡水[帽貝](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/帽貝 "wikilink")*[Ferrissia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferrissia "wikilink")*跟*[Ancylus](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancylus "wikilink")*這兩個屬來說由其合適。這些屬有時會被歸入[椎实螺科](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/椎实螺科 "wikilink")（[Lymnaeidae](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lymnaeidae "wikilink")
[Rafinesque](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/康斯坦丁·萨缪尔·拉方斯克-舒马茨 "wikilink"),
1815）；另外亦有文獻把上述兩個屬放進各自的一個科裡。然而，在[布歇特和洛克羅伊的腹足類分類
(2005年)](../Page/布歇特和洛克羅伊的腹足類分類_\(2005年\).md "wikilink")\[4\]的分類，這些屬被歸為[扁蜷亞科](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/扁蜷亞科 "wikilink")（[Planorbinae](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planorbinae "wikilink")）之下的[Ancylini族](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancylini "wikilink")。以下將詳述這個分類的具體內容。

## Shell description

[Biomphalaria_tenagophila_shell.png](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Biomphalaria_tenagophila_shell.png "fig:Biomphalaria_tenagophila_shell.png")
of *[Biomphalaria
tenagophila](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomphalaria_tenagophila "wikilink")*.
Scale bar is 3 mm.\]\] The
[shells](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/gastropod_shell "wikilink") of
most species in this family are disk-like or button-like, being coiled
in one plane, although several groups have shells that are more
higher-spired, and some are
[limpet](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/limpet "wikilink")-like.

All coiled shell Planorbidae are
[sinistral](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/sinistral "wikilink") in their
shell coiling, as is proved by their internal anatomy (the respiratory
and the genital orifice are situated on the left side), however the
animals carry their shells with what would normally be the ventral (i.e.
umbilical) surface uppermost, and because of this, the shells appear to
be dextral.

Indeed, formerly planorbids were thought to have dextral shells, and so
species of this family were figured as if they had
[dextral](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/dextral "wikilink") shells.
Although it is now understood that these species are sinistral in shell
coiling, disk-like Planorbid shells are often still shown in
illustrations oriented as if they were dextral.

Most species of coiled planorbids have a rather thin and moderately
smooth shell, although more distinct sculpture such as a keel occurs in,
and is diagnostic of, certain species. In the flat, keeled species, the
whorls tend to overlap.

The
[aperture](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/aperture_\(mollusc\) "wikilink")
has a sharp outer lip. A peristome can be present, but often the lip is
not thickened nor reflected. Those planorbid species which have a
high-spired shell may have a narrow
[umbilicus](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umbilicus_\(mollusc\) "wikilink"),
but frequently this is covered by
[callus](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/parietal_callus "wikilink").

In height most species vary between 6 mm and 6 cm, however, disk-like
shells are usually less than about 2 cm in maximum dimension.

Like all pulmonate aquatic snails, ramshorn shells do not have an
[operculum](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operculum_\(gastropod\) "wikilink")
to close the shell aperture.

### Sinistral shells

[Planorbarius_corneus_top.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Planorbarius_corneus_top.jpg "fig:Planorbarius_corneus_top.jpg")*.
View of the sunken
[spire](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/spire_\(mollusc\) "wikilink")
(held facing downwards in life)\]\]
[Planorbarius_corneus_bottom.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Planorbarius_corneus_bottom.jpg "fig:Planorbarius_corneus_bottom.jpg")*.
View of the
[umbilicus](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/umbilicus_\(mollusc\) "wikilink")
(held uppermost in life)\]\]
[Lymnea-snail.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Lymnea-snail.JPG "fig:Lymnea-snail.JPG")
Flat-coiled planorbid [gastropod
shells](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/gastropod_shell "wikilink") are
hard to understand in terms of their coiling and orientation. Many of
the shells of species in this family are almost planispiral in coiling
such that one side of the shell often looks rather like the other side,
but it is important to bear in mind that nonetheless there is an
[umbilical](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umbilicus_\(mollusc\) "wikilink")
side and a
[spire](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/spire_\(mollusc\) "wikilink") side
of the shell. In addition these are in fact sinistral shells, despite
the fact that the snail carries its shell as if it were a normal dextral
shell. To make sense of the shell coiling, the following facts are
useful:

  - In life, these pond snails hold their shells *upside down* compared
    to the normal gastropod shell orientation, with the
    [umbilicus](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/umbilicus_\(mollusk\) "wikilink")
    facing upwards
  - The
    [spire](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/spire_\(mollusc\) "wikilink")
    of the shell is quite sunken in many species, in addition it is
    carried facing downwards
  - The umbilicus of the shell is very wide and shallow
  - In some species the umbilicus is not as deeply "dished" as the
    sunken spire is, so superficially it can be hard to tell one from
    the other

However, once it is understood that the planorbid shell is sinistral, if
the shell is held with the
[aperture](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/aperture_\(mollusc\) "wikilink")
on the left and facing the observer, then the sunken spire side of the
shell is uppermost. This is a convenience for understanding the shell,
but is the opposite of the way the shell is actually carried in life.

The side of the shell which is in fact the spire (a sunken spire) faces
*down* in the living animal, contrary to what is the case in almost all
other shelled gastropods. Because the shell is carried "upside down"
like this, the
[aperture](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/aperture_\(mollusc\) "wikilink")
of the shell is angled to face downwards also, so the aperture faces a
little towards the spire, not away from it, as is usually the case in
other shelled gastropods.

## 血色素

相對於其他的軟體動物，扁卷螺科物種的血液都含有[血紅素](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/血紅素 "wikilink")。因此，這些動物呼吸氧氣時都較其他軟體動物來得更有效；而血紅素亦令這些動物的身體帶紅色，這尤其在[白化物種身上更明顯](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/白化 "wikilink")。

## 習性

扁卷螺科的大多數物種都只能在淡水生活，只有少數物種能存活於鹹淡水交界。所以對於其他未能存活於鹹淡水的物種，亦令農夫可以用食鹽來清除牠們。

## Geological history

Ancestors of ramshorn snails are known with certainty since the
[Jurassic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jurassic "wikilink") period.
Modern taxa developed since the
[Cretaceous](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cretaceous "wikilink").

## Geographical distribution

Species in this family occur worldwide. In Northwest Europe about 20
species are known (including non-indigenous species). In this region,
various extinct taxa are known to have occurred, starting in the
[Jurassic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jurassic "wikilink") period.

**扎玛尔**（，）是[也门西南部的一个城市](../Page/也门.md "wikilink")，是[扎玛尔省的首府](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/扎玛尔省 "wikilink")。海拔2700米，位于首都[萨那以南](../Page/萨那.md "wikilink")100公里处。

名称来源于公元15到35年的[示巴王國的国王](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/示巴王國 "wikilink")[Dhamar
Ali
Yahber](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhamar_Ali_Yahber "wikilink")。该地是具有[考古学意义的城市](../Page/考古学.md "wikilink")，是著名的阿拉伯与伊斯兰文化的中心，以及也门的科学中心，最大的清真寺建于[Abi-Bakr
Al-Sadeek](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abi-Bakr_Al-Sadeek "wikilink")[哈里发时代](../Page/哈里发.md "wikilink")。

## Overview

[Dhamar_Ali_Yahbur_II.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Dhamar_Ali_Yahbur_II.jpg "fig:Dhamar_Ali_Yahbur_II.jpg")
Dhamar is situated 100 km to the south of
[Sana'a](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sana'a "wikilink"), north of
[Ibb](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibb "wikilink"), and west of [Al
Bayda, Yemen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al_Bayda,_Yemen "wikilink"),
2400-2500 m above sea level. Its name “Dhamar” goes back to the king of
[Sheba](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheba "wikilink") and
[Dhuo-Raydan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zafar,_Yemen "wikilink") at
15-35 AD. whose name was [Dhamar Ali Yahbir
II](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhamar_Ali_Yahbir_II "wikilink"), and
whose [statue](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/statue "wikilink") was
found at the city of [Al-Nakhla
Al-Hamra’a](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Nakhla_Al-Hamra’a "wikilink")
("The Red Palm"). This city is one of the
[archeological](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaeology "wikilink")
sites that are found near Dhamar.

The city of Dhamar is the capital of the governorate and is situated on
the main road, which connects Sana’a with a number of other
governorates. This city was one of the famous Arabic and Islamic culture
and scientific centers in Yemen. Its Great Mosque was built in the
period of the caliph [Abi-Bakr
Al-Sadeek](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abu_Bakr "wikilink").

As Dhamar city had a great role in the politic and trading life in
Yemen. It had a very important historical role in Yemen before the
[Islamic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "wikilink") age. The
antique Yemeni engravings mentioned Dhamar city as being a very famous
center of the Islamic studies and sciences, and many of the great
scientists are attributed to this historical town.

In past times, the people of Dhamar were famous for bringing up
[horses](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/horse "wikilink"); the city was
an early center of horse-breeding in Arabia. The town is still famous in
Yemen for its numerous historical
[mosques](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/mosque "wikilink") and schools,
which are distinguished by their beautiful characteristic architecture
in harmony with the colors of its volcano land.

The city of Dhamar, in the center of the [Dhamar
basin](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhamar_basin "wikilink"), is of
ancient origin. It was built by the legendary
[Himyarite](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Himyarite "wikilink") King
[Dhamar Ali Yahbir
II](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhamar_Ali_Yahbir_II "wikilink"),
renowned for restoring the great dam of Ma’rib. Dhamar is the only town
in northern Yemen, which is not surrounded by a wall or natural
defensive formations; it is just a settlement on the plains. Centrally
situated with good connections to the nearby governorates, the town has
prospered as a Wednesday market and meeting place for tribes living
nearby.

It is home to the [University of
Dhamar](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhamar_University "wikilink") the
largest university in the country.

[Rosicrucians](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosicrucianism "wikilink")
associate Dhamar with the mystical city of
[Damcar](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damcar "wikilink") -- a city in
[Arabia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabia "wikilink") described in
the *[Fama
Fraternitatis](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fama_Fraternitatis "wikilink"),*
inhabited by scholars and alchemists who studied the lost secrets of
[Hermes
Trismegistus](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermes_Trismegistus "wikilink")
and [Solomon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solomon "wikilink"), visited
by [Christian
Rosenkreuz](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rosenkreuz "wikilink").

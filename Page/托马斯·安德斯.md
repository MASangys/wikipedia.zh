**托马斯·安德斯**（本名Bernd
Weidung；）是[德国](../Page/德国.md "wikilink")[歌手](../Page/歌手.md "wikilink")、[作曲家](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/作曲家 "wikilink")、[音樂製作人](../Page/音樂製作人.md "wikilink")。\[1\]
是德国流行重唱[摩登语录的主唱](../Page/Modern_Talking.md "wikilink")，活动于1983–1987与1998–2003.\[2\]

## 生平

### 早年

Anders was born in
[Koblenz](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/科布倫茨 "wikilink"), [West
Germany](../Page/西德.md "wikilink"). He began showing interest in music
as a child, he did his first stage-performance at age of 7. Anders first
studied music at Koblenz Eichendorff-Gymnasium, and then he studied
musicology for five semesters at [Johannes Gutenberg
University](../Page/美茵茨大学.md "wikilink") in
[Mainz](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/美茵茨 "wikilink"),
[Rhineland-Palatinate](../Page/莱茵兰-普法尔茨.md "wikilink"), Germany.\[3\]
After having performed at several clubs and discothèques, in 1979, he
participated in a music contest organized by the radio station ** which
he walked away from without earning enough points. Two days after the
contest, Anders was offered a record contract by the same jury members
of the radio contest.

It was then that his career officially began when in 1980, at age of 17,
he released his debut single "Judy". Anders released several other
singles produced by Daniel David including "Du weinst um ihn", which he
performed on Michael Schanze's TV show *Hätten sie heut' zeit für uns*.
In 1983, he met  who produced "Was macht das schön", the
[German](../Page/德国.md "wikilink") cover version of 's "Pick Up the
Phone" and also "Heißkalter Engel", the German cover version of 's "Send
Me an Angel". From late 1983 to the mid of 1984 Bohlen produced other
tracks for Anders including "Wovon träumst du denn", "Endstation
Sehnsucht" and "Es geht mir gut heut' Nacht". The success, however, came
when in late 1983, Anders and Bohlen formed the successful duo [Modern
Talking](../Page/Modern_Talking.md "wikilink"), which they started
things off by a worldwide hit single "".\[4\]

### 独走生涯

In late 1987, Anders and Bohlen decided to terminate Modern Talking and
go their separate ways due to constant quarrel between the two. Shortly
after which Anders went on a long tour, covering cities like
[Budapest](../Page/布达佩斯.md "wikilink"),
[Ljubljana](../Page/卢布尔雅那.md "wikilink"),
[Potsdam](../Page/波茨坦.md "wikilink"),
[Wrocław](../Page/弗罗茨瓦夫.md "wikilink"),
[Gdańsk](../Page/格但斯克.md "wikilink"),\[5\] [Hong
Kong](../Page/香港.md "wikilink"),
[Singapore](../Page/新加坡.md "wikilink"),
[Taipei](../Page/臺北市.md "wikilink"),
[Moscow](../Page/莫斯科.md "wikilink"),\[6\] [Viña del
Mar](../Page/比尼亚德尔马.md "wikilink")\[7\] and [Sun
City](../Page/太陽城_\(南非\).md "wikilink").\[8\] Although Anders left
Germany for the [United States](../Page/美国.md "wikilink"), in 1989, his
first solo album, ** was released in Germany on . As the title of the
album suggests, it was quite different from all of Modern Talking's
materials. All of the songs had more pop style rather than dance like
tunes. Some of the tracks on the album were produced by [Elton
John](../Page/艾爾頓·約翰.md "wikilink")'s producer  including the single
"Love of My Own". Anders also worked with 's producer , who produced
three tracks on the album. Anders did produce three of the tracks
himself along with  including "You Are My Life", "One Thing" and "Close
Your Eyes to Heaven". The first single "Love of My Own" released in the
same year is considered the most successful single of his solo career at
that time, which peaked at No.24 in Germany.\[9\] The album *Different*,
however, failed to enter the German album chart.

The second album ** was released in 1991, three singles were released
off Anders' second solo album "The Sweet Hello, the Sad Goodbye" written
by [Roxette](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/罗克塞特 "wikilink") member , "I
Can't Give You Anything (But My Love)" and "True Love". In 1992, Anders
released his third album **, the singles "How Deep Is Your Love" and
"Standing Alone" (Duet with [Glenn
Medeiros](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/格兰·梅德罗斯 "wikilink")) found some
success both in [Germany](../Page/德国.md "wikilink") and
[Austria](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/奥地利 "wikilink"). Anders released
his fourth solo album ** in 1993. The first single "When Will I See You
Again" featured the original singers of the song, [The Three
Degrees](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/第三度空間 "wikilink"). Also, in 1993,
Anders experienced something new as he got a part in a
[Swedish](../Page/瑞典.md "wikilink") movie **.\[10\] He also co-wrote
with  the title-song for that film "Marathon of Life", which is one of
the tracks in his 1993's album *When Will I See You Again*. In 1994, he
came back with an album **, which he released in
[Spanish](../Page/西班牙语.md "wikilink") only for U.S., South
America and Argentina,\[11\] this album contained songs from albums of
1992 and 1993, all redone in Spanish. Anders released one more album in
1995 called **, which he produced together with , this album contained a
duet featuring  "Feel for the Physical". Things for him then were quiet
until March 1998 when Thomas Anders and Dieter Bohlen announced the
reunion of Modern Talking.\[12\] The duo released six studio albums
between 1998 and 2003 and split again in 2003.

### 后续岁月

In 2001, Anders produced the song, "My Heart It Feels Like (Dub Dub
...)" together with Gerd Count Bernadotte for the pop girl duo
*It-Girl*.\[13\] In late 2003 Anders signed a contract with
[BMG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/贝塔斯曼音乐集团 "wikilink") international
as a solo artist after their final album of Modern Talking. The first
single, "Independent Girl" released in November 2003 reached No.17 on
the German single chart and No.5 in Russia.\[14\]\[15\] His album, **
released in 2004 entered the top-20 as well.\[16\] The second single
"King of Love" reached the top-40 in Germany.\[17\] At this point,
Anders was working with his producer , who previously had worked with
such artists as  and .

Thomas Anders received an offer from NDR to host the [Eurovision Song
Contest 2004](../Page/2004年歐洲歌唱大賽.md "wikilink"), 11 million viewers who
already knew Anders as a singer watched him announce the German voting
points.\[18\] Also, in 2004, Anders was asked to record the ballad "Just
Dream" which was to be the official anthem for the ballet  which reached
No.64 in Germany.\[19\] The song can be heard during the international
three-year  tour.

In the following year, Anders was involved in a legal battle which he
had brought against his Modern Talking partner, Dieter Bohlen.\[20\]
Anders claimed that Bohlen had slandered about him in his
autobiographical book.\[21\] In the Spring of 2005, the court rejected
to have Bohlen pay the one million Euros which Anders had sued Bohlen
for.\[22\]

In March 2006, Anders got involved in [Eurovision Song
Contest](../Page/歐洲歌唱大賽.md "wikilink") again; however, he was one of
the three contestants to represent [Germany](../Page/德国.md "wikilink")
in the [Eurovision Song Contest 2006](../Page/2006年歐洲歌唱大賽.md "wikilink")
alongside the Greek-born [Vicky
Leandros](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vicky_Leandros "wikilink") (
winner) and [country](../Page/乡村音乐.md "wikilink") band  featuring
comedian Olli Dittrich.\[23\] However, the spectators chose the
latter.\[24\]

Also in the same year, Anders left BMG and joined  and released his
swing-album ** which was peaking only at No.43.\[25\] In April 2006, his
*The DVD-Collection* arrived in stores, which contained 20 videos of his
solo career as well as some hits taken from [Modern
Talking](../Page/Modern_Talking.md "wikilink") period. In July 2008,
Anders was featured on two of download singles by Sound-Chateau, "Ibiza
Baba Baya" and "For You".\[26\]

Anders announced the German Eurovision voting results once again in
2009. In the Spring of 2009, Anders was featured on the song called "
for which he collaborated with , the single peaked at No.46.\[27\]
Anders released his next solo single, "Why Do You Cry" in 2010 which
peaked at No. 21 on Russian Airplay Chart.\[28\]</ref> In the Spring of
2010, Anders released his next studio album, ** for Russian and CIS
market.\[29\] The album reached the platinum status in Russia for sales
of 650,000 units,\[30\]\[31\] making him the best-selling international
artist in Russia that year.

In 2011, Anders came back collaborating with , best known for his
success with "" with his band  in the 1980s. The duo's first single
"Gigolo" was released on May 27, 2011 in Germany and peaked at No. 40
there.\[32\] "Gigolo" was written and produced by Fahrenkrog-Petersen
and . Their second single, "No More Tears on the Dancefloor", written by
, failed to enter the charts. Their album *Two*, released on June 10,
2011, debuted at No. 11 on the German album charts.\[33\]

In November 2012, Anders released a new solo album called *Christmas for
You*. It includes seasonal hits such as "I'll Be Home for Christmas" and
"It's the Most Wonderful Time of the Year" as well as covers of
well-known classics such as "[Silent
Night](../Page/平安夜_\(歌曲\).md "wikilink")" and
[Wham\!](../Page/威猛樂隊.md "wikilink")'s "[Last
Christmas](../Page/Last_Christmas_\(歌曲\).md "wikilink")".

On August 9, 2013, Anders in collaboration with Persian singer Omid
Soltani released the song "We Are One".\[34\]

On May 19, 2016, Anders released a new song titled "Lunatic".\[35\] He
also announced an upcoming album, *History* to be released on May 27,
2016, including the songs "Lunatic" and "Take the Chance".\[36\]

## 个人生活

Anders' parents are Helga and Peter Weidung. He has one brother, Achim,
and one sister, Tania. He was married to  from 1984\[37\] to 1999.\[38\]
Since July 2000, he has been married to Claudia Hess, with whom he has a
son, Alexander Mick, born 27 June 2002.\[39\]

**新西兰籃球國家隊**（**New Zealand national basketball
team**），是代表[新西兰參加國際籃球賽事的籃球國家隊](../Page/新西兰.md "wikilink")。世錦賽最好成績是2002年第四名。并且在大洋洲籃球錦標賽中经常获得冠軍。篮球队的昵称Tall
Blacks来源于[紐西蘭國家橄欖球隊的暱稱全黑隊](../Page/紐西蘭國家橄欖球隊.md "wikilink")（All Blacks）。

## 历史

The Tall Blacks competed at the [2000 Sydney
Olympics](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2000年夏季奥林匹克运动会 "wikilink") and
finished with a 1–5 record, their only win coming against
[Angola](../Page/安哥拉.md "wikilink") in the playoff for eleventh.

In 2001, they defeated Australia in a three-game series to qualify for
the [2002 FIBA World Championship](../Page/2002年世界篮球锦标赛.md "wikilink")
in [Indianapolis](../Page/印第安納波利斯.md "wikilink"), United States. At the
tournament they finished fourth, after beating [Puerto
Rico](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puerto_Rico_national_basketball_team "wikilink")
in the quarter-finals before losses to [Serbia and
Montenegro](../Page/塞爾維亞國家男子籃球隊.md "wikilink") and
[Germany](../Page/德國國家男子籃球隊.md "wikilink"). Tall Blacks captain [Pero
Cameron](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pero_Cameron "wikilink") was the
only non-[NBA](../Page/NBA.md "wikilink") player named to the
all-tournament team in [Indianapolis](../Page/印第安納波利斯.md "wikilink").

The Tall Blacks qualified for the [2004 Athens
Olympics](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2004年夏季奥林匹克运动会 "wikilink") but
again finished with a 1–5 record and lost to Australia in the playoff
for ninth place. Their most noted moment was on the 7th day of the
games, when they defeated reigning World Champion Serbia and Montenegro
90–87.

### 2006年世锦赛

At the [2006 FIBA World
Championship](../Page/2006年世界籃球錦標賽.md "wikilink"), the
Tall Blacks were not to repeat their fourth-place finish from 2002.
After an 0–3 start, the Tall Blacks rallied into the Second round with
two straight wins to close out the group stage. However, they would fall
in the round of 16 to defending Olympic gold medalists
[Argentina](../Page/阿根廷国家男子篮球队.md "wikilink"), 79–62. After that
disappointment, [Tab
Baldwin](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tab_Baldwin "wikilink") resigned
as the coach of the Tall Blacks and was replaced by [Nenad
Vučinić](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nenad_Vučinić "wikilink"), his
longtime assistant coach.

### 2011年[斯坦科维奇杯](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/斯坦科维奇杯 "wikilink")

The squad competed at the 2011 Boris Stanković Cup in China. They played
3 games against difficult opposition, China, Russia and Angola.

After going down to Russia in the first game by just 3 points, the Tall
Blacks quickly put that behind them to smash China in their second game
which led to a good win against a strong Angolan team.

The Tall Blacks came up against a strong Russian team in the final, but
the Russians were no match for Tall Blacks star shooting guard [Kirk
Penney](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/柯克·佩尼 "wikilink") as he dropped 30
points to give the Tall Blacks the win and the gold medal for 2011.

### Haka

The Tall Blacks perform a traditional [Haka](../Page/哈卡舞.md "wikilink")
(Māori challenge) before every game.

## 著名球员

Probably the most well-known former New Zealand Tall Black player in the
[National Basketball Association](../Page/NBA.md "wikilink") (NBA) is
former [San Antonio Spurs](../Page/圣安东尼奥马刺.md "wikilink") forward [Sean
Marks](../Page/西恩·马克斯.md "wikilink"). Another New Zealand player, former
[University of
Wisconsin](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wisconsin_Badgers_men's_basketball "wikilink")
star [Kirk Penney](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/柯克·佩尼 "wikilink"),
briefly played in the NBA, and later played with top European teams
[Maccabi Tel
Aviv](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maccabi_Tel_Aviv_B.C. "wikilink")
and [Žalgiris](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BC_Žalgiris "wikilink"),
but now plays with [Baloncesto
Fuenlabrada](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baloncesto_Fuenlabrada "wikilink")
in [Spain](../Page/西班牙.md "wikilink") after being released by the [New
Zealand
Breakers](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Zealand_Breakers "wikilink").
In past generations, players such as [Stan
Hill](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stan_Hill "wikilink") and [Glen
Denham](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glen_Denham "wikilink") were well
revered and respected players who were the face of New Zealand
basketball.

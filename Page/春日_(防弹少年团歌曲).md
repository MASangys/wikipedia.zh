**春日**是韩国男团[防弹少年团](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/防弹少年团 "wikilink")（BTS）的一首单曲。

## Background

On February 12, 2017, BTS held a comeback preview show\[1\] on
[Naver](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naver "wikilink") V LIVE.\[2\]
Starting on the 18th, they held the [2017 BTS Live Trilogy Episode III:
The Wings
Tour](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_BTS_Live_Trilogy_Episode_III:_The_Wings_Tour "wikilink")
in support of their album, having shows in South Korea, the Americas,
and various Asian countries performing songs from the new release in
front of fans for the first time.\[3\]\[4\]\[5\]\[6\] Later, week long
promotions on broadcasting networks started on February 23, with both
singles "Spring Day" and "Not Today" being performed on
[Mnet](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mnet_\(TV_channel\) "wikilink")'s
*[M\!
Countdown](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/M!_Countdown "wikilink")*\[7\]
even though one of the new songs, "Outro: Wings" was deemed unfit for
broadcast by
[KBS](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System "wikilink").\[8\]

"Spring Day" earned BTS four first-place trophies on local music
programs.\[9\] It debuted at number 15 on the [Billboard Bubbling Under
Hot
100](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_K-pop_songs_on_the_Billboard_charts#Bubbling_Under_Hot_100_Singles_\(Complete\) "wikilink")
and number 1 on the [Billboard World Digital
Songs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_K-pop_songs_on_the_Billboard_charts#World_Digital_Songs_\(Complete\) "wikilink")
chart, selling 14,000 digital downloads; moreover, all four new songs
held down the first four slots\[10\] making BTS the first K-pop act to
do so.

## Composition

"Spring Day" is in the key of Eb major and the main vocal range is
between Bb3-Bb5. It mixes various styles, such as
[pop](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pop_music "wikilink"),
[rap](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip_hop_music "wikilink"),
[world](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_music "wikilink"), and
[K-pop](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/K-pop "wikilink"), and features
backing vocals from singer-songwriter
[Arlissa](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arlissa "wikilink").\[11\] It is
107 beats per minute.\[12\]

The song uses a seasonal metaphor to describe a friend's absence. Member
[J-Hope](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/J-Hope "wikilink") stated the
genre for the song was "a mix of British rock and electronic
sounds".\[13\]

## Music video

The music video for "Spring Day" was uploaded onto the video sharing
website [YouTube](../Page/YouTube.md "wikilink") on February 12,
2017,\[14\] surpassing 9 million views within 24 hours,\[15\] breaking
BTS's previous single "[Blood, Sweat, &
Tears](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blood_Sweat_&_Tears_\(song\) "wikilink")'"
record. A week later, the music video for their follow up single "[Not
Today](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Not_Today_\(BTS_song\) "wikilink")"
overtook "Spring Day"'s record with 10 million views.\[16\] Similarly,
"Spring Day" became the fastest K-pop group music video to reach 20
million views, achieving the feat in less than four days; "Not Today"
bested it in less than three.<ref>\*

  - </ref> Many fans and media outlets rumored the music video was
    inspired by the [Sewol Ferry
    tragedy](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinking_of_MV_Sewol "wikilink").\[17\]
    It currently has over 205 million views on YouTube. Kultscene
    described the music video as "aesthetically pleasing,... beautifully
    shot and well thought out", with references made to movies and
    books, like Snowpiercer and Omelas, which "added layers to the
    video".\[18\] Similarities to the
    [1974](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/1974 "wikilink") [Ursula K. Le
    Guin](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursula_K._Le_Guin "wikilink")
    novella [The Ones Who Walk Away From
    Omelas](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Ones_Who_Walk_Away_From_Omelas "wikilink")
    are discussed widely among fans. The novella tells the story of a
    utopian city called Omelas, whose citizens’ happiness depends on the
    ongoing suffering of a single child. The video suggests through
    imagery including the name Omelas, that the members are among those
    who, once enlightened to the suffering, walk away from Omelas.\[19\]

The music video was directed by Choi YongSeok of Lumpens.\[20\] Other
key personnel working on staff are Lee Wonju serving as the assistant
director, Nam Hyunwoo the director of photography, Song Hyunsuk working
the gaffer, Emma Sungeun Kim as the producer, and Park Jinsil the art
director.\[21\]

## Reception

*Billboard*'s Tamar Herman wrote an article on the repackaged album *You
Never Walk Alone*, describing the single "Spring Day" as an "alt hip-hop
song infused with rock instrumentals and EDM synths" combining the rap
elements for which BTS has become known with "dreamy vocals and yearning
lyrics." Herman calls "Not Today" the dual single counterpart to "Spring
Day", a power anthem for "all the underdogs in the world."\[22\] "Spring
Day" ranked number one on [Dazed
Digital](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dazed_Digital "wikilink")'s "20
Best K-Pop Songs of 2017" list, which described the song as "an
intelligent, compelling and elegantly restrained study of loss and
longing" that "deliberately avoids cliché pomp and drama".\[23\]

"Spring Day" is the longest charting song on
[Melon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melon_\(online_music_service\) "wikilink")
by an idol, as of July 10, 2018 charting for seventy-three weeks and
counting. The prior song that charted the longest was BTS' [Blood Sweat
&
Tears](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blood_Sweat_&_Tears_\(song\) "wikilink").\[24\]
Upon release the song in South Korea became BTS' first "roof hit" and
remained number one in real time on MelOn for twenty-four hours. It also
ranked number one on eight other South Korean portals.\[25\]

The song is the 6th most watched K-pop video of 2017.\[26\]

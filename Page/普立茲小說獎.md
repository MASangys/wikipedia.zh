**普立茲小說獎**（**Pulitzer Prize for Fiction**，最初叫**Pulitzer Prize for the Novel**）是根據美籍[匈牙利人](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/匈牙利人 "wikilink")[約瑟夫·普立茲](../Page/約瑟夫·普立茲.md "wikilink")的遺願設立的[普立茲獎的其中一個獎](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/普立茲獎 "wikilink")，只有美國公民有資格競逐這個獎項。普立茲小說獎從1917年開始頒發，1948年才改稱現有的英文名字。

## 歷屆得獎名單

至今共有三位作家兩次贏得普立茲小說獎：[布斯·塔金頓](../Page/布斯·塔金頓.md "wikilink")、[威廉·福克納](../Page/威廉·福克納.md "wikilink")及[約翰·厄普代克](../Page/約翰·厄普代克.md "wikilink")。

The fiction jury had recommended the 1957 award to Elizabeth Spencer's *The Voice at the Back Door*, but the Pulitzer board, which has sole discretion for awarding the prize, made no award. **Source**: McDowell, Edwin. ["Publishing: Pulitzer Controversies"](http://www.nytimes.com/1984/05/11/books/publishing-pulitzer-controversies.html). The New York Times, 11 May 1984: C26.
2.  The three novels the Pulitzer committee put forth for consideration to the Pulitzer board were: *[Losing Battles](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Losing_Battles "wikilink")* by Eudora Welty; *Mr. Sammler's Planet* by Saul Bellow; and *The Wheel of Love* by Joyce Carol Oates. The board rejected all three and opted for no award. **Source**: Fischer, Heinz-Dietrich. *The Pulizer Prize Archive*, Volume 10, "Novel/Fiction Awards 1917-1994". Munich: K.G. Saur, 1994. LX-LXI.
3.  The fiction jury had unanimously recommended the 1974 award to [Thomas Pynchon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Pynchon "wikilink")'s *[Gravity's Rainbow](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravity's_Rainbow "wikilink")*, but the Pulitzer board, which has sole discretion for awarding the prize, made no award. **Source**: McDowell, Edwin. ["Publishing: Pulitzer Controversies"](http://www.nytimes.com/1984/05/11/books/publishing-pulitzer-controversies.html). *[The New York Times](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times "wikilink")*, 11 May 1984: C26.
4.  The fiction jury had recommended the 1977 award to [Norman MacLean](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norman_MacLean "wikilink")'s *[A River Runs Through It](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_River_Runs_Through_It "wikilink")*, but the Pulitzer board, which has sole discretion for awarding the prize, made no award. **Source**: McDowell, Edwin. ["Publishing: Pulitzer Controversies"](http://www.nytimes.com/1984/05/11/books/publishing-pulitzer-controversies.html). *[The New York Times](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times "wikilink")*, 11 May 1984: C26.

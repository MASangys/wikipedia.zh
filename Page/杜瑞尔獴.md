**杜瑞尔獴**（[学名](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/学名 "wikilink")：**）是一种分布于[马达加斯加](../Page/马达加斯加.md "wikilink")的哺乳动物，分类学上属于[食肉目](../Page/食肉目.md "wikilink")[食蟻狸科](../Page/食蟻狸科.md "wikilink")，和[純色獴共同组成了純色獴属](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/純色獴 "wikilink")。杜瑞尔獴和纯色獴基因上很相似，但是形态上有所区别，因此科学家将其归为不同物种。2004年，科学家观测到杜瑞尔獴的个体，后于2010年将其命名为杜瑞尔獴。杜瑞尔獴仅出现于[阿劳特拉湖](../Page/阿劳特拉湖.md "wikilink")区域。
\<\!-- A small, reddish-brown carnivore, *Salanoia durrelli* is
characterized by broad feet with prominent [Paw\#Common
characteristics](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/肉垫 "wikilink"),
reddish-[buff](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/wikt:buff "wikilink")
underparts, and broad, robust teeth, among other differences from the
brown-tailed mongoose. In the only two weighed specimens, body mass was
. It is a marsh-dwelling animal that may feed on
[crustaceans](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/crustacea "wikilink") and
[mollusks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/mollusk "wikilink"). The Lac
Alaotra area is a threatened ecosystem, and *S. durrelli* may also be
endangered by competition with [introduced
species](../Page/外來物種.md "wikilink").

## Taxonomy

An individual *Salanoia durrelli* was observed swimming in 2004 by the
[Durrell Wildlife Conservation
Trust](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Durrell_Wildlife_Conservation_Trust "wikilink")
(DWCT) during a survey of [bamboo lemurs](../Page/馴狐猴屬.md "wikilink")
(*Hapalemur*) in the [Lake Alaotra](../Page/阿劳特拉湖.md "wikilink") area,
the largest [wetlands](../Page/湿地.md "wikilink") of Madagascar. The
animal was captured, photographed, and then released, but examination of
the photograph showed that it could not be identified with any known
species of Malagasy carnivoran (family
[Eupleridae](../Page/食蟻狸科.md "wikilink")). Therefore, two
specimens were caught in 2005 by the DWCT. One was killed to facilitate
additional morphological comparisons.\[1\] In 2010, it was formally
described as *Salanoia durrelli* in a paper by conservationist Joanna
Durbin and a team of scientists from the [Climate, Community &
Biodiversity
Alliance](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate,_Community_&_Biodiversity_Alliance "wikilink"),
[Nature
Heritage](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nature_Heritage "wikilink"), the
[Natural History Museum, London](../Page/自然史博物馆_\(伦敦\).md "wikilink"),
[Conservation
International](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/保護國際 "wikilink"),
and the DWCT.\[2\] The [specific name
(zoology)](../Page/种加词.md "wikilink"), *durrelli*, honors [Gerald
Durrell](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gerald_Durrell "wikilink"), a
noted conservationist and the founder of the DWCT.\[3\] Previously,
local villagers had already reported the presence of a small carnivoran
at Alaotra, and it was speculated that the animal was the closely
related [brown-tailed
mongoose](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/純色獴 "wikilink") (*Salanoia
concolor*).\[4\]

*Salanoia durrelli* was placed in the genus
*[Salanoia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salanoia "wikilink")*, which
previously included only the brown-tailed mongoose of eastern
Madagascar. *S. durrelli* shows substantial
[morphological](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/morphology_\(anatomy\) "wikilink")
differences from the brown-tailed mongoose, but the [mitochondrial
DNA](../Page/线粒体DNA.md "wikilink") of the two species is very
similar.\[5\] The discoverers chose to recognize the Lac Alaotra
population as a separate species in view of its significant
morphological differentiation. The observed morphological
distinctiveness might be result of
[adaptations](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/適應 "wikilink") to life in
the Alaotra wetlands, similar to the Alaotra bamboo lemur species,
*[Hapalemur
alaotrensis](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hapalemur_alaotrensis "wikilink")*,
which is also recognized as a distinct species

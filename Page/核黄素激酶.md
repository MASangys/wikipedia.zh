**核黄素激酶**（，）是一个[催化](../Page/催化.md "wikilink")以下[化学反应](../Page/化学反应.md "wikilink")的[酶](../Page/酶.md "wikilink")：

  -
    ATP + 核黄素 \(\rightleftharpoons\) ADP + FMN

该[酶](../Page/酶.md "wikilink")催化的反应的[底物为](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/酶底物_\(生物学\) "wikilink")[ATP和](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATP "wikilink")[核黄素](../Page/核黄素.md "wikilink")，[产物是](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/产物_\(化学\) "wikilink")[ADP和](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/ADP "wikilink")[黄素单核苷酸](../Page/黄素单核苷酸.md "wikilink")（FMN）。

但是，在[古菌核黄素激酶](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/CTP依赖核黄素激酶 "wikilink")（）中，常使用[CTP而非ATP作为反应底物](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/CTP "wikilink")，催化如下反应：

  -
    CTP + 核黄素 \(\rightleftharpoons\) CDP + FMN \[1\]

核黄素激酶也在许多细菌中发现，具有类似的功能，但存在若干数量的氨基酸不同。

## 反应

[150px](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/file:Riboflavin2.svg "wikilink") + XTP → [150px](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/file:Riboflavin-5'-phosphat.svg "wikilink") + XDP

Riboflavin is converted into catalytically active cofactors (FAD and FMN) by the actions of riboflavin kinase (), which converts it into FMN, and FAD synthetase (), which adenylates FMN to FAD. Eukaryotes usually have two separate enzymes, while most prokaryotes have a single bifunctional protein that can carry out both catalyses, although exceptions occur in both cases. While eukaryotic monofunctional riboflavin kinase is orthologous to the [bifunctional prokaryotic enzyme](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryotic_riboflavin_biosynthesis_protein "wikilink"),\[2\] the monofunctional FAD synthetase differs from its prokaryotic counterpart, and is instead related to the PAPS-reductase family.\[3\] The bacterial FAD synthetase that is part of the bifunctional enzyme has remote similarity to nucleotidyl transferases and, hence, it may be involved in the adenylylation reaction of FAD synthetases.\[4\]

This enzyme belongs to the family of [transferases](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/transferase "wikilink"), to be specific, those transferring phosphorus-containing groups ([phosphotransferases](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/phosphotransferase "wikilink")) with an alcohol group as acceptor. The systematic name of this enzyme class is **ATP:riboflavin 5'-phosphotransferase**. This enzyme is also called **flavokinase**. This enzyme participates in [riboflavin metabolism](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/riboflavin_metabolism "wikilink").

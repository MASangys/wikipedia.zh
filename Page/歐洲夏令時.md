**歐洲夏令時**是為了利用季節性的日光而在春季開始提前一個小時的作息方法。此方法在全歐洲除冰島外的所有國家實行（冰島全年實行[格林尼治時間](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/格林尼治時間 "wikilink")）。在歐盟國家和其他一些非欧盟国家中，此方法實行時期從每年三月份的最後一個周日1:00（格林尼治時間）開始，至十月份最後一個周日 1:00（格林尼治時間）結束。

## 历史

在历史上，欧洲的不同国家采用过各种不同的夏令时制度，但这给交通、通讯和其他活动带来了不便。二十世纪八十年代，[欧洲共同体](../Page/欧洲共同体.md "wikilink")开始发布关于夏令时制度的指令，要求其成员国通过立法来确定夏令时的开始和结束日期。

自1981年起，每一个指令中都规定了统一的夏令时开始时间（在3月最后一个周日的格林尼治时间1:00将时间调前1小时），但是结束的时间并不一致。1981年和1982年的结束日期为10月的第四个周日；1983年，除[欧洲西部时区外的所有欧洲时区均将结束日期改为](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/欧洲西部时间 "wikilink")9月的最后一个周日；1996年，所有欧洲时区均把结束日期改为10月的第四个周日。1998年，这一日期再一次被修改为10月的最后一个周日。\[1\]目前具有效力的第九条指令，则将这一时间范围定为永久有效。\[2\]

## 未來發展

There were proposals in 2015 and 2016 from members of the European Parliament to abolish summer time observance, but the [European Commission](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/歐洲聯盟委員會 "wikilink") did not at that time put forward proposals to be considered, saying it had not found conclusive evidence in favour of a change, and [member states](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/歐盟成員國 "wikilink") were divided. It did however note that a cost would be incurred if harmonisation between member states' summer time rules was lost.\[5\]\[6\] In 2017 the Finnish and Lithuanian parliaments both voted in favour of proposals calling on the EU to reconsider daylight saving, with similar criticism from Poland and Sweden. The European Commission at the time was reviewing the practice.\[7\]\[8\]

On 8 February 2018, the European Parliament voted to ask the European Commission to re-evaluate DST in Europe.\[9\] After a web survey showing high support for not switching clocks twice annually,\[10\] on 12 September 2018 the European Commission decided to propose that an end be put to seasonal clock changes (repealing Directive 2000/84/EC).\[11\] In order for this to be valid, the  must be followed, mainly that the [Council of the European Union](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/歐洲聯盟委員會 "wikilink") and the European Parliament must both approve the proposal.

Under the proposal, member countries were expected to decide by 31 March 2019 which time they would observe year round. This was however considered a fairly tight timescale by many.\[12\]\[13\] The Internet Assigned Numbers Authority, which coordinates changes to the time zone database used by most computers and smartphones, notes that "With less than a year's notice there is a good chance that some computer-based clocks will operate incorrectly after the change, due to delays in propagating updates to software and data."\[14\] The airline industry points out the complexity of revising all airline schedules, particularly in terms of ensuring slot availability on flights outside the EU, and recommends keeping the status quo or deferring the change until at least 2021.\[15\] An informal meeting of EU transport ministers on 29 October 2018 suggested that many member states would not support the "unrealistic" timetable and that implementation could be pushed back to 2021.\[16\]

Discussions have shown support for year-round "winter time" in e.g. Denmark, the Netherlands ([UTC+01:00](../Page/UTC+01:00.md "wikilink")), and Finland ([UTC+02:00](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/UTC+02:00 "wikilink")) while permanent summer time was supported in Portugal ([UTC+01:00](../Page/UTC+01:00.md "wikilink")), Poland ([UTC+02:00](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/UTC+02:00 "wikilink")), and Cyprus ([UTC+03:00](../Page/UTC+03:00.md "wikilink")).\[17\] Spanish opinion is split on the matter; the mainland largely favours shifting to [UTC](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/UTC "wikilink"), while the [Balearic Islands](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balearic_Islands "wikilink") would prefer to remain on [UTC+01:00](../Page/UTC+01:00.md "wikilink") and the [Canary Islands](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canary_Islands "wikilink") on [UTC-01:00](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/UTC-1 "wikilink").\[18\] The United Kingdom is due to [leave the EU](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brexit "wikilink") before the reform becomes effective; any new EU directive would apply during the transition period (if any) but thereafter the UK could choose to make its own arrangements.\[19\]\[20\] If the UK were thus to continue observing summer/winter time, [Northern Ireland](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Ireland "wikilink") would have a one-hour time difference for half the year either with the rest of [Ireland](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ireland "wikilink") or with the rest of the UK.\[21\] The Irish government is to hold a public consultation on the EU proposal;\[22\] , the UK Government "has no plans" to end daylight saving.\[23\]

On 4 March 2019, the European Parliament Transport and Tourism Committee approved the Commission's proposal by 23 votes to 11. The start date will however be postponed until 2021 to ensure a smooth transition and the Commission must ensure that countries' decisions to retain winter or summer time are coordinated and do not disrupt the internal market.\[24\] This decision was confirmed by the full European Parliament on 26 March; it must now be approved by the Council of Ministers.\[25\] Under the draft directive, member states would be able to choose whether to remain on their current summer time, in which case the last transition would be on the last Sunday of March 2021, or their current winter time, which would take permanent effect from the last Sunday of October 2021.\[26\]

A consultation by the Irish government found that 80% of those surveyed would not support any measure that resulted in different time zones between Northern Ireland and the Republic. In July 2019, Ireland announced its opposition to the proposed directive and intends to lobby other EU states on the issue. A qualified majority of 55% of member states representing at least 65% of the European population is required for the Council of Ministers to implement a directive.\[27\] In the UK, the [House of Lords](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Lords "wikilink") launched a new inquiry in July 2019 to consider the implications of the European changes, explore the preparations that should be made and the factors that should inform the UK's response.\[28\]

## 各地情况

当夏令时开始实施时，欧洲绝大部分地区都会在当地的[时区](../Page/时区.md "wikilink")之前加入“夏令”二字。例如在[UTC+1时区](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/UTC+1 "wikilink")，[欧洲中部时间](../Page/欧洲中部时间.md "wikilink")变成了[欧洲中部夏令时间](../Page/欧洲中部夏令时间.md "wikilink")（[UTC+2](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/UTC+2 "wikilink")）。

在[英国](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/英国 "wikilink")，实施夏令时后的当地时间被称作[英国夏令时间](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/英国夏令时间 "wikilink")（**B**ritish **S**ummer **T**ime，缩写BST），而一年中的其他时候应英国当地时间一般被称作[格林尼治标准时间](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/格林尼治标准时间 "wikilink")（**G**reenwich **M**ean **Time**，缩写GMT）。

在[爱尔兰](../Page/爱尔兰.md "wikilink")，夏令时时段中的当地时间是“IST”，也就是“爱尔兰标准时间（**I**rish **S**tandard **T**ime）”而非“爱尔兰夏令时间（**I**rish **S**ummer **T**ime）”。爱尔兰的官方使用的时间是[欧洲中部时间](../Page/欧洲中部时间.md "wikilink")（UTC+1），但实际上这仅仅在使用夏令时，时钟比冬季快1小时的时候才有效（欧洲中部时间这时候是欧洲中部夏令时间）。不适用夏令时的时候，爱尔兰的时间实际上是[欧洲西部时间](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/欧洲西部时间 "wikilink")，也就是[协调世界时](../Page/协调世界时.md "wikilink")或者[格林尼治平均时间](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/格林尼治平均时间 "wikilink")。

### 西欧

除冰岛外的所有西欧国家，不论是否加入了欧盟，都使用欧盟规定的夏令时开始和结束时间。

### 俄罗斯和白俄罗斯

[俄罗斯](../Page/俄罗斯.md "wikilink")和[白俄罗斯](../Page/白俄罗斯.md "wikilink")也使用夏令时制度，并与欧盟采用相同的开始和结束日期（即3月和10月的最后一个周日）。但是，与欧洲其他国家有明显区别的是，夏令时开始时，俄罗斯各个时区均在[当地时间的凌晨](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/当地时间 "wikilink")2:00，而非统一的格林尼治时间1:00。而夏令时结束时，将时钟拨慢一小时的时间则是各个时区的当地时间凌晨3:00。 俄罗斯自2011年3月27日开始永久使用夏令时，把时间拨快一小时，不再调回。在总统德米特里·梅德韦杰夫支持下，俄罗斯各时区当地时间下午2时把时间永久调快一小时，使用夏令时。例如，莫斯科时间与格林尼治时间将永久相差4小时。按法新社的说法，由于苏联1930年要求全国统一把时间调快一小时，这一举措意味着俄罗斯各地时间将比原来快两小时。苏联1981年出于节能考虑引入夏令时制度，每年3月最后一个星期日开始，至10月最后一个星期日结束。

### 土耳其

土耳其同样根据欧盟的有关规定实施夏令时制度。2016年10月30日夏令时结束后，不往回拨1个小时，相当于结束了夏令时并且将时区向前挪了1小时。

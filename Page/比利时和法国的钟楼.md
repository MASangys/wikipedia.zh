一个出自56座**[比利时和](../Page/比利时.md "wikilink")[法国的](../Page/法国.md "wikilink")[钟楼](../Page/钟楼.md "wikilink")**（Belfry）的无与伦比的“合奏团”被[UNESCO列入了](../Page/UNESCO.md "wikilink")[世界遗产名录](../Page/世界遗产名录.md "wikilink")。它们被认为是[历史上佛兰德及邻近地区的公民自封建和宗教影响下开始走向独立的建筑表现](../Page/佛兰德伯国.md "wikilink")，此种独立导向一定程度的地方民主，在人类史上具有重大意义。

[UNESCO](../Page/UNESCO.md "wikilink")1999年将[比利时](../Page/比利时.md "wikilink")32座钟楼列入了其**[佛兰德和](../Page/佛兰德.md "wikilink")[瓦隆的钟楼](../Page/瓦隆.md "wikilink")**（Belfries
of Flanders and
Wallonia）项下的名单。2005年，[比利时](../Page/比利时.md "wikilink")[瓦隆](../Page/瓦隆.md "wikilink")[让布卢的钟楼以及](../Page/让布卢.md "wikilink")[法国最北部](../Page/法国.md "wikilink")[北部-加来海峡和](../Page/北部-加来海峡.md "wikilink")[皮卡第](../Page/皮卡第.md "wikilink")[大区的](../Page/大区.md "wikilink")23座钟楼也被列入更名后的该项目的名单中。一个显而易见的遗漏是[布鲁塞尔市政厅的钟楼](../Page/布鲁塞尔市政厅.md "wikilink")，其实它已经随[布鲁塞尔大广场被列入了世界遗产名录](../Page/布鲁塞尔大广场.md "wikilink")。

除了市民（civic）钟楼，或例如市政厅之类的明显提供类似服务的建筑外，该名录还包括了亦作瞭望塔和警钟塔使用的宗教建筑：[安特卫普的](../Page/安特卫普.md "wikilink")[圣母主教座堂](../Page/圣母主教座堂_\(安特卫普\).md "wikilink")，[梅赫伦的](../Page/梅赫伦.md "wikilink")[圣路茂狄主教座堂](../Page/圣路茂狄主教座堂.md "wikilink")，以及位于[祖时莱厄夫的圣良德教堂](../Page/祖时莱厄夫.md "wikilink")——所有这三座均位于[比利时](../Page/比利时.md "wikilink")[法兰德斯区](../Page/法兰德斯区.md "wikilink")。

## 脚注

[Category:比利时和法国的钟楼](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category:比利时和法国的钟楼 "wikilink")

1.  Quote from [external link](../Page/#External_links.md "wikilink")
    *Detailed argumentation for list ID 943/943bis, UNESCO Website*:
    "The Hôtel de Ville in Antwerpen (1564) is an excellent example of
    the transposition of Renaissance principles in the central risalith
    with superposed diminishing registers flanked by obelisks and
    scrollwork and finished with a pediment, reiterating the theme of
    the central belfry." – *Hôtel de Ville* is French for 'City Hall',
    *Antwerpen* is the native name of 'Antwerp' in Dutch.

2.  UNESCO states, inappropriately in French: ID 943-016 *Tour de
    Saint-Rombaut* ; in native Dutch language this is
    *Sint-Romboutstoren* which is the main tower of the cathedral, once
    also used as a watchtower against fires.

3.  UNESCO states, inappropriately in French: ID 943-015 *Ancienne Halle
    avec Beffroi* ; in native Dutch language this is *Oude \[or:
    Voormalige\] Halle met Belfort*. This 14th century Cloth Hall with
    never to its designed height built Belfry – both hardly ever used
    for the intended purposes – with more recent adjacent buildings,
    constitute the present-day City Hall.

4.  The belfry is known as *Halletoren*, because of an adjacent Cloth
    Hall that no longer exists; the tower is now free-standing.

5.  The belfry is known as *Hallentoren* or Tower of the Halls, plural:
    of the two adjacent wings or halls, only one remains, hence Cloth
    Hall, singular.

6.  The city centre's *Landhuis* (literally: 'country-house') was once
    the seat of the *kasselrij* or *burggraafschap* (viscounty)
    [Veurne-Ambacht](../Page/Veurne-Ambacht.md "wikilink"), serving the
    countryside; here as opposed to the adjacent *Stadhuis* (literally:
    'city-house' though always meaning the City Hall) serving the city.
    The *Landhuis* later became the Court of Justice and recently a
    place for cultural purposes, e.g. exhibitions, dance acts, concerts,
    etc.

7.  The name *Mammelokker* (assumedly: 'Allurer of breasts') for the
    guard house at the part of the Cloth Hall that once served as a
    prison, refers to the [story of a
    prisoner](../Page/Belfry_of_Ghent#Cloth_hall_and_Mammelokker.md "wikilink").

    *Beffroi de l'église Saint-Eloi* – further reading from other

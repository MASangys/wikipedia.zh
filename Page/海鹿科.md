**海鹿科**（學名：）是**海鹿總科**（學名：，又名**海兔超科**）之下唯一的一個[科](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/科_\(生物\) "wikilink")。與其牠海蛞蝓相比，海鹿的體型相比較大。牠們的頭小，有著又長又圓的身體，還有長長的一對。這些物種都是一些沒有螺殻的「海螺」，一般「海鹿」會用來叫體型相對大很多的物種，而體型較小的會叫作「海兔」，但也有文獻不論大小都稱呼為「海兔」。在這些物種當中，以[加州黑海兔](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/加州黑海兔 "wikilink")（*[Aplysia vaccaria](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aplysia_vaccaria "wikilink")*）的體型最大，也是所有腹足綱物種中體型最大的一種，經常在內容農場的「海怪」文章提及。

## 型態描述

[Aplysia_parvula_2.png](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Aplysia_parvula_2.png "fig:Aplysia_parvula_2.png")'' showing general anatomy of sea hares.\]\]  Members of the Aplysiidae have an atrophied inner shell (in contrast with the [nudibranchs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/nudibranch "wikilink"), which have no shell at all). In *Aplysia* and *Syphonota*, this shell is a soft flattened plate over the [visceral](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/visceral "wikilink") rear end, where it is fully or partially enclosed in the [mantle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mantle_\(mollusc\) "wikilink") skin. In *Dolabella auricularia*, the shell is ear-shaped. The shell is only present in the larval stage of the two genera *Bursatella* and *Stylocheilus*, and on this basis they have been grouped into the subfamily Dolabriferinae.\[1\]

They are rather large animals. Their length can reach up to 75 cm (*Aplysia vaccaria*), and they can weigh well over 2 kg. They are [cosmopolitan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cosmopolitan_distribution "wikilink") and found in temperate and tropical seas, inhabiting shallow coastal areas and sheltered bays, thick with vegetation.

The Aplysiidae are [herbivorous](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbivore "wikilink"), eating a variety of red, green or brown [algae](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/alga "wikilink") and [eelgrass](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seagrass "wikilink"). Their color is diet-derived from the pigments of the algae. They concentrate the toxins found on algae. ## Defenses

Some species spout ink when disturbed or attacked, and they may also swim (rather than crawl) away, using their broad wing-like flaps or [parapodia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/parapodia "wikilink"). The ink is extracted from their algal food, rather than being synthesized.\[2\]

Sea hares have two main secretory glands in their mantle cavity.

  - purple gland: lying on top of the mantle cavity, above the gill. It gives off a red or purple fluid, or, in some species, a white ink.
  - opaline gland: situated on the floor of the mantle cavity, beneath the gill. It gives off a white opaque secretion.

The genus *[Aplysia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aplysia "wikilink")* has proved useful as a model in [neurobiology](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/neurobiology "wikilink") for the study of [electrical synapses](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/electrical_synapse "wikilink"), which mediate the release of the clouds of ink.\[3\]

## Mating habits

Sea hares are [hermaphrodites](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/hermaphrodite "wikilink"), with fully functional male and female reproductive organs. The [penis](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/penis "wikilink") is on the right side of the head while the [vagina](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/vagina "wikilink") is situated in the mantle cavity, beneath the shell, deep down between the parapodia. It is therefore physically impossible for mating partners to act as both male and female at the same time.

They have unusual mating habits: they can mate in pairs with one acting as a male, the other as a female, but they commonly occur in quite crowded numbers during the mating season, and this often leads to chains of three or more sea hares mating together. The one at the front acts as a female and the one at the back as a male. The animal(s) in between are acting as both males and females, in other words, the animal receiving sperm passes its own sperm to a third sea hare.

## 分類學

2004年的一份研究文獻\[4\]顯示海鹿科是一個[單系群](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/單系群 "wikilink")，由兩個支序組成：

  - Aplysiinae
  - Dolabellinae + Dolabriferinae + Notarchinae.

The authority of this family is still somewhat in dispute. The family was incorrectly originally spelled as Laplysiana. This was a Latinized form of the original common name "les Laplysiens" as described in *Philosophie zoologique*, 1:320 by [Lamarck](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Lamarck "wikilink") in 1809.\[5\] [Rafinesque](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constantine_Samuel_Rafinesque "wikilink") introduced the new name Laplysinia in 1815. In 2001 Bouchet & Rocroi advocated the attribution of the name Aplysiidae to Lamarck.\[6\]

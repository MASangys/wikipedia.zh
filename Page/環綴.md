**環綴**（、前後綴、接周辭、兩面接辭）為[詞綴](../Page/詞綴.md "wikilink")之一種，就是將一[語素](../Page/語素.md "wikilink")放置於另一語素的周圍。環綴與前綴對比，附加於字詞的開頭；與後綴對比，即附於字詞末尾；與中綴對比，則置於字詞中間。另請參閱[插音](../Page/插音.md "wikilink")(epenthesis)。環綴常見於[南島語系](../Page/南島語系.md "wikilink")\[1\]及[格魯吉亞語等語言](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/格魯吉亞語 "wikilink")。\[2\]

### 日耳曼語言

[德語](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/德語 "wikilink")[過去分詞可能為最廣人知的環綴](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/過去分詞 "wikilink")，比如"規則動詞"*ge〉〈t*。以動詞*spielen*為例，比如其分詞為*gespielt*。[荷蘭語](../Page/荷蘭語.md "wikilink")也有類似的系統(在這種例子的變化情形為：*spelen*
→ *gespeeld*）。

### 東亞語言

在日本，一些語言學家認為 〉〈 及 〉〈 為尊稱性的環綴\[3\]；比如  → お〉〈になる（敬語）、お〉読み〈する（謙稱語）。

### 南島語

[馬來語有八個環綴](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/馬來語 "wikilink")，分別是：

  -
    *perkan*,
    *peri*,
    *beran*,
    *kean*,
    *penan*,
    *peran*,
    *senya*,
    *kei*. \<\!--

For example, a circumfix can be added to the root *adil* "fair" to form
*keadilan* "fairness".\[4\]

### 其它語言

In most North African and some Levantine [varieties of
Arabic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/varieties_of_Arabic "wikilink"),
verbs are negated by placing the circumfix *maš* around the verb
together with all its prefixes and suffixed direct- and indirect-object
pronouns. For example,
[Egyptian](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_Arabic "wikilink")
*bitgibuhum-laha* "you bring them to her" is negated as *maš* "you don't
bring them to her".

In [Berber
languages](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berber_languages "wikilink")
the feminine is marked with the circumfix *tt*. The word *afus* "hand"
becomes *tt*. In
[Kabyle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kabyle_language "wikilink"), *θθ*
"bride" derives from *issli* "groom". From bni, to build, with *tt* we
obtain *tbnit* "thou buildest".

In [Hebrew](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_language "wikilink"),
*magdelet* "magnifier", for example, the root is *gdl* "big" (in the
H-stem *hagdel* "to enlarge") and the circumfix is *met*.

Negation in
[Guaraní](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guaraní_language "wikilink")
is also done with circumfixes, *ndi* and *ndmo'ãi* for
[future](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/future_tense "wikilink")
negations.

In some [Slavic
languages](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavic_languages "wikilink"),
and in
[Hungarian](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_language "wikilink"),
the [superlative](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/superlative "wikilink")
of adjectives is formed with a circumfix. For example, in
[Czech](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czech_language "wikilink"), the
circumfix *nejší* is used – *mladý* "young" becomes *nejmladší*
"youngest". The corresponding circumfix in Hungarian is *legbb*, as in
*legnagyobb* "biggest", from *nagy* "big". (In both cases, the
[comparative](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/comparative "wikilink") form
is produced using the suffix without the prefix: *mladší* "younger";
*nagyobb* "bigger".)

In
[Gurmanchema](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gurmanchema_language "wikilink"),
noun classes are indicated by circumfix. --\>

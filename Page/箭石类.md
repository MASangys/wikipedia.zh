**箭石類**動物（[學名](../Page/學名.md "wikilink")：），舊作**箭石下綱**，是一種已經滅絕[頭足綱生物的分支](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/頭足綱 "wikilink")，生活在[泥盆紀至](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/泥盆紀 "wikilink")[白堊紀之間](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/白堊紀 "wikilink")。箭石在許多方面都與現代的[魷魚相當接近](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/魷魚 "wikilink")\[1\]，而且與現代的[烏賊關係密切](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/烏賊 "wikilink")。箭石也擁有[墨囊](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/墨囊 "wikilink")\[2\]，不過牠們有10條大約相同長度的[觸腕](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/觸腕 "wikilink")，其中並沒有特別長的觸腕\[3\]。

箭石的學名"belemnoid"是來自於[希臘文βέλεμνον](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/希臘文 "wikilink")（*belemnon*，意指「飛鏢或箭頭」），以及另一希臘字είδος（*eidos*，意指「形式」），即「箭頭形狀」的動物。\[4\]

箭石類動物包括了[箭石目](../Page/箭石目.md "wikilink")、[閉箭石目](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/閉箭石目 "wikilink")、[溝箭石目和](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/溝箭石目 "wikilink")[Diplobelida](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplobelida "wikilink")。

## 演化歷史

箭石類在[侏羅紀與白堊紀時數量相當多](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/侏羅紀 "wikilink")，所以牠們的化石在[中生代的海洋岩層中相當豐富](../Page/中生代.md "wikilink")，經常伴隨著[菊石類出現](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/菊石類 "wikilink")。不過箭石也跟菊石一起在白堊紀末期滅絕了。箭石是由[桿石於泥盆紀時期演化而來](../Page/桿石目.md "wikilink")，從[密西西比紀](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/密西西比紀 "wikilink")（或[早石炭世](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/早石炭世 "wikilink")）至[白堊紀末之間的地層中可以發現良好的箭石化石](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/白堊紀 "wikilink")，其他頭足類如[桿菊石](../Page/桿菊石.md "wikilink")、[鸚鵡螺類及](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/鸚鵡螺亞綱 "wikilink")[稜菊石類等也可發現](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/稜菊石目 "wikilink")。

## 解剖學

Belemnoids possessed a central
[phragmocone](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/phragmocone "wikilink") made
of [aragonite](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/aragonite "wikilink") and
with negative buoyancy.\[5\] To the rear of the creature was a heavy
[calcite](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/calcite "wikilink") guard whose
main role appears to have been to counterbalance the front (towards the
head) of the organism; it positions the centre of mass below the centre
of buoyancy, increasing the stability of the swimming organism.\[6\] The
guard would account for between a third and a fifth of the length of the
complete organism, arms included.\[7\]

Like some modern squid, belemnoid arms carried a series of small hooks
for grabbing [prey](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/prey "wikilink").
Belemnoids were efficient
[carnivores](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/carnivore "wikilink") that
caught small [fish](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/fish "wikilink") and
other marine animals with their arms and ate them with their [beak-like
jaws](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cephalopod_beak "wikilink"). In
turn, belemnites appear to have formed part of the diet of marine
[reptiles](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/reptile "wikilink") such as
[Ichthyosaurs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ichthyosaur "wikilink"),
whose fossilized
[stomachs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/stomach "wikilink") frequently
contain
[phosphatic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phosphate_minerals "wikilink")
hooks from the arms of cephalopods.

## 生態學

Belemnoids were effectively neutrally buoyant, and swam in near-shore to
mid-shelf oceans.\[8\] Their fins could be used to their advantage in
all water speeds; in a gentle current they could be flapped for
propulsion; in a stronger current they could be held erect to generate
lift; and when swimming rapidly by jet propulsion they could be tucked
in to the body for streamlining.\[9\]

hastatus*）護甲化石，其表面的[藤壺](../Page/藤壺.md "wikilink")（*Rogerella*）顯現出牠死後軀體在海底待了很長一段時間。\]\]

箭石形成化石的部份大多是其結實的「護甲」，通常呈拉長的子彈形。(though in some subgroups the rostrum may
only exist as a thin layer coating the phragmocone).The hollow region at
the front of the guard is termed the *alveolus*
護甲後端的中穴內包覆或連接著[圓錐狀的](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/圓錐 "wikilink")，而閉錐就是箭石調節浮力的氣室，大多數箭石標本的護甲末端中穴與閉錐部份常會被壓碎，沒有完整的保存下來，只有保存較好的標本能保留下來。Projecting
forwards from one side of the phragmocone is the thin *pro-ostracum*.

While belemnoid phragmocones are
[homologous](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homology_\(biology\) "wikilink")
with the shells of other
[cephalopods](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cephalopods "wikilink") and
are similarly composed of
[aragonite](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/aragonite "wikilink"),
belemnoid guards are evolutionarily novel and are composed of
[calcite](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/calcite "wikilink") or
[aragonite](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/aragonite "wikilink"), thus
tending to preserve well. Broken guards show a structure of radiating
calcite fibers and may also display concentric [growth
rings](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/growth_rings "wikilink").

The guard, phragmocone and pro-ostracum were all internal to the living
creature, forming a skeleton which was enclosed entirely by soft
muscular tissue. The original living creature would have been larger
than the fossilized shell, with a long streamlined body and prominent
eyes. The guard would have been in place toward the rear of the
creature, with the phragmocone behind the head and the pointed end of
the guard facing backward.

The guard of the belemnoid *[Megateuthis
gigantea](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megateuthis_gigantea "wikilink")*,
which is found in
[Europe](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "wikilink") and
[Asia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "wikilink"), can measure up to
 in length, giving the living animal an estimated length of .

Very exceptional belemnoid specimens have been found showing the
preserved soft parts of the animal. Elsewhere in the fossil record,
bullet-shaped belemnite guards are locally found in such profusion that
such deposits are referred to semi-formally as "belemnite battlefields"
(cf. ["orthocone
orgies"](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/orthoceras "wikilink")). It
remains unclear whether these deposits represent post-mating mass death
events, as are common among modern
[cephalopods](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cephalopods "wikilink") and
other
[semelparous](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/semelparous "wikilink")
creatures.

### 雷石

在數百年前的[歐洲](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/歐洲 "wikilink")，尚未有[古生物學概念的時代](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/古生物學 "wikilink")，當時的[英國人認為箭石的化石是](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/英國 "wikilink")[雷打到地面而形成的](../Page/雷.md "wikilink")，所以稱其為「雷石（Thunderstones）」，成為箭石化石的別稱。\[10\]

## 用途

The [stable
isotope](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/isotope_geochemistry "wikilink")
composition of a belemnoid rostrum from the [Peedee
Formation](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peedee_Formation "wikilink")
([Cretaceous](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cretaceous "wikilink"),
southeast USA) has long been used as a global standard (Peedee
Belemnite, "PDB") against which other [isotope
geochemistry](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/isotope_geochemistry "wikilink")
samples are measured, for both [carbon
isotopes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/isotopes_of_carbon "wikilink")
and [oxygen
isotopes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/isotopes_of_oxygen "wikilink").

Some belemnoids (such as *Belemnites* of
[Belemnitida](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belemnitida "wikilink"))
serve as [index
fossils](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/index_fossil "wikilink"),
particularly in the Cretaceous [Chalk
Formation](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chalk_Formation "wikilink") of
Europe, enabling
[geologists](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/geologist "wikilink") to date
the age the
[rocks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/rock_\(geology\) "wikilink") in
which they are found.

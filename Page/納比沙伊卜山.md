**納比沙伊卜山**（，意为先知[舒阿卜之山](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/舒阿卜 "wikilink")）是一座位于[也门](../Page/也门.md "wikilink")[萨那省的山](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/萨那省 "wikilink")。它是也门最高的山，同时也是[阿拉伯半岛最高的山](../Page/阿拉伯半岛.md "wikilink")。

納比沙伊卜山的海拔高度常被说成3760米，但根据（SRTM）以及一些较新的数据，该山的海拔高度应为3666米\[1\]。

***Jabal An-Nabī Shuʿayb*** () is a mountain of the [Haraz
subrange](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jabal_Haraz "wikilink") of the
[Sarawat](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarawat_Mountains "wikilink"),
located in [Sana'a
Governorate](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sana'a_Governorate "wikilink"),
[Yemen](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemen "wikilink"). It is the
highest mountain of the country and the [Arabian
Peninsula](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula "wikilink").\[2\]\[3\]
It is one of the most
[prominent](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/prominence "wikilink") peaks
in the world and the [third most prominent peak in the Middle
East](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Ultras_of_West_Asia "wikilink").

## Description

Its elevation is often reported to be , but this is not supported by
[SRTM](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/SRTM "wikilink") data or more
recent cartographic sources.\[4\] The height of the mountain is , and is
located near the Yemeni capital city of
[Sana'a](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sana'a "wikilink"). Yemen's
second highest peak, [Jabal
Tiyal](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jabal_Tiyal "wikilink"), is nearly
equidistant from the capital as Jabal an-Nabi Shu'aib is. The mountain
may seem like a rocky knoll from observation center like the Sana'a-Al
Hudayda highway, but from its western face it is a massive mountain
rising from about . This side of the mountain halts clouds burdened with
precipitation, causing that side to be relatively fertile. Atop the
mountain is a military post with a radar and the shrine of the [Islamic
*Nabī*](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prophets_and_messengers_in_Islam "wikilink")
([Prophet](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prophet "wikilink"))
[Shu'ayb](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shuaib "wikilink").\[5\]\[6\] It
is relatively difficult to be allowed to its summit, but restrictions
have eased as of late. Although the summit is not snow-capped like its
counterparts in northern
[Lebanon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanon "wikilink") and
[Syria](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syria "wikilink"), there have been
reports of snow on the peak and frost in the winter. Wind speeds are
very high at the summit.

**索拉娜·科斯蒂亚**（，）是[羅馬尼亞](../Page/羅馬尼亞.md "wikilink")女子網球運動員，2006年轉為職業網球員。她的WTA最高單打排名為第21位，現時世界單打排名為第53位，而世界雙打排名為第889位。

## 風格

Cîrstea is a power player. Her groundstrokes are often erratic with an
emphasis on going for a winner leading her to make a high amount of
unforced errors per match but with a high number of winners.\[1\]
According to the *Tennis Spy*, her long stride means she covers ground
while not appearing to move particularly quickly.\[2\] As a doubles
player, she is also a very solid
[volleyer](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/volley_\(tennis\) "wikilink").\[3\]
Her [forehands](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/forehand "wikilink") and
[backhands](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/backhand "wikilink") are
delivered quickly on both sides.\[4\]

## 早年及個人生活

Cîrstea was born to Mihai and Liliana in
[Bucharest](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bucharest "wikilink"),\[5\]
but currently resides in
[Târgoviște](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Târgoviște "wikilink")
where her parents are from.\[6\] She also has a younger brother,
Mihnea.\[7\] Cîrstea was introduced to tennis at the age of four by her
mother.\[8\] Sorana's father owns an ice cream factory in
Târgoviște.\[9\] She has cited [Steffi
Graf](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steffi_Graf "wikilink") and [Roger
Federer](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roger_Federer "wikilink") as her
idols.\[10\] She is currently coached by Marius Comanescu and Andrei
Cociasu.

In 2014, Cîrstea started dating Colombian tennis player [Santiago
Giraldo](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santiago_Giraldo "wikilink").\[11\]
Cîrstea speaks Romanian, English, Spanish, and French.\[12\]

## 職業生涯

### 2005–2006年: Last junior years and turning pro

Cîrstea is a former
[ITF](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Tennis_Federation "wikilink")
Junior Circuit top-ten player, reaching her highest ranking of no. six
on 26 June 2006. Her best results include a title in the 2005 German
Junior Open (Grade 1) defeating Erika Zanchetta 6–2, 6–7, 6–3 in the
final, a tournament she was finalist the year before and a runner-up
place in the 2006 Trofeo Bonfiglio (Grade A) where she lost in the final
to her compatriot [Ioana Raluca
Olaru](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ioana_Raluca_Olaru "wikilink")
after having defeated the then-top ranked world junior player [Anastasia
Pavlyuchenkova](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anastasia_Pavlyuchenkova "wikilink")
in the semifinals. She also made final appearances at the year-ending
ITF Grade 1 juniors tournaments, Eddie Herr International, and Yucatán
World Cup in 2005 and 2006, and in the Opus Nottinghill International in
2006.\[13\]

She turned pro in 2006 and ended her first professionial year as No.353
in WTA Rankings.

### 2007–2008年: First WTA title and reaching Top 40

[Sorana_Cirstea_2008.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Sorana_Cirstea_2008.jpg "fig:Sorana_Cirstea_2008.jpg")
In April 2007, she reached the final of the [Budapest Grand
Prix](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budapest_Grand_Prix "wikilink"), a
Tier III event held in
[Budapest](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budapest "wikilink"), Hungary,
as a qualifier. During the tournament, all of her main draw matches were
pushed to three sets. She beat [Martina
Müller](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martina_Müller_\(tennis\) "wikilink")
in the second round, [Eleni
Daniilidou](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eleni_Daniilidou "wikilink")
in the quarter-finals and [Karin
Knapp](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karin_Knapp "wikilink") in the
semi-finals before losing to [Gisela
Dulko](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gisela_Dulko "wikilink") in the
final. By making it to the final, she became the first
[Romanian](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romania "wikilink") player to
reach a Tour final since [Ruxandra
Dragomir](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruxandra_Dragomir "wikilink") in
June 2000. During her run, she beat two players ranked inside the
[WTA](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Women's_Tennis_Association "wikilink")
top 40. Next month, Cîrstea played in the junior tournament of the [2007
French Open](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2007_French_Open "wikilink")
where she reached the doubles final, along with partner [Alexa
Glatch](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexa_Glatch "wikilink"), losing
to the third seeds [Ksenia
Milevskaya](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ksenia_Milevskaya "wikilink")
and [Urszula
Radwańska](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urszula_Radwańska "wikilink").

In October 2008, Sorana won her first WTA title in
[Tashkent](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_Tashkent_Open "wikilink")
by defeating 4th seed and World no.64 [Sabine
Lisicki](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabine_Lisicki "wikilink") in
three sets. She also won her first two WTA titles in doubles that year.
At the end of 2008, she was No.36 in WTA Rankings (singles) and the best
ranked Romanian woman player in that moment, with only 18 years old.

In doubles, she partnered with Ioana Olaru and reached the

Ladies

Cîrstea began the year as world no. 244. In January she played at
$25,000 ITF tournaments in
[Guaruja](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guaruja "wikilink") and
[Bertioga](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bertioga "wikilink"). In

Maia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beatriz_Haddad_Maia "wikilink")
before losing to [Montserrat
Gonzalez](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montserrat_Gonzalez "wikilink")
in three sets in the final. At Bertioga, she beat [Sandra
Zaniewska](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandra_Zaniewska "wikilink"),
[Victoria
Bosio](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victoria_Bosio "wikilink"), [Jil

[Rebecca

before beating [Catalina
Pella](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalina_Pella "wikilink") in the
final. It was the first tournament win after a period of almost 4 years.

In February, she returned to the WTA Tour, by playing in [Rio de
Janeiro](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Rio_Open "wikilink") after
receiving a wildcard, and here she reached the semifinals, with straight
victories against Brazilian wildcard [Beatriz Haddad
Maia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beatriz_Haddad_Maia "wikilink")
(score 6–1, 6–2), seed no.5 [Polona
Hercog](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polona_Hercog "wikilink") (7–5,
7–6<sup>(7–4)</sup>) and seed no.3 [Danka

6–2), before losing to [Shelby
Rogers](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shelby_Rogers "wikilink") in two
tight sets. These performances made her climb up more than 90 places in
the rankings. Also in February she played at a $25,000 ITF tournament at
[São Paulo](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/São_Paulo "wikilink"). She
defeated [Yvonne
Cavalle-Reimers](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yvonne_Cavalle-Reimers "wikilink"),
[Martina
Caregaro](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martina_Caregaro "wikilink") and
[Jil Teichmann](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jil_Teichmann "wikilink")
en route to the semifinals where she lost to [Sara Sorribes
Tormo](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sara_Sorribes_Tormo "wikilink").

In March she attended the tournament in
[Miami](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Miami_Open "wikilink"),
receiving a wildcard in the qualifying tournament, beating [Kurumi
Nara](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kurumi_Nara "wikilink") before
losing to [Francesca
Schiavone](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francesca_Schiavone "wikilink").
Also in March, Sorana attended the ITF $50,000 tournament at
[Croissy-Beaubourg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croissy-Beaubourg "wikilink").
She defeated [Elitsa
Kostova](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elitsa_Kostova "wikilink"),
[Josephine
Boualem](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josephine_Boualem "wikilink"),
and [Andreea
Mitu](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andreea_Mitu "wikilink") en route to
the semifinals, where shee lost to 2nd seed [Pauline
Parmentier](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pauline_Parmentier "wikilink").

Cîrstea kicked off her clay season in
[Istanbul](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_İstanbul_Cup "wikilink")
and
[Prague](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_J&T_Banka_Prague_Open "wikilink"),
playing the qualifying round in both tournaments. In Istanbul she beat
defending champion [Lesia
Tsurenko](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lesia_Tsurenko "wikilink")
before losing to eventual champion [Çağla
Büyükakçay](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Çağla_Büyükakçay "wikilink").
In Prague, she lost in the first round in three sets against Samantha
Stosur .

In
[Madrid](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Mutua_Madrid_Open "wikilink")-after
receiving a wildcard-she reached the quarterfinals, beating [Jelena
Jankovic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jelena_Jankovic "wikilink"),
[Danka Kovinić](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danka_Kovinić "wikilink")
and [Laura
Siegemund](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laura_Siegemund "wikilink") en
route. In the last match she faced [Dominika
Cibulková](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominika_Cibulková "wikilink")
and lost in three sets. Her next tournament was the [French
Open](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_French_Open "wikilink") where
she entered the main draw as a qualifer and lost in the first round to
[Elina
Svitolina](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elina_Svitolina "wikilink").

She started her grass season at the [Mallorca
Open](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mallorca_Open "wikilink"), where she
once again entered the main draw through the qualifying round, and
defeated fifth seed [Yulia
Putintseva](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yulia_Putintseva "wikilink")
and [Daniela
Hantuchová](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniela_Hantuchová "wikilink")
before losing to Jankovic. After failing to qualify to the [Eastbourne
International](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastbourne_International "wikilink"),
Cîrstea lost in the first round of
[Wimbledon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Championships,_Wimbledon "wikilink")
to [Petra
Kvitová](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petra_Kvitová "wikilink").

### 2017年: Australian Open Round of 16

Cirstea started her 2017 season at the [Shenzhen
Open](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Shenzhen_Open_–_Singles "wikilink").
She defeated [Kristina
Kucova](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristina_Kucova "wikilink") in the
first round before losing to [Agnieszka
Radwanska](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agnieszka_Radwanska "wikilink")
in the second round. Cirstea made it to the 4th round of the [Australian
Open](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Australian_Open_–_Women's_Singles "wikilink")
for the first time in her career defeating [Irina
Khromacheva](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irina_Khromacheva "wikilink"),
10th seed [Carla Suárez
Navarro](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carla_Suárez_Navarro "wikilink")
and [Alison
Riske](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alison_Riske "wikilink") in the
first three rounds. She lost to seventh seed [Garbiñe
Muguruza](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garbiñe_Muguruza "wikilink") in
the fourth round. Cirstea's next tournament was the [Taiwan
Open](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Taiwan_Open_–_Singles "wikilink")
where she was forced to retire mid match while trailing to [Risa
Ozaki](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Risa_Ozaki "wikilink") in the first
round. She was part of the Romanian team at the [Fed
Cup](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Fed_Cup_World_Group_II "wikilink")
against Belgium for the World Group II 1st Round match up. She lost the
2nd rubber against [Yanina
Wickmayer](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yanina_Wickmayer "wikilink") in
a close three setter which went on for 3 hours & 22 minutes. For the
doubles, she teamed up with [Monica
Niculescu](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monica_Niculescu "wikilink") to
give Romania their only win at the meet up against Belgium.

After making first round exits at the [Hungarian Ladies
Open](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Hungarian_Ladies_Open_–_Singles "wikilink")
and [Indian
Wells](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_BNP_Paribas_Open_–_Women's_Singles "wikilink"),
she won her first singles match in nearly two months after defeating
[Monica Puig](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monica_Puig "wikilink") in
the first round of the [Miami
Open](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Miami_Open_–_Women's_Singles "wikilink").
Cirstea would later fall to [Caroline
Wozniacki](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caroline_Wozniacki "wikilink")
in the third round. She made the quarterfinals of the [Mutua Madrid
Open](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Mutua_Madrid_Open_–_Women's_Singles "wikilink")
where she lost to [Kristina
Mladenovic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristina_Mladenovic "wikilink").
She made the semifinals of [Nuremberg
Cup](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Nürnberger_Versicherungscup_–_Singles "wikilink")
after overcoming [Yulia
Putintseva](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yulia_Putintseva "wikilink")'s
antics in the quarterfinals.

Cirstea made it to the third round of
[Wimbledon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Wimbledon_Championships_–_Women's_Singles "wikilink")
before bowing out to [Garbiñe
Muguruza](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garbiñe_Muguruza "wikilink").

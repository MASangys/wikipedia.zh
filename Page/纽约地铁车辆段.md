**[纽约地铁](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/纽约地铁 "wikilink")**线网复杂，每条线路都有对应的**[车辆段](../Page/车辆段.md "wikilink")**供列车停放，车辆段还具有对列车的保养以及维修的功能，这些车辆段遍布纽约各地，其中一些车辆段是位于地下的。

### 174街车辆段

**174街车辆段**（）是[IND第八大道線的一个地下车辆段](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IND第八大道線 "wikilink")，用于存放纽约地铁****线的列车。车辆段有5条轨道，位于两条客运服务轨道以东。该车站在[168街車站以北](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/168街車站 "wikilink")，相距6个街区，并且邻近[175街](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/175街車站_\(IND第八大道線\) "wikilink")。靠内的轨道通向车辆段，用于调度C线车辆。该场站虽然有五条线路，但仅可容纳三列十车厢60英寸宽列车和两列六车厢60英寸宽列车，不过C线只需两条用于容纳十车厢60英寸宽列车的轨道即可正常服务。车辆段北端有一堵混凝土墙，以及邻近[跨曼哈顿高速公路的砖墙](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/跨曼哈顿高速公路 "wikilink")，这是因为该线路原计划行经[乔治华盛顿大桥](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/乔治华盛顿大桥 "wikilink")。\[5\]\[6\]

### 207街车辆段

[NYCSubway-207thStreetYard(2009-10-10).jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:NYCSubway-207thStreetYard\(2009-10-10\).jpg "fig:NYCSubway-207thStreetYard(2009-10-10).jpg") [NYCS_207th_St_Yard.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:NYCS_207th_St_Yard.jpg "fig:NYCS_207th_St_Yard.jpg") **207街车辆段**（）位于曼哈顿上城，[Harlem River](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harlem_River "wikilink") north of the [University Heights Bridge](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_Heights_Bridge "wikilink"). It serves as the home yard for the [R32s](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/R32_\(New_York_City_Subway_car\) "wikilink") assigned to the  train.

The 207th Street Shop is one of two heavy overhaul shops in the New York City Subway system (the other being the Coney Island Yard in Brooklyn) and provides overhaul and rebuilding of most [A Division](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Division_\(New_York_City_Subway\) "wikilink") cars as well as some [B Division](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/B_Division_\(New_York_City_Subway\) "wikilink") rolling stock.

The yard also stores cars that are being retired or awaiting scrapping and restores cars designated for the [New York Transit Museum](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Transit_Museum "wikilink").

South of the yard, connecting tracks lead to the [IND Eighth Avenue Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IND_Eighth_Avenue_Line "wikilink"). A separate connecting track and flyover leads to the [IRT Broadway – Seventh Avenue Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IRT_Broadway_–_Seventh_Avenue_Line "wikilink").   {{-}}

### Canarsie Yard

[Canarsie_yard_jeh.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Canarsie_yard_jeh.JPG "fig:Canarsie_yard_jeh.JPG") The **Canarsie Yard** (also known as **AY Yard** from its [telegraphy letters](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/telegraph "wikilink")) is located on the south end of the [BMT Canarsie Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_Canarsie_Line "wikilink") adjacent to [Canarsie – Rockaway Parkway](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canarsie_–_Rockaway_Parkway_\(BMT_Canarsie_Line\) "wikilink"). It is the primary layup yard for the **** train and hosts the only car wash for the entire BMT Eastern Division, washing trains for the ****, ****, **** and **** services. Signals were installed in 2003 in conjunction with the [BMT Canarsie Line automation](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automation_of_the_New_York_City_Subway "wikilink") project. This greatly increases the safety of train movements in the yard, as the **** train has some of the fastest growing ridership in the system.  {{-}}

### Church Avenue Yard

The **Church Avenue Yard** is an underground [rail yard](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/rail_yard "wikilink") on the [IND Culver Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IND_Culver_Line "wikilink"), used to store trains for the **** service. It is composed of four tracks directly under the four main line tracks above. This yard is directly connected through the IND [Church Avenue](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_Avenue_\(IND_Culver_Line\) "wikilink") station which presently is the terminus for "G" service. At least one of the yard's inner-most 4-tracks is in continuous use to turn-back equipment to the opposite direction. There are two ramps between each local and express track south of Church Avenue station for access. Each track can hold one full-length train between the [bumper blocks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/bumper_block "wikilink") and the crossovers.  {{-}}

### Coney Island Complex

[CI_Yard_Entrance_jeh.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:CI_Yard_Entrance_jeh.jpg "fig:CI_Yard_Entrance_jeh.jpg")

The **Coney Island Complex** is the largest [rapid transit](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/rapid_transit "wikilink") yard in the state of New York. Located in [Brooklyn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brooklyn "wikilink"), [New York](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York "wikilink"), it covers  and operates [24/7](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/24/7 "wikilink"). [Coney_Island_Complex_Outside.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Coney_Island_Complex_Outside.JPG "fig:Coney_Island_Complex_Outside.JPG") [Coney_Island_Complex_Workshop.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Coney_Island_Complex_Workshop.JPG "fig:Coney_Island_Complex_Workshop.JPG")

The complex was built in 1926 on former [marshlands](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/marshland "wikilink") that, along with [Coney Island Creek](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coney_Island_Creek "wikilink"), formerly separated [Coney Island](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coney_Island "wikilink") from the main body of Brooklyn. Much of this land had originally been proposed for use as a [ship canal](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/ship_canal "wikilink") and [port](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/port "wikilink") facility.

Regular scheduled maintenance is performed here for a fleet of nearly 800 cars serving the ****, ****, ****, **** and **[Franklin Avenue Shuttle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franklin_Avenue_Shuttle "wikilink")** trains. The shop facility along with 207th St. does heavy maintenance and overhaul for every one of the approximately 6,000 cars in the subway system, including the [Staten Island Railway](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Staten_Island_Railway "wikilink").

In addition to heavy maintenance facilities and track facilities for cars undergoing maintenance and overhaul, the complex includes three related railroad storage yards. The main yard facility, known as "Coney Island Yard," includes direct connections to the adjacent [BMT Sea Beach Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_Sea_Beach_Line "wikilink") ( train) and a two-track elevated structure to the [BMT West End Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_West_End_Line "wikilink") ( train). The main yard also serves trains on the [BMT Brighton Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_Brighton_Line "wikilink") ( trains) via tracks C & D (aka 3 & 4) of [Coney Island – Stillwell Avenue](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coney_Island_–_Stillwell_Avenue_\(New_York_City_Subway\) "wikilink") station. The adjacent but separate Culver (or "City") Yard connects to the [IND Culver Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IND_Culver_Line "wikilink") ( train) at the eastern border of the yard complex. Another yard, "Stillwell Yard," used mainly for off-peak train storage, is located across the Sea Beach Line from the main yard complex in a "V" between the divergent Sea Beach and West End Lines.

In addition to the maintenance shop and yards, there is a Health Center (gym) for Transit Authority employees and a firing range for the [New York City Police Department](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department "wikilink") (NYPD). The range was originally built for the [New York City Transit Police](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City_Transit_Police "wikilink") Department, which was merged with the NYPD in 1995.

The [Coney Island Yard Electric Motor Repair Shop](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coney_Island_Yard_Electric_Motor_Repair_Shop "wikilink") and [Coney Island Yard Gatehouse](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coney_Island_Yard_Gatehouse "wikilink") were listed on the [National Register of Historic Places](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places "wikilink") in 2006.\[7\]  {{-}}

### Concourse Yard

[NYCS_Concourse_yard.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:NYCS_Concourse_yard.jpg "fig:NYCS_Concourse_yard.jpg") [Concourseydjeh.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Concourseydjeh.jpg "fig:Concourseydjeh.jpg") The **Concourse Yard** is located in northern [Bronx](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronx "wikilink") near 205th Street and Jerome Avenue. This yard is home to all cars assigned to the **** trains and are maintained at this yard, although many trains assigned to the **** and **** services are stored here as well. Connecting tracks lead north from the yard to the [IND Concourse Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IND_Concourse_Line "wikilink") and south to the [IRT Jerome Avenue Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IRT_Jerome_Avenue_Line "wikilink"). Concourse Yard is spanned across its middle by Bedford Park Boulevard West, and at its northern end by a 205th Street viaduct. The Jerome Yard used by the IRT Jerome Avenue Line lies to the north of 205th Street.

The **Concourse Yard Entry Buildings** and **Concourse Yard Substation** were listed on the [National Register of Historic Places](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places "wikilink") in 2006.\[8\] The former consists of two, three-story brick buildings with only the top story visible from the street that are built next to one another to form a [gateway](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/gate "wikilink") to the Concourse Yard. They feature ornamental [limestone](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/limestone "wikilink") columns and [aluminum](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/aluminum "wikilink") doors. The buildings are connected by an iron bridge that retains its original Art Deco [balustrade](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/baluster "wikilink").\[9\] The latter is a one-story brick building measuring 50 feet by 100 feet and featuring a brick [parapet](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/parapet "wikilink") with ornamental [limestone](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/limestone "wikilink") and [aluminum](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/aluminum "wikilink") doors.\[10\]  {{-}}

### East New York Yard

**East New York Yard** (also known as **DO Yard** from its [telegraphy letters](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/telegraph "wikilink")) is primarily used to store and repair trains on the [J](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/J_\(New_York_City_Subway_service\) "wikilink"), [L](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/L_\(New_York_City_Subway_service\) "wikilink"), [M](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/M_\(New_York_City_Subway_service\) "wikilink") and [Z](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Z_\(New_York_City_Subway_service\) "wikilink") trains. [Subway](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rapid_transit "wikilink") equipment is inspected and maintained there on a regular basis.\[11\]

It is located at the junction of the [Canarsie](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_Canarsie_Line "wikilink") and [Jamaica Lines](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_Jamaica_Line "wikilink") near the intersection of Broadway and Jamaica Avenue in [East New York, Brooklyn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_New_York,_Brooklyn "wikilink"). A separate part of the facility houses the [East New York Bus Depot](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_New_York_Bus_Depot "wikilink"), formerly a [trolley](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/tram "wikilink") depot. The yard is entirely equipped with hand-operated switches. Only the Fresh Pond Yard and 36th–38th Street Yard share this characteristic.

Portions of the yard date back to 1885 and the [Lexington Avenue Elevated](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lexington_Avenue_Elevated "wikilink") and the yard predates the rebuilding of nearby [Broadway Junction](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadway_Junction_\(BMT_Jamaica_Line\) "wikilink"), which was formerly known as **Manhattan Junction** or **East New York Loop**.

The yard and its main lead configuration remained the same before and after the extensive elevated line rebuilding nearby, but additional track and structure was built, so that, at its peak, East New York Yard had direct connections to the Broadway Elevated going west, [Jamaica Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_Jamaica_Line "wikilink") going east, [Canarsie Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_Canarsie_Line "wikilink") going east, and [Fulton Street Elevated](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kings_County_Elevated_Railway "wikilink") both east and west.

### Fresh Pond Yard

The **Fresh Pond Yard** in [Middle Village](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Village "wikilink"), [Queens](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queens "wikilink") is located to the back of the [Fresh Pond Bus Depot](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fresh_Pond_Bus_Depot "wikilink") formerly a [trolley](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/tram "wikilink") depot, and is used for storing the [R160As](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/R160A_\(New_York_City_Subway_car\) "wikilink") that run on the **[M](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/M_\(New_York_City_Subway_service\) "wikilink")** train. It is located between [Fresh Pond Road](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fresh_Pond_Road_\(BMT_Myrtle_Avenue_Line\) "wikilink") and [Middle Village – Metropolitan Avenue](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Village_–_Metropolitan_Avenue_\(BMT_Myrtle_Avenue_Line\) "wikilink") on the [BMT Myrtle Avenue Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_Myrtle_Avenue_Line "wikilink"), but is only accessible from the latter station. Trains must first platform there and then reverse into the yard. The yard is entirely equipped with hand-operated switches. Only East New York Yard and 36th–38th Street Yard share this characteristic.

### Jamaica Yard

[Jamaica_Yard_east_jeh.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Jamaica_Yard_east_jeh.JPG "fig:Jamaica_Yard_east_jeh.JPG") [Jamaica_Yard_doorway_jeh.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Jamaica_Yard_doorway_jeh.JPG "fig:Jamaica_Yard_doorway_jeh.JPG") [Jamaica_Yard,_view_of_R160_and_R46,_Mar_2013.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Jamaica_Yard,_view_of_R160_and_R46,_Mar_2013.jpg "fig:Jamaica_Yard,_view_of_R160_and_R46,_Mar_2013.jpg") **Jamaica Yard** is located in [Kew Gardens, Queens](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kew_Gardens,_Queens "wikilink") at the south end of [Flushing Meadows–Corona Park](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flushing_Meadows–Corona_Park "wikilink"). It is connected to the [IND Queens Boulevard Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IND_Queens_Boulevard_Line "wikilink") at a three-way [flying junction](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/flying_junction "wikilink"). The yard is on surface level, and the four-track approach includes a bridge over the [Grand Central Parkway](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Central_Parkway "wikilink"), despite the Queens Boulevard Line being underground.\[12\] The site upon which the yard sits at the head of the valley of the [Flushing River](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flushing_River "wikilink") was, during the [American Revolution](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Revolution "wikilink"), occupied by British troops after the [Battle of Long Island](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Long_Island "wikilink").\[13\]

The yard was built for the [Independent Subway System](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Independent_Subway_System "wikilink") in the 1930s and served as the south end of the [World's Fair Railroad](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/World's_Fair_Railroad "wikilink") in 1939 and 1940. The yard provides carwash, interior cleaning, grease and minor repair services to the [R46s](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/R46_\(New_York_City_Subway_car\) "wikilink") and [R160s](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/R160_\(New_York_City_Subway_car\) "wikilink") that are assigned to the ****, ****, and **** trains.\[14\]\[15\] Some R160s for the **[M](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/M_\(New_York_City_Subway_service\) "wikilink")** train are stored here as well during weekdays.

The [Metropolitan Transportation Authority](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolitan_Transportation_Authority_\(New_York\) "wikilink") plans to enlarge the yard.\[16\]

### Pitkin Yard

[Pitkin_Av_Yard_jeh.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pitkin_Av_Yard_jeh.JPG "fig:Pitkin_Av_Yard_jeh.JPG") The **Pitkin Yard** is located in [East New York, Brooklyn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_New_York,_Brooklyn "wikilink"). It holds the Pitkin Shops, which maintain the [R46s](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/R46_\(New_York_City_Subway_car\) "wikilink") used on the **** train and [Rockaway Park Shuttle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockaway_Park_Shuttle "wikilink"). Some [R32s](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/R32_\(New_York_City_Subway_car\) "wikilink") used on the **** train are also stored here.

Track connections from the yard connect both railroad north to [Euclid Avenue](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclid_Avenue_\(IND_Fulton_Street_Line\) "wikilink") and railroad south past [Grant Avenue](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grant_Avenue_\(IND_Fulton_Street_Line\) "wikilink") on the [IND Fulton Street Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IND_Fulton_Street_Line "wikilink"). This allows trains to be added or removed from service in either direction.  {{-}}

### Rockaway Park Yard

[Rockaway_Yard_east_jeh.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Rockaway_Yard_east_jeh.jpg "fig:Rockaway_Yard_east_jeh.jpg") **Rockaway Park Yard** is located in [Rockaway Park, Queens](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockaway_Park,_Queens "wikilink"). It is a layup yard for the [R46s](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/R46_\(New_York_City_Subway_car\) "wikilink") on the [Rockaway Park Shuttle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockaway_Park_Shuttle "wikilink") and **** train, although they are primarily maintained at the [Pitkin Yard](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pitkin_Yard "wikilink") in [Brooklyn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brooklyn "wikilink"). [R40A](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/R40A_\(New_York_City_Subway_car\) "wikilink") \#4392-93 are used for school training in this yard. [Rockway_Yard_west_jeh.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Rockway_Yard_west_jeh.jpg "fig:Rockway_Yard_west_jeh.jpg") This yard is adjacent to [Rockaway Park – Beach 116th Street](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockaway_Park_–_Beach_116th_Street_\(IND_Rockaway_Line\) "wikilink"). Like the [IND Rockaway Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IND_Rockaway_Line "wikilink") itself, the Rockaway Yard was originally a yard for the [Rockaway Beach Branch](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockaway_Beach_Branch "wikilink") of the [Long Island Rail Road](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_Island_Rail_Road "wikilink"). It included a water tower, a roundhouse, and an elevated loop track for [Brooklyn Rapid Transit](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brooklyn_Rapid_Transit "wikilink") trains.  {{-}}

## 其他车辆段

### 36th-38th Street Yard

[BMT_36-38_St_yard_jeh.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:BMT_36-38_St_yard_jeh.JPG "fig:BMT_36-38_St_yard_jeh.JPG") The **36th–38th Street Yard** is located between Fifth and Seventh Avenues in [Sunset Park, Brooklyn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunset_Park,_Brooklyn "wikilink"), adjacent to the [Jackie Gleason Bus Depot](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bus_depots_of_the_New_York_City_Transit_Authority#Jackie_Gleason_Depot "wikilink"). This yard is not normally used for revenue-service train maintenance, though some trains for the **** service are stored here. Its primary function is to store diesel and electrically powered maintenance-of-way and other non-revenue service rolling stock. It is also used to transfer trash from garbage collector trains to trucks via platforms inside the yard just south of 37th Street.

This southern part of the yard was formerly the center of the [South Brooklyn Railway](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Brooklyn_Railway "wikilink"), which extended from [Bush Terminal](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bush_Terminal "wikilink") through the north part of the yard, then down Gravesend Avenue and into the [Coney Island Yard](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coney_Island_Complex "wikilink"). The yard is entirely equipped with hand-operated switches. Only [Fresh Pond Yard](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fresh_Pond_Yard "wikilink") and [East New York Yard](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_New_York_Yard "wikilink") share this characteristic.

### Linden Shops

**Linden Shops** is a track shop, where track switches and other components are assembled. It has track connections to the [IRT New Lots Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/IRT_New_Lots_Line "wikilink") and [BMT Canarsie Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_Canarsie_Line "wikilink"), but no [third rail](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/third_rail "wikilink"), restricting the facility to diesel-powered trains only. There is also a track connection to the [Long Island Rail Road](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_Island_Rail_Road "wikilink")'s [Bay Ridge Branch](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_Ridge_Branch "wikilink"). This connection is one of two from the subway to the mainline United States rail network (the [BMT West End Line](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMT_West_End_Line "wikilink") is the other).

[London_School_of_Economics_Statistics_Machine_Room_1964.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:London_School_of_Economics_Statistics_Machine_Room_1964.jpg "fig:London_School_of_Economics_Statistics_Machine_Room_1964.jpg") of the [London School of Economics](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/London_School_of_Economics "wikilink") in 1964.\]\] **Computational statistics**, or **statistical computing**, is the interface between [统计学](../Page/统计学.md "wikilink") and [计算机科学](../Page/计算机科学.md "wikilink"). It is the area of [计算科学](../Page/计算科学.md "wikilink") (或科学计算) specific to the mathematical science of [statistics](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/statistics "wikilink"). This area is also developing rapidly, leading to calls that a broader concept of computing should be taught as part of general [statistical education](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/statistical_education "wikilink").\[1\]

As in [traditional statistics](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistics "wikilink") the goal is to transform [raw data](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/raw_data "wikilink") into [knowledge](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/knowledge "wikilink"),\[2\] but the focus lies on [computer](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/computer "wikilink") intensive [statistical methods](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/statistical_methods "wikilink"), such as cases with very large [sample size](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sample_size_determination "wikilink") and non-homogeneous [data sets](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/data_set "wikilink").\[3\]

The terms 'computational statistics' and 'statistical computing' are often used interchangeably, although Carlo Lauro (a former president of the [International Association for Statistical Computing](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Association_for_Statistical_Computing "wikilink")) proposed making a distinction, defining 'statistical computing' as "the application of computer science to statistics", and 'computational statistics' as "aiming at the design of algorithm for implementing statistical methods on computers, including the ones unthinkable before the computer age (e.g. [bootstrap](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/bootstrapping_\(statistics\) "wikilink"), [simulation](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monte_Carlo_simulation "wikilink")), as well as to cope with analytically intractable problems" \[*[sic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/sic "wikilink")*\].\[4\]

The term 'Computational statistics' may also be used to refer to computationally *intensive* statistical methods including [resampling](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/resampling_\(statistics\) "wikilink") methods, [Markov chain Monte Carlo](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markov_chain_Monte_Carlo "wikilink") methods, [local regression](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/local_regression "wikilink"), [kernel density estimation](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/kernel_density_estimation "wikilink"), [artificial neural networks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/artificial_neural_networks "wikilink") and [generalized additive models](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/generalized_additive_model "wikilink").

**圣达味日**（, ）是[威尔士](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/威尔士 "wikilink")[主保圣人](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/主保圣人 "wikilink")[圣达味](../Page/圣达味.md "wikilink")（圣公会称圣大卫）的[瞻礼日](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/圣人历 "wikilink")，纪念于每年3月1日。12世纪[天主教会将圣达味](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/天主教会 "wikilink")[封圣](../Page/封圣.md "wikilink")后，3月1日即成为圣达味的瞻礼日，不过目前圣达味日不是英国的[公众假日](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/公众假日 "wikilink")，仅是威尔士地区的法定假日。传统上圣达味日里人们应该佩戴威尔士的象征[韭葱](../Page/韭葱.md "wikilink")或[水仙](../Page/水仙.md "wikilink")、食用威尔士的传统餐点（）与[威尔士干酪](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/威尔士干酪 "wikilink")，女士要穿上威尔士的传统服装。[卡迪夫](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/卡迪夫 "wikilink")、[斯旺西](../Page/斯旺西.md "wikilink")，以及[阿伯里斯特威斯](../Page/阿伯里斯特威斯.md "wikilink")等地在圣达味日会举行纪念游行活动。

## Significance of the day

Saint David () was born in [Caerfai](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caerfai_Bay "wikilink"), south west Wales into an aristocratic family.\[1\]\[2\] He was reportedly a [scion](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinship "wikilink") of the royal house of Ceredigion,\[3\] and founded a Celtic monastic community at Glyn Rhosyn (The Vale of Roses) on the western headland of Pembrokeshire () at the spot where [St David's Cathedral](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_David's_Cathedral "wikilink") stands today.\[4\]

David's fame as a teacher and his [asceticism](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/asceticism "wikilink") spread among [Celtic Christians](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celtic_Christianity "wikilink"), and he helped found about 12 monasteries.\[5\] His foundation at Glyn Rhosyn became an important Christian [shrine](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/shrine "wikilink"),\[6\] and the most important centre in Wales. The date of Saint David's death is believed to be 1 March 589.\[7\] His final words to the community of monks were: "Brothers be ye constant. The [yoke](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/yoke "wikilink") which with single mind ye have taken, bear ye to the end; and whatsoever ye have seen with me and heard, keep and fulfil."\[8\]

For centuries, 1 March has been a national festival. Saint David was recognised as a national patron saint in the 12th century\[9\] at a peak time of Welsh resistance to the Normans.\[10\] He was canonised by [Pope Callixtus II](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope_Callixtus_II "wikilink") in 1120. The 17th-century diarist [Samuel Pepys](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_Pepys "wikilink") noted how Welsh celebrations in London for Saint David's Day would spark wider counter-celebrations amongst their English neighbours: life-sized effigies of Welshmen were symbolically lynched,\[11\] and by the 18th century the custom had arisen of confectioners producing "taffies"—[gingerbread](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/gingerbread "wikilink") figures baked in the shape of a Welshman riding a goat—on Saint David's Day.\[12\]

Unlike [Saint Patrick's Day](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint_Patrick's_Day "wikilink") in Ireland, Saint David's Day is not a national holiday, though there is strong support for it becoming a [bank holiday](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/bank_holiday "wikilink") in Wales. In the past, schools have taken a half-day holiday, which continues in some parts of Wales. Saint David's Day is also celebrated in expatriate Welsh communities outside the UK.\[13\] Cross-party support resulted in the [National Assembly for Wales](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Assembly_for_Wales "wikilink") voting unanimously to make Saint David's Day a public holiday in 2000. A poll conducted for Saint David's Day in 2006 found that 87% of people in Wales wanted it to be a bank holiday, with 65% prepared to sacrifice a different bank holiday to achieve this.\[14\] A petition in 2007 to make Saint David's Day a bank holiday was rejected by the office of the British Prime Minister [Tony Blair](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Blair "wikilink").

In the poem *[Armes Prydein](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armes_Prydein "wikilink")* (*The Prophesy of Britain*), composed in the early to mid-10th century, the anonymous author prophesies that the Cymry (the [Welsh people](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welsh_people "wikilink")) will unite and join an alliance of fellow-Celts\[15\] to repel the Anglo-Saxons, under the banner of Saint David: *A lluman glân Dewi a ddyrchafant* ("And they will raise the pure banner of Dewi").\[16\] Although there were occasional Welsh uprisings in the Middle Ages, the country was briefly united by various Welsh princes before its conquest\[17\] at different times, and it arguably had a very short period of independence during the rising of Owain Glyndŵr,\[18\] but Wales as a whole was never an independent kingdom for long. [Henry Tudor, 2nd Earl of Richmond](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry,_Earl_of_Richmond "wikilink"), who was born in [Pembroke Castle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pembroke_Castle "wikilink") as a patrilineal descendant of the [Tudor Dynasty of North Wales](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tudors_of_Penmynydd#Lineage "wikilink"), became [King](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_of_England "wikilink") [Henry VII of England](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_VII_of_England "wikilink") after his victory over [Richard III](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_III_of_England "wikilink") at the [Battle of Bosworth Field](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Bosworth_Field "wikilink") in 1485, to end the [Wars of the Roses](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wars_of_the_Roses "wikilink").\[19\] Henry's green and white banner with a red dragon became a rallying point for Welsh patriotism with the memory of Saint David on his Feast Day. Henry was the first monarch of the [House of Tudor](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Tudor "wikilink"), and during the reign of that dynasty the royal coat of arms included the [Welsh Dragon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welsh_Dragon "wikilink"), a reference to the monarch's origin. The banner from Henry's victory was not adopted as the official [Flag of Wales](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flag_of_Wales "wikilink") until 1959.\[20\] The [flag of Saint David](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/flag_of_Saint_David "wikilink"), however, a golden cross on a black background, was not part of the symbolism of House of Tudor.\[21\]

## Saint David's Day events

### Cardiff

Around Wales each year, Saint David is commemorated in parades, the largest of which in [Cardiff](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardiff "wikilink"). The parade is a non-military celebration of Welsh heritage and culture.\[22\]

To mark Saint David's Day, and their return from a six-month tour of Afghanistan, soldiers from the [Royal Welsh Regiment](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Welsh_Regiment "wikilink") provided [The Changing of the Guard](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guard_Mounting "wikilink") ceremony at [Cardiff Castle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardiff_Castle "wikilink")'s south gate on 27 and 28 February 2010.\[23\]

On 1 March 2010, the seventh National Saint David's Day Parade took place in [Cardiff city centre](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardiff_city_centre "wikilink"). Celebrations included concerts, a parade and a food festival. The food festival ran from 26 February with the third annual Really Welsh Food Festival in [Queen Street](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen_Street,_Cardiff "wikilink"), featuring all-Welsh produce.\[24\]\[25\] Following the parade, a number of Welsh entertainers performed and in the evening [Cardiff Central Library](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardiff_Central_Library "wikilink") provided free entertainment and food.\[26\]

### Other locations

In many towns an annual parade through the centre of town is now held. Concerts are held in pubs, clubs, and other venues.\[27\] In the town of [Colwyn Bay](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colwyn_Bay "wikilink") in north Wales, an annual parade through the centre of town is now held with several hundred citizens and schoolchildren taking part. Other events are centred on the parade.\[28\]

The town of [Prestatyn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prestatyn "wikilink") closes the top of the High Street for local schools to participate in Welsh singing and local fundraising.

[Swansea](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swansea "wikilink") inaugurated a [St David's Week](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_David's_Week "wikilink") festival in 2009 with a range of musical, sporting and cultural events held throughout the city to mark the national day.\[29\]

[Disneyland Paris](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disneyland_Paris "wikilink") also organises yearly events to celebrate Saint David's Day which include a Welsh-themed week, fireworks, parades and Disney characters dressed in traditional Welsh attire.\[30\]

[Washington, DC](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington,_DC "wikilink") holds a St. David's Day congressional reception at the [United States Capitol](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Capitol "wikilink") in honor of the [First Minister of Wales](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Minister_of_Wales "wikilink")' biannual visits.\[31\]

The [Los Angeles St. David's Day Festival](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_St._David's_Day_Festival "wikilink") – National Day of Wales is the largest annual event of its kind in the United States encompassing an [eisteddfod](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/eisteddfod "wikilink"), [Celtic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celts "wikilink") marketplace, classes, and a concert.\[32\]

## Traditions

[St_david's_day_swansea_2009.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:St_david's_day_swansea_2009.jpg "fig:St_david's_day_swansea_2009.jpg") fountain dyed red for Saint David's Day\]\] Children in Wales take part in school concerts or [eisteddfodau](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/eisteddfod "wikilink"), with recitation and singing being the main activities. Formerly, schoolchildren were given a half-day holiday. Officially this custom does not continue, although the practice can vary between schools.\[33\] The younger girls sometimes wear [traditional Welsh costumes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/traditional_Welsh_costume "wikilink") to school. This costume consists of a long woollen skirt, apron, white blouse, woollen shawl and a [Welsh hat](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welsh_hat "wikilink").\[34\]

Also, various Welsh [Regiments](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_British_Army_regiments "wikilink") of the [British Army](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Army "wikilink") use aspects of Saint David's cross, Saint David himself, or songs of Saint David in their formalities during the celebrations. Many Welsh people wear one or both of the [National symbols of Wales](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_symbols_of_Wales "wikilink") to celebrate St. David: the [daffodil](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/daffodil "wikilink") (a generic Welsh symbol) or the [leek](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/leek "wikilink") (Saint David's personal symbol) on this day.\[35\] The leek arises from an occasion when a troop of Welsh were able to distinguish each other from a troop of English enemy (some historical accounts indicate Saxon invading forces), dressed in similar fashion, by wearing leeks.\[36\]

The [flag of Saint David](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/flag_of_Saint_David "wikilink") often plays a central role in the celebrations and can be seen flying throughout Wales. Popular dishes traditionally eaten on Saint David's Day include [cawl](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cawl "wikilink") (soup), *bara brith* tea loaf, [Welsh Cakes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welsh_cake "wikilink"), lamb and [Welsh rarebit](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welsh_rarebit "wikilink").\[37\]

**藝術與建築索引典**（，AAT）是一个用来描述关于[艺术](../Page/艺术.md "wikilink")、[建筑](../Page/建筑.md "wikilink")和[物质文化项目的](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/物质文化 "wikilink")[受控词表](../Page/受控词表.md "wikilink")。AAT收录各种通用术语，如“大教堂”，但不收录专有名词，如“圣母大教堂”。AAT供[博物馆](../Page/博物馆.md "wikilink")、艺术[图书馆](../Page/图书馆.md "wikilink")、[档案馆以及艺术和艺术史研究人员等使用](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/档案馆 "wikilink")。AAT符合，和ANSI / NISO Z39.19标准。

AAT是一个约44000个概念的结构化词汇表\[1\]，包括131,000个术语、描述、书目引用，以及与美术、建筑、装饰艺术、档案材料和物质文化有关的其他信息\[2\]。

## 历史

The AAT project began in the late 1970s in response to the gradual automation of records by art libraries, art journal indexing services, and catalogers of museum objects and visual resources. Automation required consistency in [cataloging](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cataloging "wikilink") as well as more efficient [retrieval of information](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/information_retrieval "wikilink"); a controlled vocabulary was a solution to both these problems. The project was conceived by [library directors](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/library_director "wikilink") and architectural experts Toni Petersen, Dora Crouch, and Pat Molholt and was originally headquartered part-time at RPI in Troy, NY, then at Bennington College in Bennington, VT and later moved to [Williamstown, Massachusetts](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Williamstown,_Massachusetts "wikilink"), with the [J. Paul Getty Trust](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._Paul_Getty_Trust "wikilink") providing technical advice and funding. In 1983 the Getty Trust took over [editorial](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/editing "wikilink") responsibility. The AAT offices relocated to the Getty's Los Angeles headquarters in order to better coordinate with two other similar Getty projects, the [Union List of Artist Names](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_List_of_Artist_Names "wikilink") (ULAN) and [Getty Thesaurus of Geographic Names](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Getty_Thesaurus_of_Geographic_Names "wikilink") (TGN) soon after its publication.

The AAT was published in 1990 and 1994 in both print and electronic form. By 1997, the size and frequency of updates made hard-copy publication unfeasible and the decision was made to publish via a searchable online Web interface and in data files available for licensing. The online Web interface is freely-accessible from any computer connected to the Internet. Final editorial control of the AAT is maintained by the [Getty Vocabulary Program](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Getty_Vocabulary_Program "wikilink"), part of the [Getty Research Institute](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Getty_Research_Institute "wikilink").

Since 2008, Taiwan e-Learning and Digital Archives Program (TELDAP)\[3\] collaborated with [Getty Research Institute](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Getty_Research_Institute "wikilink") (GRI) in developing the [Chinese-language](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese-language "wikilink") Art & Architecture Thesaurus (AAT-Taiwan). The initial goal of this project is to provide multilingual search and corresponding images in integrate digital archives systems of [Taiwan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "wikilink"), and broaden the inclusion of terms related to Asian art, architecture and material culture in AAT.

The AAT can be used in several ways:

  - at the data entry stage, by catalogers or indexers who are describing works of art, architecture, material culture, archival materials, visual surrogates, or bibliographic materials;
  - as knowledge bases, providing information for researchers;
  - as search assistants to enhance end-user access to online resources;
  - as target for enriching free-text descriptions of cultural objects;\[4\]
  - as a pivot vocabulary for coreferencing (interlinking) other art vocabularies\[5\]

AAT is available as [Linked Open Data](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linked_Open_Data "wikilink") at vocab.getty.edu\[6\] since February 2014\[7\] and is updated bi-weekly.

## 项目

The initial core set of terms was derived from authority lists and the literature of art and architectural history; this core set was reviewed, approved and added to by an advisory team made up scholars from all relevant disciplines, including art and architectural historians, [architects](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/architect "wikilink"), [librarians](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/librarian "wikilink"), visual resource [curators](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/curator "wikilink"), [archivists](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/archivist "wikilink"), museum personnel, and specialists in [thesaurus](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/thesaurus "wikilink") construction. Its [hierarchy](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/hierarchy "wikilink") was inspired by the [Medical Subject Headings](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medical_Subject_Headings "wikilink"). All eras from antiquity to the present are covered, and it is not limited geographically.

As of January 2007, the AAT contained approximately 131,000 terms. While the thesaurus contains many variations on a term, such as [singular and plural forms](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/grammatical_number "wikilink"), spelling variants, various forms of speech, and [synonyms](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/synonym "wikilink"), one is always flagged as the preferred term. Terms are updated biweekly and regular users are encouraged to propose new terms.

In 2015 AAT contains 354,000 terms.\[8\] They are available in 4 major languages (English, Dutch, Spanish and Chinese), and some terms in various native languages.

## 设计

The AAT is a [faceted classification](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/faceted_classification "wikilink") system as well as a hierarchical one. There are seven facets:

  - Associated Concepts - [abstract concepts](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/abstract_concept "wikilink"), such as beauty, balance, connoisseurship, metaphor, freedom, socialism (Hierarchy: Associated concepts)
  - Physical Attributes - perceptible or measurable characteristics such as size, shape, chemical properties, texture and hardness, such as strapwork, borders, round, waterlogged, brittleness. (Hierarchies: Attributes and Properties, Conditions and Effects, Design Elements, Color)
  - Styles and Periods - stylistic groupings and distinct chronological periods, such as [French](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "wikilink"), Louis XIV, [T'ang Dynasty](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/T'ang_Dynasty "wikilink"), [Chippendale](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Chippendale "wikilink") (Hierarchy: Styles and Periods)
  - Agents - people, groups of people, and organizations such as [printmakers](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/printmaker "wikilink"), [landscape architects](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/landscape_architect "wikilink"), corporations, [religious orders](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/religious_order "wikilink"). (Hierarchies: People, Organizations)
  - Activities - areas of endeavor, physical and mental actions or methods, such as [archaeology](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/archaeology "wikilink"), [engineering](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/engineering "wikilink"), analyzing, contests, [exhibitions](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Art_exhibition "wikilink"), running, drawing (image-making), [corrosion](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/corrosion "wikilink"). (Hierarchies: Disciplines, Functions, Events, Physical and Mental Activities, Processes and Techniques)
  - Materials - physical substances, such as iron, clay, adhesive, emulsifier, artificial ivory, [millwork](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Millwork_\(building_material\) "wikilink"), nylon. (Hierarchy: Materials)
  - Objects - objects either fabricated or given form by human activity, such as [paintings](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/painting "wikilink"), [amphorae](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/amphora "wikilink"), [facades](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/facade "wikilink"), [cathedrals](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cathedral "wikilink"), [Brewster Chairs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brewster_Chair "wikilink"), [gardens](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/garden "wikilink") (Hierarchies: Object Groupings and Systems, Object Genres, Components; Built Environment: Settlements and Landscapes, Built Complexes and Districts, Single Built Works, Open Spaces and Site Elements; Furnishings and Equipment: Furnishings, Costume, Tools and Equipment, Weapons and Ammunition, Measuring Devices, Containers, Sound Devices, Recreational Artifacts, Transportation Vehicles; Visual and Verbal Communication: Visual Works, Exchange Media, Information Forms)

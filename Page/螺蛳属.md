**螺蛳属**（學名：**）是一個[淡水生](../Page/水生動物.md "wikilink")、有的的[属](../Page/属.md "wikilink"),是[田螺科](../Page/田螺科.md "wikilink")[环棱螺亚科](../Page/环棱螺亚科.md "wikilink")的[腹足纲](../Page/腹足纲.md "wikilink")[软体动物](../Page/软体动物.md "wikilink")。

## 分類學

螺蛳属的學名**源於本屬模式種**樣本的採集者A.R. Margary：他在[中國](../Page/中國.md "wikilink")[雲南](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/雲南 "wikilink")[洱海](../Page/洱海.md "wikilink")採集了本物種的標本，然後再由Nevill (1877) 描述。基於螺殼及螺肋的形態，[张玺及](../Page/张玺_\(动物学家\).md "wikilink")[齊鍾彥](../Page/齊鍾彥.md "wikilink")於1949年首度修訂本屬，本辨識出本屬有七個不同的物種\[1\]。在這次修訂裡，張璽建立了兩個螺螄屬的亞屬，即：[玺螺蛳亚属](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/玺螺蛳属 "wikilink")（**）及*[Mabillemargarya](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mabillemargarya "wikilink")*亞屬\[2\]。The first molecular phylogeny showed that the genus *Margarya* is [多系群](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/多系群 "wikilink") and divided into three distinct clades. Combining study of comparative morphology and molecular phylogeny, Zhang *et al*. revised the systematics of *Margarya* and recognized eight genuine extant species. However, only four species were found belonging to *Margarya* s. str., the rest species belonging to distinct genus ** or **.\[3\]

## 分佈

本屬物種只見於[中华人民共和国](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/中华人民共和国 "wikilink")[云南省](../Page/云南省.md "wikilink")的湖泊中，包括有：[滇池](../Page/滇池.md "wikilink")、[洱海](../Page/洱海.md "wikilink")、[茈碧湖](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/茈碧湖 "wikilink")、[Jianhu Lake](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jianhu_Lake "wikilink")、[Xihu Lake及](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xihu_Lake "wikilink")[泸沽湖](../Page/泸沽湖.md "wikilink")\[4\]。

## Cladogram

The cladogram based on sequences of mitochondrial [16S 核糖体RNA](../Page/16S_核糖体RNA.md "wikilink") and  (COI), and nuclear  genes showing the phylogenic relationships of the genus *Margarya* indicates that *Margarya* is [多系群](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/多系群 "wikilink").\[13\]\[14\] Margarya as traditionally delimited decayed into three distinct clades.The sister lineages of two of these clades are representatives from different vivparid genera, which are widely distributed in East and Southeast Asian rivers and lakes. These predominantly riparian viviparids also differ markedly in their shell morphology. Herein only genus-level phylogenic relationship is given, whereas relationship between lower taxonomic units deserve further study.

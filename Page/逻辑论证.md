在[逻辑中](../Page/逻辑.md "wikilink")，**论证**是基于叫做[前提的一组断言](../Page/前提.md "wikilink")，证明叫做[结论的断言的](../Page/结论.md "wikilink")[真实性的尝试](../Page/真理.md "wikilink")。[演绎和](../Page/演绎推理.md "wikilink")[归纳推理的证明过程形成了论证](../Page/归纳推理.md "wikilink")，并假定了某种交流方式，它可以是书写的文本、演讲或交谈。

## 概述

一般的，哲学和科学论证也常使用[溯因论证和](../Page/溯因推理.md "wikilink")[类推论证](../Page/类推.md "wikilink")。论证可以是**[有效的](../Page/有效性.md "wikilink")**或**无效的**，尽管如何确定论证是这两个范畴中的某一个，自身也经常是大量讨论的对象。非形式的，你可能期望有效的论证是**强制性的**，在它有能力使人信服结论的真实性的意义上。但是，这样的有效性标准是不充足的甚至是令人误解的，因为这更多的依赖于构造论证的人的技巧去操纵要说服的人，而更少依赖于论证自身。

论证的有效性的更少主观性的标准经常是明显需要的，并且在某些情况下我们甚至期望一个论证是[严格的](../Page/严格.md "wikilink")，就是说，坚持精确的有效性规则。这是在数学证明中使用的论证的情况。注意严格的证明不必须是[形式证明](../Page/形式证明.md "wikilink")。

在平常语言中，人们提及**论证的逻辑**，或使用暗示了一个论证是基于[形式逻辑的](../Page/形式逻辑.md "wikilink")[推理规则的术语](../Page/推理规则.md "wikilink")。尽管论证确可使用无可争议的纯逻辑推论（比如三段论），在实际论证中几乎总是使用其他种类的推论。例如，常处理[因果关系](../Page/因果关系.md "wikilink")、[或然性和](../Page/或然性.md "wikilink")[统计或更特殊化的领域如](../Page/统计.md "wikilink")[经济学的论证](../Page/经济学.md "wikilink")。在这些情况下，**逻辑**指称论证的结构而不是可以用在其中的纯逻辑的原理。

## 參考文獻

  -
  - [Robert Audi](../Page/Robert_Audi.md "wikilink"), *Epistemology*,
    Routledge, 1998. Particularly relevant is Chapter 6, which explores
    the relationship between knowledge, inference and argument.

  - J. L. Austin *[How to Do Things With
    Words](../Page/How_to_Do_Things_With_Words.md "wikilink")*, Oxford
    University Press, 1976.

  - H. P. Grice, *Logic and Conversation* in *The Logic of Grammar*,
    Dickenson, 1975.

  - [Vincent F. Hendricks](../Page/Vincent_F._Hendricks.md "wikilink"),
    *Thought 2 Talk: A Crash Course in Reflection and Expression*, New
    York: Automatic Press / VIP, 2005, ISBN 87-991013-7-8

  - R. A. DeMillo, R. J. Lipton and A. J. Perlis, *Social Processes and
    Proofs of Theorems and Programs*, Communications of the ACM, Vol.
    22, No. 5, 1979. A classic article on the social process of
    acceptance of proofs in mathematics.

  - [Yu. Manin](../Page/Yu._Manin.md "wikilink"), *A Course in
    Mathematical Logic*, Springer Verlag, 1977. A mathematical view of
    logic. This book is different from most books on mathematical logic
    in that it emphasizes the mathematics of logic, as opposed to the
    formal structure of logic.

  - [Ch. Perelman](../Page/Chaim_Perelman.md "wikilink") and L.
    Olbrechts-Tyteca, *The New Rhetoric*, Notre Dame, 1970. This classic
    was originally published in French in 1958.

  - [Henri Poincaré](../Page/Henri_Poincaré.md "wikilink"), *Science and
    Hypothesis*, Dover Publications, 1952

  - Frans van Eemeren and [Rob
    Grootendorst](../Page/Rob_Grootendorst.md "wikilink"), *Speech Acts
    in Argumentative Discussions*, Foris Publications, 1984.

  - [K. R. Popper](../Page/K._R._Popper.md "wikilink") *Objective
    Knowledge; An Evolutionary Approach*, Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1972.

  - [L. S. Stebbing](../Page/L._S._Stebbing.md "wikilink"), *A Modern
    Introduction to Logic*, Methuen and Co., 1948. An account of logic
    that covers the classic topics of logic and argument while carefully
    considering modern developments in logic.

  - [Douglas Walton](../Page/Doug_Walton.md "wikilink"), *Informal
    Logic: A Handbook for Critical Argumentation*, Cambridge, 1998.

  - Walton, Douglas; Christopher Reed; Fabrizio Macagno, *Argumentation
    Schemes*, New York: Cambridge University Press, 2008.

  - Carlos Chesñevar, Ana Maguitman and [Ronald
    Loui](../Page/Ronald_Loui.md "wikilink"), *Logical Models of
    Argument*, ACM Computing Surveys, vol. 32, num. 4, pp. 337–383,
    2000.

  - [T. Edward Damer](../Page/T._Edward_Damer.md "wikilink").
    *[Attacking Faulty
    Reasoning](../Page/Attacking_Faulty_Reasoning.md "wikilink")*, 5th
    Edition, Wadsworth, 2005. ISBN 0-534-60516-8

  - Charles Arthur Willard, A Theory of Argumentation. 1989.

  - Charles Arthur Willard, Argumentation and the Social Grounds of
    Knowledge. 1982.

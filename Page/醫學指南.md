[Edwin_Smith_Papyrus_v2.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Edwin_Smith_Papyrus_v2.jpg "fig:Edwin_Smith_Papyrus_v2.jpg")（約西元前17世記） 的第6,7頁，是相當早期的醫學指南\]\] **醫學指南**（medical guidelines）也稱為**臨床指南**（clinical guideline）或是'''臨床實踐指南 '''（clinical practice guideline） ，是目的在提供醫療決策及準則指南的文件，會針對[醫療保健特定領域中的診斷](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/醫療保健 "wikilink")、管理及治療進行說明。在[医学史](../Page/医学史.md "wikilink")中，幾千年前就有類似醫學指南的文件，不過以往的醫學指南是以傳統或是權威為基礎，現代的醫學指南是依[循证医学](../Page/循证医学.md "wikilink")的范式，以現有實證的检查為基礎\[1\]\[2\]\[3\]。醫學指南一般會包括經過整理或是醫療保健上的[最佳實踐](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/最佳實踐 "wikilink")。醫療保健人員必須瞭解其專業科別中的醫學指南，在個別治療時則需依個別情形，決定是否依照醫學指南的建議進行\[4\]。

## 背景

現代的醫學指南會針對[預防](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/預防醫學 "wikilink")、[診斷](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/醫學診斷 "wikilink")、[预后](../Page/预后.md "wikilink")、治療（包括藥品劑量、及等），識別、整理、評估最高水準的實證以及最新的資料。之後定義有關此臨床實務最重要的一些問題，並且找出所有可能的[决策](../Page/决策.md "wikilink")及。有些指南會包括決策或是計算的，因此將臨床的決策點以及各自的行動方針和臨床決策及醫療人員的經驗整合起來。許多指南會將可用的治療方式進行歸類，協助醫療人員決定要用哪一種治療方式。

醫學指南的另一個目的是為了醫療的[标准化](../Page/标准化.md "wikilink")、提昇醫療品質、降低各種風險（包括對病患的風險、對醫療從業者的風險、對[醫療保險](../Page/醫療保險.md "wikilink")及健康計劃的風險等） ，在成本以及醫療相關參數（如、[靈敏度和特異度](../Page/靈敏度和特異度.md "wikilink")等）之間取得最好的平衡。經過許多實證證明，醫療機構（例如[醫院](../Page/醫院.md "wikilink")）的醫療人員使用醫學指南，是有效達到上述目的的方式之一。

An example is the Surgical Safety Checklist developed for the [世界卫生组织](../Page/世界卫生组织.md "wikilink") by Dr. [阿图·葛文德](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/阿图·葛文德 "wikilink").\[7\] According to a meta-analysis after introduction of the checklist mortality dropped by 23% and all complications by 40%, but further high-quality studies are required to make the meta-analysis more robust.\[8\] In the UK, a study on the implementation of a checklist for provision of medical care to elderly patients admitting to hospital found that the checklist highlighted limitations with frailty assessment in acute care and motivated teams to review routine practices, but that work is needed to understand whether and how checklists can be embedded in complex multidisciplinary care.\[9\]

## 問題

Guidelines may lose their clinical relevance as they age and newer research emerges.\[10\] Even 20% of strong recommendations, especially when based on opinion rather than trials, from practice guidelines may be retracted.\[11\]

*[纽约时报](../Page/纽约时报.md "wikilink")* reported in 2004 that some simple clinical practice guidelines are not routinely followed to the extent they might be.\[12\] It has been found that providing a [护理学](../Page/护理学.md "wikilink") or other medical assistant with a [checklist](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/checklist "wikilink") of recommended procedures can result in the attending physician being reminded in a timely manner regarding procedures that might have been overlooked.

Guidelines may have both methodological problems and conflict of interest.\[13\] As such, the quality of guidelines may vary substantially, especially for guidelines that are published on-line and have not had to follow methodological reporting standards often required by reputable clearinghouses.\[14\]

Guidelines may make recommendations that are stronger than the supporting evidence.\[15\]

In response to many of these problems with traditional guidelines, the BMJ created a new series of trustworthy guidelines focused on the most pressing medical issues called *BMJ* Rapid Recommendations.\[16\]

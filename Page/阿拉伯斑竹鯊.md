**阿拉伯斑竹鯊**（Chiloscyllium arabicum），又稱**阿拉伯地毯鯊**、**阿拉伯肩章鯊**、**阿拉伯毛鯊**，是天竺鯊科中的一種[竹鯊](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/竹鯊 "wikilink")，分布於[波斯灣沿海到](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/波斯灣 "wikilink")[印度](../Page/印度.md "wikilink")的珊瑚礁及其他淺海區。這種鯊魚長78厘米（31英寸），特點是細長褐色身體和兩個[背鰭](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/背鰭 "wikilink")、[尾鰭筆直](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/尾鰭 "wikilink")，第二個比第一個小但長一些。 阿拉伯斑竹鯊以[輻鰭魚綱](../Page/輻鰭魚綱.md "wikilink")的魚類及無脊椎動物為食。繁殖是為卵生，每年循環進行。雌性一次儲四個卵囊，卵會在70-80天後孵化。這種小型鯊魚經常被捕獲為副捕品，但甚少使用。由於其分布範圍內不斷增加的捕魚壓力和棲息地退化，國際自然保護聯盟（IUCN）將其評定為近危。目前已並已被人工飼養，有些[水組館有此鯊魚](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/水組館 "wikilink")。

## Taxonomy

Prior to being described as a new species in Gubanov and Schleib's 1980 *Sharks of the Arabian ([sic](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/sic "wikilink").) Gulf*, the Arabian carpetshark was misreported as the [grey bamboo shark](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/grey_bamboo_shark "wikilink") (*C. griseum*); it is uncertain whether the ranges of these two similar sharks in fact overlap.\[1\] No [type specimens](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/type_specimen "wikilink") are known.\[2\] Other [common names](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/common_name "wikilink") for this species include Arabian bamboo shark and confusing bamboo shark.\[3\]

## Distribution and habitat

The Arabian carpetshark inhabits coastal waters  deep, though most are found shallower than .\[4\]\[5\] Its range extends from the [Persian Gulf](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_Gulf "wikilink") to [Pakistan](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan "wikilink") and western [India](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "wikilink"); it is abundant in the Persian Gulf in spring and summer, and seldom reported from [Oman](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oman "wikilink") and India. This [demersal](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/demersal "wikilink") species favors [coral reefs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/coral_reef "wikilink"), [lagoons](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/lagoon "wikilink"), rocky coastlines, and [mangrove](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/mangrove "wikilink") [estuaries](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/estuaries "wikilink").\[6\]\[7\]

## Description

[Chiloscyllium_arabicum_pakistan_top.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Chiloscyllium_arabicum_pakistan_top.jpg "fig:Chiloscyllium_arabicum_pakistan_top.jpg") The Arabian carpetshark has a slender, nearly cylindrical body and a relatively long, thick, rounded snout. The [nostrils](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/nostril "wikilink") are set a good distance from the snout tip and preceded by a pair of short barbels. The eyes are medium-sized and placed high on the head, each with a low ridge above and a large [spiracle](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spiracle_\(vertebrates\) "wikilink") behind and below. The small mouth lies well forward of the eyes; there is a continuous fold of skin across the chin that wraps around the corners of the mouth. There are 26–35 upper tooth rows and 21–32 lower tooth rows. The teeth have a large central cusp and a pair of lateral cusplets. The five pairs of [gill slits](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/gill_slit "wikilink") are short, with the fourth and fifth pairs very close together.\[8\]\[9\]

The [pectoral fins](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/pectoral_fin "wikilink") are small, broad, and rounded; the [pelvic fins](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/pelvic_fin "wikilink") are similar and almost as large. The two [dorsal fins](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/dorsal_fin "wikilink") have straight trailing margins and are spaced well apart. The first dorsal fin is as large or larger than the pelvic fins, originating behind the middle of the pelvic fin bases. The second dorsal fin is slightly smaller than the first but has a longer base. There is a prominent midline ridge along the back, which continues between the dorsal fins. The long, keel-like [anal fin](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/anal_fin "wikilink") originates behind the second dorsal fin. The [caudal fin](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/caudal_fin "wikilink") is low and lacks a lower lobe; the upper lobe has a strong ventral notch near the tip. Adults are a plain tan color above and white below, sometimes with an orange tint on the fin margins; juveniles have faint lighter spots on the fins.\[10\]\[11\] This species reaches a maximum known length of .\[12\]

## Biology and ecology

Commonly found sheltering inside caves and crevices, the Arabian carpetshark is a [predator](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/predator "wikilink") of [bony fishes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/bony_fish "wikilink") (including [snake eels](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/snake_eel "wikilink")) and [invertebrates](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/invertebrate "wikilink") (including [stomatopods](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/stomatopod "wikilink"), [shrimp](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/shrimp "wikilink"), [crabs](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/crab "wikilink"), [squid](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/squid "wikilink"), [gastropods](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/gastropod "wikilink"), and [echiuroid worms](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Echiura "wikilink")).\[13\]\[14\] This hardy species can survive for some time out of water.\[15\] It is [oviparous](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/oviparous "wikilink") like other members of its family, with a six-month breeding season. The reproductive cycle has been documented in captivity: [copulation](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/copulation "wikilink") involves the male holding onto one of the female's pectoral fins with his mouth, while inserting a single [clasper](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/clasper "wikilink") into her [cloaca](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/cloaca "wikilink") for 5–15 minutes. When competing for mates, male sharks have been known to bite the claspers of rival males.\[16\] Females produce an average of 33 [egg capsules](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/egg_case_\(Chondrichthyes\) "wikilink") per year in batches of four, that are laid over a period of 20 minutes to two days. Of those eggs, about 7% are infertile.\[17\]\[18\] The egg cases have adhesive tendrils for securing them to coral, and hatch in 70–80 days at a temperature of .\[19\]\[20\] Newly emerged young measure under  long; [sexual maturity](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/sexual_maturity "wikilink") is attained at a length of .\[21\]

## Human interactions

Small and harmless to humans, the Arabian carpetshark is one of the few shark species suitable for [private aquaria](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/fishkeeping "wikilink").\[22\]\[23\] It is collected for the aquarium trade, which is not believed to pose a substantial threat to its population.\[24\] The meat and possibly the fins of this shark can be used, but because of its size most individuals landed are discarded. It is [captured incidentally](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/bycatch "wikilink") in [intertidal](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/intertidal_zone "wikilink") *hadra* ([artisanal](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/artisan_fishing "wikilink") [stake-net traps](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/fish_trap "wikilink")), as well as in demersal [trammel](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/trammel_net "wikilink") and [trawl](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/trawl "wikilink") nets. The Arabian carpetshark forms the predominant component of the "cat shark" catch of [Kuwaiti](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuwait "wikilink") [prawn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/prawn "wikilink") trawls, which represents the second-largest bycatch (14% of total) of the [fishery](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/fishery "wikilink"). It is also caught off [Bahrain](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bahrain "wikilink") and likely elsewhere. Another potentially major threat to this species is [habitat degradation](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/habitat_degradation "wikilink"): coral reefs in the Persian Gulf face [bottom trawling](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/bottom_trawl "wikilink"), coastal development (especially large-scale [land reclamation](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/land_reclamation "wikilink") projects such as in the [United Arab Emirates](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates "wikilink")), [Turkish](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "wikilink") [dams](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/dam "wikilink") on the [Tigris](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tigris "wikilink")-[Euphrates](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euphrates "wikilink") [river system](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/river_system "wikilink"), draining of [marshes](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/marsh "wikilink") in [Iraq](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq "wikilink"), and [oil spills](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/oil_spill "wikilink"). Coastal habitats off India are also similarly pressured. As both fishing and habitat degradation are likely to intensify in the region, the [International Union for Conservation of Nature](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature "wikilink") (IUCN) has assessed the Arabian carpetshark as [Near Threatened](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Near_Threatened "wikilink").\[25\]\[26\]

## 製作

### 發展

After
[ABC](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company "wikilink")'s
cancellation of *[Twin
Peaks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twin_Peaks "wikilink")* in 1991,
and the release of [David
Lynch](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Lynch "wikilink")'s 1992
prequel film *[Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with
Me](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twin_Peaks:_Fire_Walk_with_Me "wikilink")*,
Lynch reportedly planned two more films that would have continued and
then concluded the series' narrative. But in a 2001 interview, he said
that the *Twin Peaks* franchise was as "dead as a doornail."\[159\]

In 2007, artist [Matt
Haley](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matt_Haley "wikilink") and *Twin
Peaks* producer [Robert
Engels](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Engels "wikilink") began
work on a graphic-novel continuation of the series, to be included in
the "Complete Mystery" DVD box set. Haley stated: "Bob and I had a
number of discussions about what the story would be, I was keen to use
whatever notes they had for the proposed third season. I really wanted
this to be a literal 'third season' of the show." Lynch vetoed the
project, stating that he respected the effort but did not want to
continue the story of *Twin Peaks* in any way.\[160\]

Rumors spread in early 2013 that some form of return to *Twin Peaks* was
imminent, but these were dismissed by Lynch's daughter [Jennifer
Lynch](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jennifer_Lynch "wikilink") (author
of *[The Secret Diary of Laura
Palmer](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Secret_Diary_of_Laura_Palmer "wikilink")*),
as well as co-creator [Mark
Frost](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Frost "wikilink"), who wrote
that "truth is more valuable and much harder to come by."\[161\] In May
2013, cast member [Ray
Wise](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ray_Wise "wikilink") recounted what
Lynch had said to him about a possible continuation: "Well, Ray, you
know, the town is still there. And I suppose it's possible that we could
revisit it. Of course, you're already dead... but we could maybe work
around that."\[162\]

In 2014, the development and release of the *Twin Peaks: The Complete
Mystery* Blu-ray set, in addition to Wise's earlier comments, caused
fans to speculate that *Twin Peaks* would return in some form. In
January 2014, a casting call for a "*Twin Peaks* promo" to be directed
by Lynch was revealed to be the filming of a featurette for *The
Complete Mystery*.\[163\] In September 2014, Lynch answered a question
about *Twin Peaks* at the [Lucca Film
Festival](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucca_Film_Festival "wikilink")
by saying it was a "tricky question", and that "there's always a
possibility... and you just have to wait and see."\[164\]

### 公告

On October 6, 2014, it was announced that
[Showtime](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Showtime_\(TV_network\) "wikilink")
would air a nine-episode miniseries\[165\] written by Lynch and Frost
and directed entirely by Lynch. Frost emphasized that the new episodes
are not a remake or reboot but a continuation of the series. The
episodes are set in the present day, and the passage of 25 years is an
important element in the plot. Regarding whether the miniseries will
become an ongoing series, Frost said, "The proof will be in the pudding.
If we have a great time doing it and everybody loves it and they decide
there's room for more, I could see it going that way."\[166\]

In March 2015, Lynch expressed doubts about the production due to
"complications". But Showtime confirmed the series was moving forward,
stating, "Nothing is going on that's any more than any preproduction
process with David Lynch. Everything is moving forward and everybody is
crazy thrilled and excited."\[167\] In April 2015, Lynch said he would
not direct the nine episodes due to budget constraints.\[168\] But Lynch
and Showtime came to an agreement, with Lynch confirming on May 15,
2015, that he would direct, and that there would be more episodes than
the originally announced nine.\[169\] At a *Twin Peaks* panel in
Seattle, cast members Sherilyn Fenn and Sheryl Lee said that the new
series would consist of 18 episodes and [Angelo
Badalamenti](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angelo_Badalamenti "wikilink")
would return as composer.\[170\]

### 選角

On January 12, 2015, Kyle MacLachlan was confirmed as returning to the
series.\[171\] It was reported that [Peter
Sarsgaard](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Sarsgaard "wikilink") had
been cast\[172\] but he later said, "I'm not doing *Twin Peaks*" and it
"was a rumor that was not founded on nothing."\[173\] In October 2015,
it was confirmed that [Michael
Ontkean](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Ontkean "wikilink"), who
portrayed Sheriff Harry S. Truman and has since retired from acting,
would not return for the revival. In October 2015 it was reported that
the role of town sheriff would be filled by [Robert
Forster](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Forster "wikilink"), later
confirmed as playing Frank Truman, the brother of an ailing Harry.
Forster was originally cast as Harry in the 1990 pilot, but was replaced
by Ontkean due to scheduling issues.\[174\] Also in October, [David
Duchovny](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Duchovny "wikilink")
teased his return as Agent Denise Bryson.\[175\] In November 2015, it
was reported that [Miguel
Ferrer](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguel_Ferrer "wikilink") would
reprise his role as Albert Rosenfield and that [Richard
Beymer](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Beymer "wikilink") and
[David Patrick
Kelly](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Patrick_Kelly "wikilink")
would return as Benjamin Horne and Jerry Horne, respectively.\[176\] In
December 2015, [Alicia
Witt](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alicia_Witt "wikilink") confirmed
she would reprise her role as Gersten Hayward.\[177\]

[Russ Tamblyn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russ_Tamblyn "wikilink")
underwent open-heart surgery in late 2014 and was still recovering in
2015. Lynch and Frost were still hoping Tamblyn would join the cast for
the new season.\[178\] On September 28, 2015, [Catherine E.
Coulson](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_E._Coulson "wikilink"),
who was planned to reprise her role of the [Log
Lady](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Log_Lady "wikilink") in the new
series,\[179\] died of cancer.\[180\] Both Tamblyn and Coulson were
later confirmed as part of the cast, as Coulson had filmed a few scenes
before her death.\[181\]

The series' first teaser trailer, released in December 2015, confirmed
the involvement of [Michael
Horse](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Horse "wikilink") (Tommy
"Hawk" Hill).\[182\] In January 2016, it was reported that [Sherilyn
Fenn](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sherilyn_Fenn "wikilink") would
reprise her role as [Audrey
Horne](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audrey_Horne "wikilink") in a
"major presence."\[183\] In February 2016, it was reported that Lynch
would reprise his role as Gordon Cole.\[184\] Frequent Lynch
collaborator [Laura
Dern](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laura_Dern "wikilink") was cast in a
"top-secret pivotal role".\[185\] In April 2016, a complete cast list
was released, featuring 217 actors, with actors returning from the
earlier series marked with asterisks.\[186\]

Before his death in January 2016, [David
Bowie](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Bowie "wikilink") had
intended to make a cameo appearance as FBI Agent Phillip Jeffries, his
character from *[Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with
Me](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twin_Peaks:_Fire_Walk_with_Me "wikilink")*.\[187\]
In January and February 2017, respectively, cast members [Miguel
Ferrer](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguel_Ferrer "wikilink") and
[Warren Frost](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warren_Frost "wikilink")
died, but both appear in the new series.\[188\]\[189\]

### 拍攝

In July 2015, Frost suggested that the series would premiere in 2017
rather than 2016, as originally planned.\[190\] The series began filming
in September 2015, and Showtime president [David
Nevins](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Nevins_\(television_producer\) "wikilink")
said, "I'm hoping we make 2016. It's not clear. It's ultimately going to
be in \[series co-creators David Lynch and Mark Frost's\]
control."\[191\] Nevins also stated, "I don't know \[how many episodes
there will be\]. They're going to decide, I expect it to be more than
nine, but it's open-ended. I know what the shooting schedule is and then
we'll have him cut into it however many episodes it feels best
at."\[192\] In January 2016, Nevins confirmed that the series would
premiere in the first half of 2017.\[193\] The series was shot
continuously from a single, long shooting script before being edited
into episodes. In January 2016, it was reported that filming was more
than halfway done.\[194\] Filming was completed by April 2016.\[195\]

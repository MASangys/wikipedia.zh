<span data-segmentid="94" class="cx-segment" id="cxmwCA" data-source="mwCA" data-cx-mt-provider="Yandex" data-cx-weight="0" style="min-height: 912px;" data-cx-draft="true" contenteditable="true" autocomplete="off"></span>**馬特萊**（[僧伽罗语](../Page/僧伽罗语.md "wikilink")：මාතලේ，[泰米尔语](../Page/泰米尔语.md "wikilink")：மாத்தளை）是[斯里蘭卡](../Page/斯里蘭卡.md "wikilink")[中央省](../Page/中央省_\(斯里蘭卡\).md "wikilink")[馬特萊區的最大城鎮](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/馬特萊區 "wikilink")，距离首都[科伦坡和](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/科伦坡 "wikilink")[康提](../Page/康提.md "wikilink")142公里。馬特萊的附近被[指节山脉包圍](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/指节山脉 "wikilink")，英国人把丘陵地带称为[威尔特郡](../Page/威爾特郡.md "wikilink")。馬特萊是农业地区，茶叶、橡胶、蔬菜和香料占主导地位。

## 歷史

The
[Aluvihare](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluvihare_Rock_Temple "wikilink")
Temple, on the north side of the town, is the historic location where
the [Pali Canon](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pali_Canon "wikilink")
was first written down completely in text on ola (palm) leaves in 29
BCE.

Matale was a site of a major battle in 1848 when the [Matale
Rebellion](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matale_Rebellion "wikilink")
started and the British garrison in the [Fort
MacDowall](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fort_MacDowall "wikilink") in
Matale was placed under siege by the rebels led by [Weera Puran
Appu](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puran_Appu "wikilink") and
[Gongalegoda
Banda](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gongalegoda_Banda "wikilink") who
are considered as national heroes in Sri Lanka.

This historical city was also home to Monarawila Keppetipola, another
national hero who led the Wellasa rebellion against the British troops.
His ancestral home, the Kappetipola walawuwa is still present at
Hulangamuwa, Matale.

Christ Church, Matale was consecrated by Bishop James Chapman on 30
December 1860.

The church site chosen was Fort McDowall, commanding a view of the
entrance to Matale via
[Trincomalee](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trincomalee "wikilink"). The
church having been erected and furnished, the Revd William Frederick
Kelly, Minister and Chaplain in Matale, and 36 others sent their
petition to the Rt Revd James Chapman, D.D., to dedicate and consecrate
Christ Church. The parsonage was opened on 16 August 1862. The 75th
Anniversary was celebrated in 1935. The centenary was celebrated on 28
and 29 January 1961. The 125th Anniversary was celebrated on 6 September
1986. The church was gutted by fire in 1985 and was completely restored.
Services were held in the Baptist Church in the interim period.

On 22 June 1911, there was a special service held in this church in
connection with the Coronation of His Majesty King George V, and Queen
Mary. The offertory on that was given to the King Edward VIII.\[1\]

The 150th Anniversary was celebrated on 2 October 2010.

Matale is the only district of Sri Lanka, with a book of [written
history](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/recorded_history "wikilink"). The
book is known as ''Aithihaasika matale - ඓතිහාසික මාතලේ ("ancient
Matale" in English).

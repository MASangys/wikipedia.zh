**高效滤网**（，即**高效率空氣微粒子過濾網**，簡稱**HEPA**\[1\]\[2\]）是一种用于[空气净化的器材](../Page/空气净化.md "wikilink")。
符合HEPA標準的過濾器的用途很廣泛，從醫療設備、汽車、飛機及家居均可應用。
截经过滤网的[空气中所含有的微小](../Page/空气.md "wikilink")[尘埃](../Page/尘埃.md "wikilink")。濾網的標準由美國能源部設定，对粒径在0.3μm的粒子，
過濾效果約為DOP
99.97%以上。通常使用的高效滤网可达到99.99%的拦截效率，也有更高或相对低一些的品种，各国有相应的规格标准。高效濾網的過濾材質通常由无规则排布的[化学纤维](../Page/化学纤维.md "wikilink")（例如：[聚丙烯纤维即](../Page/聚丙烯纤维.md "wikilink")[丙纶或](../Page/丙纶.md "wikilink")[聚酯纤维即](../Page/聚酯纤维.md "wikilink")[涤纶的](../Page/涤纶.md "wikilink")[无纺布](../Page/无纺布.md "wikilink")）或[玻璃纤维制成](../Page/玻璃纤维.md "wikilink")，直徑約0.5到2.0微米，通过微观的絮状结构，主要是用來去除0.5µm以上之微粒。

同样被使用于空气净化的滤网，按过滤效率由低至高，命名为[粗效滤网](../Page/粗效滤网.md "wikilink")、[中效滤网](../Page/中效滤网.md "wikilink")、[亚高效滤网及比高效滤网還要高的](../Page/亚高效滤网.md "wikilink")[超高效滤网](../Page/超高效滤网.md "wikilink")。

## 技术规格

[HEPA_filter_unit.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:HEPA_filter_unit.jpg "fig:HEPA_filter_unit.jpg")

根据[美国能源部](../Page/美国能源部.md "wikilink")（DOE, United States Department of
Energy）公布，且为大多数美国工业部门采纳的标准，HEPA滤网应当至少过滤99.97%直径 ≧
0.3[微米](../Page/微米.md "wikilink")(µm)的[空气中的颗粒](../Page/气胶.md "wikilink")。
滤网的最小气流阻力，或者[压降](../Page/压降.md "wikilink")，通常规格为在其标称[流量下](../Page/體積流率.md "wikilink")，为300[帕斯卡](../Page/帕斯卡.md "wikilink")。

[欧盟国家中](../Page/欧盟.md "wikilink")，该技术规范通常也以以EN
1822:2009.呈现。其根据最易穿透粒径的过滤效果(MPPS)，定义了HEPA滤网的几类等级：

| HEPA等级    | 濾淨率 (total)   | 濾淨率(local)   |
| --------- | ------------- | ------------ |
| E10       | \> 85%        | \---         |
| E11 (H11) | \> 95%        | \---         |
| E12 (H12) | \> 99.5%      | \---         |
| H13       | \> 99.95%     | \> 99.75%    |
| H14       | \> 99.995%    | \> 99.975%   |
| U15       | \> 99.9995%   | \> 99.9975%  |
| U16       | \> 99.99995%  | \> 99.99975% |
| U17       | \> 99.999995% | \> 99.9999%  |

Today, a HEPA filter rating is applicable to any highly efficient air
filter that can attain the same filter efficiency performance standards
as a minimum and is equivalent to the more recent
[NIOSH](../Page/NIOSH.md "wikilink") N100 rating for respirator filters.
The [United States Department of
Energy](../Page/United_States_Department_of_Energy.md "wikilink") (DOE)
has specific requirements for HEPA filters in DOE regulated
applications. In addition, companies have begun using a marketing term
known as "True HEPA" to give consumers assurance that their air filters
are indeed certified to meet the HEPA standard.\[3\]

Products that claim to be "HEPA-type", "HEPA-like", "HEPA-style" or "99%
HEPA" do not satisfy these requirements and may not have been tested in
independent laboratories. Some of these sub-par quality filters may come
reasonably close to HEPA filtration, while others will fall
significantly short, making them truly inferior.\[4\]

## 历史

Bonaventure Island (Île Bonaventure), with Percé, was among the early seasonal fishing ports of [New France](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_France "wikilink"), and was associated with the [lineage](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lineage_\(anthropology\) "wikilink") of [Nicolas Denys](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolas_Denys "wikilink"). The island became a migratory bird sanctuary in 1919 due to the [1916 Migratory Bird Convention](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/1916_Migratory_Bird_Convention "wikilink") between Canada and the United States. The Province of Quebec acquired ownership of the entire island in 1971 and later grouped it together with [Percé Rock](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percé_Rock "wikilink") into the [Parc national de l'île-Bonaventure-et-du-Rocher-Percé](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parc_national_de_l'île-Bonaventure-et-du-Rocher-Percé "wikilink") (Bonaventure Island and Percé Rock National Park) in 1985. One of the largest and most accessible bird sanctuaries in the world, with more than 280,000 birds, Bonaventure Island is a major [tourist destination](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/tourist_destination "wikilink") with boat and island tours from May to October.

The aircraft carrier [HMCS *Bonaventure*](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMCS_Bonaventure "wikilink") was named after the island.

## 鸟类

293 different species of birds have been recorded as visiting, migrating to, or living on Bonaventure island.

The most common bird found on the island is the [Northern Gannet](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Gannet "wikilink"). The island is home to the largest colony of gannets in the world, with over 121,000 birds.\[1\] [Parc_national_de_l'Île-Bonaventure.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Parc_national_de_l'Île-Bonaventure.JPG "fig:Parc_national_de_l'Île-Bonaventure.JPG") [Gannet_up_close.jpg](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Gannet_up_close.jpg "fig:Gannet_up_close.jpg") on the island.\]\] [Northern_Gannet_de_l'Île-Bonaventure.JPG](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Northern_Gannet_de_l'Île-Bonaventure.JPG "fig:Northern_Gannet_de_l'Île-Bonaventure.JPG") Other populous colonies include the [Black-legged Kittiwake](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black-legged_Kittiwake "wikilink") and the [Common Murre](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_Murre "wikilink"). [Terns](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tern "wikilink"), [Black Guillemots](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Guillemot "wikilink"), [Auks](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auk "wikilink"), [Herring Gulls](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Herring_Gull "wikilink"), [Great Black-backed Gulls](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Black-backed_Gull "wikilink"), [Razorbills](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Razorbill "wikilink"), [Leach's Storm-Petrels](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leach's_Storm-Petrel "wikilink"), [Great Cormorants](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Cormorant "wikilink"), [Double-crested Cormorants](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Double-crested_Cormorant "wikilink"), [Atlantic Puffins](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Puffin "wikilink"), [Boreal Chickadees](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boreal_Chickadee "wikilink") and [Blackpoll Warblers](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackpoll_Warbler "wikilink") can also be observed on Bonaventure.

## 诗意地标

The Bonaventure Island has been an important source of inspiration to numerous artists and poets. The surrealist writer [André Breton](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/André_Breton "wikilink") declared that, while working on his novel *Arcane 17*, as he stayed in Percé (1944), he would never tire watching the birds of the Island.\[2\] The Island never ceased to attract painters and writers : the American painter Frederick James (d. 1905), Franco-Germans Claire et [Yvan Goll](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yvan_Goll "wikilink") (1946), etc., — by the sixties several artists would spend the summer on the Island, and would stay over the summer with the inhabitants -descendants of Irish and Norman settlers- the best known of whom was the naturalist William Du Val. Among these inspired visitors: painter Jacques Hurtubise \[3\] and Kittie Bruneau,\[4\] the sculptor Morton Rosengarten, the poet Michaël La Chance.\[5\] Artists and landowners were evicted in the seventies.\[6\]
